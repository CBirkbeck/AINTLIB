# Reviewer reply — round 7 (2026-05-29)

## Executive verdict

Do **not** pivot wholesale to Stepanov yet. Stepanov is a valid proof of Hasse and for genus 1 can be made explicit, but it is not free of heavy infrastructure: it replaces dual isogenies with an auxiliary-function existence theorem, zero-vs-pole counting, multiplicity estimates, and Hasse-derivative/Frobenius-derivative bookkeeping in small characteristic. Given the assembly is already reduced to two leaves and Frobenius, V, separability, point/kernel identification, valuation at O, and discriminant wiring are in hand, the better plan:

1. Finish Leaf 2 via the shortest separable-isogeny fibre-count theorem, not the heavy Sinf detour, if a direct finite-étale/fibre-degree route is available.
2. For Leaf 1, prove only the restricted dual-additivity / degree-QF theorem on the Frobenius plane, not full arbitrary dual isogeny theory.
3. Keep Stepanov as a serious fallback only after a one-week prototype of its two core lemmas: explicit L(nO) basis and zero-counting by pole degree.

Deepest obstruction correctly identified: Frobenius has a dual (V), but the proof still needs a degree-bearing dual of (r·π−s), or at least the restricted identity (r·π−s)^ = r·V−s at the level where pullbacks/degrees are genuine.

## Q1 — Switch to Stepanov? NOT YET.

Stepanov avoids isogenies/duals but needs: auxiliary function construction + multiplicity at all E(F_q) points + zero-counting by pole degree + derivative/Hasse-derivative handling in char p.

For genus 1 you can avoid full Riemann–Roch by proving explicitly that L(nO) = {f ∈ K(E) : div_∞(f) ≤ nO} has a concrete basis 1, x, y, x², xy, … with pole orders generated by 2 and 3. Lighter than general RR. But Stepanov still needs: 0 ≠ f ∈ L(D) ⟹ Σ_{P ∈ E(F_q)} ord_P(f) ≤ deg D (a principal-divisor / zero-vs-pole counting theorem — not full RR but nontrivial global function-field infrastructure).

Stepanov's first target should not be the full Hasse proof, but the prototype:
1. explicit basis/dimension of L(nO);
2. nonzero f ∈ L(nO) has at most n zeros counted with multiplicity;
3. Hasse-derivative/multiplicity lemma for functions vanishing at all F_q-points.

If those three are easy in your framework, Stepanov becomes attractive. If any becomes a new multi-week project, stay with Silverman.

Why Stepanov may be worse than it looks: "elementary" is misleading for formalisation — dimension-counting that is RR in disguise; high-multiplicity vanishing at many rational points; Frobenius/Hasse derivative tricks to avoid p-derivative collapse; carefully optimised integer inequalities. By contrast the current route has already shipped substantial Silverman infrastructure. So: prototype Stepanov, but do not pivot yet.

## Q2 — Is the general dual unavoidable? Within Silverman, YES some duality; but NOT full III.6.1.

Prove the restricted Frobenius-plane theorem: (r·π−s)^ = r·V−s. Then it's formal algebra:
(rV−s)(rπ−s) = r²Vπ − rs(V+π) + s² = q r² − t rs + s². And since rV−s is the actual dual, (rV−s)(rπ−s) = [deg(rπ−s)], so deg(rπ−s) = q r² − t rs + s² ≥ 0.

Without that dual identification, V π=[q] and V+π=[t] are NOT enough: they give the composition identity but not that the integer q r²−trs+s² is THE degree of rπ−s. Degree multiplicativity only gives the square relation deg(rV−s)deg(rπ−s) = (q r²−trs+s²)², which fixes neither sign nor identifies either factor.

Parallelogram law is not a real shortcut: deg(φ+ψ)+deg(φ−ψ)=2deg φ+2deg ψ is essentially "deg is a quadratic form," usually proved from duality/Rosati or an equivalent intersection/torsion-determinant theorem. So proving it without duals is likely the same problem again.

Best reduced target: do NOT prove III.6.1 for all isogenies first. Prove the narrow `frobeniusPlane_dual : IsDualOf (r•V − s•1) (r•π − s•1)` with genuine pullbacks/comorphisms. Strictly less ambitious than general dual existence but enough for Hasse.

## Q3 — Cleanest Leaf 2 shortcut: separable-isogeny fibre-count theorem.

The clean theorem: α separable isogeny ⟹ #ker α = deg α. For 1−π: deg(1−π) = #ker(1−π) = #E(F_q).

Lean-friendly options:
- Option A (finite étale fibre count): separable isogeny ⇒ finite étale; finite étale of degree d over alg-closed field ⇒ every fibre has d points; apply to fibre over O. Clean but may be scheme-heavy.
- Option B (ramification-sum, but generic): you already use Σ_{P|Q} e_P f_P = [K(E):α*K(E)]. For separable α, translation invariance shows every ramification index is 1, and over K̄ every residue degree is 1, so the fibre has deg α points. Close to what you formalised, avoids the special Sinf pole-locus detour.
- Option C (function-field embeddings): finite separable L/M has [L:M] embeddings into K̄; translating to the fibre over O needs the curve/place dictionary; elegant but not necessarily lighter.

Recommendation: if you already have ramification/inertia infrastructure, use Option B:
1. prove separable isogenies are unramified everywhere (translation invariance + nonzero differential);
2. apply Σ e f = deg;
3. specialise to the fibre over O;
4. use ker(1−π)=E(F_q).
Likely less work than finishing the specialised Sinf pole-locus dictionary.

## Q4 — Remodel isogenies? YES, but staged, not a giant blocking refactor.

Immediate rule: every theorem relating point-maps to pullbacks/degrees should require a predicate `IsGenuine (φ : Isogeny E E')` (or use a compatible isogeny type if one exists).

Short-term quarantine: keep the raw type internally, but public APIs use GenuineIsogeny / Isogeny+IsGenuine / bare AddMonoidHom for point-map-only statements.

Long-term refactor: split the record — RawIsogeny (independent fields, no semantic claim), GenuineIsogeny (raw + compatibility/basepoint-preservation), PointHomOnly (only rational-point group hom).

WARNING: do NOT add a naive field `compat : ∀ P, point_map P = evaluate_pullback_at P` — rational functions have poles and projective points need local/projective evaluation. The round-5 CoordHom issue shows affine coordinate-ring compatibility is not globally available for 1−π. The compatibility predicate must be local/projective/function-field aware.

So: carry explicit genuine hypotheses now; refactor the core type later.

## Q5 — Which assets survive a switch?

Continue Silverman/QF route: almost all §4 assets remain useful (Frobenius+deg π=q; Vπ=πV=[q]; separability criterion; 1−π separable; ker(1−π)=E(F_q); discriminant wiring; valuation at O; deg[m]=m², trace of mult maps). Main missing asset: restricted dual additivity on the Frobenius plane.

Switch to Stepanov: survives K(E); Frobenius comorphism f↦f^q; valuations/pole-order at O; explicit Weierstrass coordinate algebra; maybe division-polynomial/mult-map facts. Less useful: V; dual-isogeny scaffolding; degree of isogenies; deg[m], trace[m]; separability of 1−π; deg(1−π)=#E(F_q). So Stepanov strands a lot of the isogeny work.

Leaner QF argument: assets 1,2,6,8 central; still needs restricted duality, Weil pairing/torsion determinant, or equivalent positivity.

Least-stranding plan: finish Leaf 2 via separable-isogeny fibre count; for Leaf 1 target restricted duality on Zπ+Z, not full duality; prototype Stepanov only if restricted duality still looks too large.

## Additional corrections and warnings

- "Positive-definite" should be "positive semidefinite": on the Frobenius plane q r²−t rs+s² can be semidefinite where rπ−s=0 for nonzero (r,s). For Hasse you only need nonnegativity. Say "positive semidefinite" / "nonnegative binary quadratic form."
- Stepanov still needs a no-RR substitute: first lemma should be explicit L(nO) (basis {x^i, x^j y with 2i ≤ n, 2j+3 ≤ n}), then zeros_le_poles (f ≠ 0 → zeroMultiplicityOnRationalPoints f ≤ poleDegree f). Those are the two key formalisation costs.
- Leaf 2 "direct" route should avoid circularity: don't argue "separable ⇒ #ker = deg" as a black box unless that theorem is itself the target. Prove it via a general separable-isogeny fibre-count theorem or ramification-sum + unramifiedness. The shortcut must be an actual proof of III.4.10(a), not a restatement.
