## Plain-English content (Silverman III.1.5)

For any isogeny `α : E → E`, the invariant differential `ω = dx/u` (where `u = 2y + a₁x + a₃`) is a **global generator** of `Ω[K(E)/F]` with NO zeros and NO poles. Therefore the pullback `α*ω = a_α · ω` defines `a_α ∈ K(E)` to also have NO zeros and NO poles (the divisor of `a_α` is the divisor of `α*ω` minus the divisor of `ω`, both of which are zero divisors).

A rational function on E with no poles is in the coordinate ring (integral over F[X]). A rational function in the coordinate ring with no zeros at infinity has nonneg ord at infinity. Combined: `a_α` is a **constant** ∈ image of `algebraMap F KE`.
