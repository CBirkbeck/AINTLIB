## Statement (Silverman III.3.1.1)
For a Weierstrass elliptic curve `E`, the function field `K̄(E)` is generated by
the coordinate functions `x, y` and `[K̄(E) : K̄(x)] = 2` (with `y` satisfying
the Weierstrass equation as a quadratic over `K̄(x)`).

theorem WeierstrassCurve.degree_function_field_over_kx
    (E : WeierstrassCurve F) [Fact (E.Δ ≠ 0)] :
    Module.finrank (RatFunc F) E.toAffine.FunctionField = 2

end HasseWeil.EC

## Notes
- Direct proof: the affine coordinate ring is `K[X,Y]/(weierstrass)`, and over
  `K(X)`, the polynomial `Y² + (a₁X + a₃)Y - (X³ + a₂X² + a₄X + a₆)` is
  irreducible (since it has no root: discriminant in `X` is `Δ_E ≠ 0`...).
- Avoid Riemann-Roch by working directly with the polynomial relation.
- Mathlib already provides `Affine.CoordinateRing.basis` which gives `{1, Y}` as
  a basis for the coordinate ring over `F[X]`. The fraction-field version
  follows by base change to `FractionRing F[X]`.
