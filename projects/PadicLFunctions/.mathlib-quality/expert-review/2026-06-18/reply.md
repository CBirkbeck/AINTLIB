# Reviewer's reply — 2026-06-18 (Iwasawa-theory specialist)

## Reviewer's conclusion

Taking the formalised inventory in the brief as fixed:

**There is no Coleman-only or Chebotarev-only shortcut to the RJW exact sequence.** Local
reciprocity can identify local principal units with local inertia, but the assertion that the
diagonal closure of global units is exactly the kernel—and that the resulting local quotient
exhausts the global inertia subgroup—is precisely the p-ramified part of global reciprocity.

However, **the particular ramified-CFT sequence is not logically indispensable to the Main
Conjecture.** The most credible genuine bypass is:

  class-group Main Conjecture  +  Iwasawa–Kummer duality/reflection  ⟹  Main Conjecture for 𝒳⁺_∞.

That route replaces ray-class reciprocity by a global Kummer-pairing theorem, together with
direct-limit class groups, Pontryagin duality, Tate twists, and the Iwasawa adjoint. It is
probably the smallest serious non-ray-class alternative.

For the **Vandiver theorem presently being formalised**, assuming the specialised ramified-CFT
sequence is overwhelmingly the right engineering decision. Replacing it by Kummer duality would
turn a short conditional argument into something close to the full Main Conjecture.

RJW themselves isolate the finite-level global-CFT exact sequence and then pass to the inverse
limit by Mittag–Leffler exactly as described in the brief.

| Route | Avoids RJW step 2? | Replacement input | Formalisation verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| RJW/Vandiver | No | Specialised p-ramified reciprocity | Best immediate route |
| Minus class groups + Kummer duality | Yes | Global Kummer pairing, direct limits, adjoint/twist | Best genuine alternative |

## Q1. Coleman/local reciprocity construct the missing iso semi-locally? NOT from stated ingredients.

One can build maps U^1_{n,v} → I_v ⊂ Gal(M_n⁺/F_n⁺) for v|p; the closed subgroup generated by
these inertia groups is Gal(M_n⁺/L_n⁺) (quotient by all inertia at p = maximal everywhere-unramified
subextension). The unresolved part is the KERNEL: proving ker(∏_{v|p} U^1_{n,v} → Gal(M_n⁺/L_n⁺)) =
closure(E⁺_{n,1}) needs (1) the principal-idèle/product-formula reciprocity (global units map
trivially) and (2) an existence theorem (every local character annihilating global units is realised
by a global abelian extension unramified outside p). Those are exactly the reciprocity-law and
existence components of global CFT, restricted to p-power ray class fields. Chebotarev detects existing
extensions; it does not globalise arbitrary local characters or prove the principal-idèle relation.
Coleman/Coates–Wiles target an Iwasawa algebra/measure space, not Gal(M_∞⁺/L_∞⁺); relevant only after
a global bridge is supplied. A Kummer-theory dual construction replaces global CFT by the degree-one
case of Poitou–Tate, not eliminating the global theorem. No standard reference derives the iso from
Coleman alone. The cleanest "semi-local" formulation remains 𝒰⁺_{∞,1}/𝓔⁺_{∞,1} ≃ Gal(𝓜⁺_∞/𝓛⁺_∞).

## Q2. Work entirely on the minus class-group side? YES — the main genuine bypass, but precise.

Use the DIRECT-limit class group A_∞^dir := colim_n A_n (extension/capitulation maps), DIFFERENT from
𝒴_∞ = lim_n A_n (norm maps). The Iwasawa–Kummer pairing gives 𝒳⁺_{∞,S_p} ≃ ((A_∞^dir)^(ω))^∨(1);
equivariantly for even χ: (𝒳⁺_∞)^(χ) ≃ ((A_∞^dir)^(ωχ^{-1}))^∨(1) (ψχ=ω from μ_{p^∞} carrying the
cyclotomic character). Standard: NSW Theorem 11.4.3, or Washington Proposition 13.32; plus the Iwasawa
adjoint relating direct- and inverse-limit class groups ((A_∞^dir)^∨ ≃ 𝒴_∞^*; under no-finite-submodule
hypotheses (𝒳⁺_{∞,S_p})^* ≃ 𝒴_∞^(ω)(-1)). Transfer needs Pontryagin duality, Tate twist, Iwasawa
involution/adjoint, and the matching transform of the Stickelberger / p-adic L-element; at the
characteristic-ideal level finite/pseudo-null terms vanish but twists/involutions must be formalised.

Qualifications: (a) NOT the undifferentiated minus part — every even χ-component of 𝒳⁺_∞ sees the
reflected odd ωχ^{-1}; the trivial even component sees the Teichmüller ω (delicate; modern work warns
results away from ω don't auto-handle it). (b) NOT free from existing unramified CFT — must formalise
the Kummer pairing + perfectness + transition maps on A_∞^dir, then either prove the MC for (A_∞^dir)^∨
directly or connect to 𝒴_∞ via the adjoint. Plausibly much smaller than full ray-class CFT, but still
a significant global theorem. **Vandiver does NOT make this short**: Vandiver annihilates EVEN
class-group components; Kummer duality for 𝒳⁺_∞ involves reflected ODD components (where irregularity
and the p-adic L-factors live) — it effectively asks you to prove the reflected class-group MC. So:
full MC = excellent alternative architecture; Vandiver = major detour.

## Q3. Euler-system proof avoid 𝓜⁺_∞ entirely? YES for the class-group form; NO for the final 𝒳⁺ identification without another bridge.

The cyclotomic-unit/Kolyvagin–Rubin–Thaine argument organises entirely via global units/cyclotomic
units, ideal class groups, auxiliary primes, characteristic ideals — proving char(𝒴_∞^(ψ)) =
char((𝓔_∞/𝓒_∞)^(ψ)) (or one divisibility, the other via index/class-number + control), without ever
defining 𝓜⁺_∞. Aoki's proof uses Euler systems of Gauss sums and directly studies minus ideal-class
groups (contrast Rubin's cyclotomic-unit proof); Washington 2nd ed. has a full chapter on
Thaine–Kolyvagin–Rubin + a proof of the MC. But to conclude char(𝒳⁺_∞) = I(𝒢⁺)ζ_p one must still
invoke either (1) the RJW four-term sequence (ramified-CFT input), or (2) Kummer duality/reflection +
Iwasawa adjoint. There is no purely formal implication 𝒴_∞-theorem ⟹ 𝒳⁺_∞-theorem (they classify
different global extensions). Beyond finite-level Thaine, the tower-level proof still needs:
norm-compatible Euler classes through the tower; Kolyvagin-derivative/corestriction compatibility;
character-component control across levels; conversion of finite-level annihilation/index into
height-one valuations of characteristic ideals; finite-submodule/μ treatment; the second divisibility
(or finite-level index ⟹ char-ideal equality, made compatible with specialisation + the structure
theorem over 𝒪⟦T⟧, not just equality of two finite cardinalities).

## Q4. Ramified CFT intrinsic or expository? The exact sequence is NOT intrinsic as a particular device.

What IS intrinsic: the need for SOME global bridge between the analytic object (from local/semi-local
units) and the Galois object (defined via global p-ramified extensions). Available bridges: ramified
global reciprocity; Kummer pairing/reflection; Poitou–Tate / Selmer complexes; modular/Eisenstein
congruences. Coleman alone does not provide it. Recommended references: Vandiver argument → RJW/CS06
+ Washington Cor 13.6 (minimal; proof becomes formal algebra once granted). Non-ray-class full theorem
→ Washington's/Rubin's Euler-system proof on class groups + Washington Prop 13.32 / NSW Thm 11.4.3
(Kummer pairing) + the Iwasawa-adjoint relation. Direct minus-side → Aoki's Gauss-sum approach
(would require formalising a new Euler system). Mazur–Wiles: little to recommend here.

## Q5. Selmer formulation reduce the burden? Cleanest general architecture, but does NOT reduce the current burden.

For W_n=(ℚ_p/ℤ_p)(n), ordinary Greenberg local conditions give (even positive n)
H^1_{Gr}(F_∞⁺,W_n) ≅ Hom_cts(𝒳⁺_∞,W_n), so its Pontryagin dual is a Tate twist of 𝒳⁺_∞; this
identification is elementary (H^1 of a trivial discrete module is continuous Hom; local conditions
impose unramifiedness away from p). But connecting that Selmer group to units/Coleman requires:
(1) continuous cohomology of profinite groups with finite/compact p-primary modules; (2) global+local
Kummer sequences incl. Hilbert 90 and H^1(K_v,μ_{p^m}) ≅ K_v^×/K_v^{×p^m}; (3) unramified local
cohomology + inertia restriction; (4) local Tate duality + perfect cup-products; (5) Poitou–Tate
global duality (or a Selmer-complex exact triangle); (6) restricted products + the global invariant-sum
relation; (7) inverse/direct limits + Pontryagin duality + Γ-/Δ-compatibility. Mathlib's finite-group
groupCohomology is not close. Tractable initial pieces: continuous H^1, Kummer theory, unramified
local conditions. Local Tate duality + Poitou–Tate are the major barriers. A specially-proved
degree-one Poitou–Tate for μ_{p^m} might be smaller than general duality — but unwound, it is
essentially the p-ray-class reciprocity statement presently missing.

## Q6. Honest to axiomatise the sequence? YES — exactly the right boundary for the current Vandiver milestone.

Recommended change: black-box a FINITE-LEVEL NATURAL FAMILY rather than only the already-passed-to-the-
limit sequence. For every n, package an equivariant continuous map
  Art_{n,p} : 𝒰⁺_{n,1} → Gal(𝓜⁺_n/𝓛⁺_n)
with ker(Art_{n,p}) = 𝓔⁺_{n,1} and Art_{n,p} surjective, together with: compatibility with norm on
units and restriction/corestriction on Galois groups; equivariance for the finite cyclotomic Galois
action; compatibility with the plus projector. Then DERIVE in Lean
  0 → 𝓔⁺_{∞,1} → 𝒰⁺_{∞,1} → Gal(𝓜⁺_∞/𝓛⁺_∞) → 0
by inverse-limit exactness + Mittag–Leffler. This follows the actual source boundary (RJW state the
finite-level sequence from Washington and do the inverse-limit step separately). Preferable to assuming
an abstract infinite-level isomorphism: it preserves the actual reciprocity map, naturality in the
tower, the source of the Λ-action, and a future discharge route. A more reusable but larger black box:
the p-primary ray-class exact sequence for a number field K,
  E_K → ∏_{v|p} U_v^1/U_v^{(m)} → Cl_K(p^m)^(p) → Cl_K^(p) → 0,
plus the Artin realisation of the ray-class group (more fundamental, but creates modulus/ray-class/
conductor APIs the current proof doesn't otherwise need).

## Recommended project decision

For the current Stage G:
1. Assume the FINITE-LEVEL p-ramified reciprocity exact sequence, with tower compatibility.
2. Derive the inverse-limit sequence, splice it, complete the Vandiver theorem + conditional MC.
3. Treat elimination of that axiom as a separate project.

For eventual axiom elimination, the most promising route relative to the existing monorepo is:
  tower-level class-group Euler system + Kummer pairing/reflection + Iwasawa adjoint
rather than full ray-class CFT or Poitou–Tate.

Decisive trade-off: Vandiver now = specialised CFT black box; full IMC without ray classes later =
reflected class-group MC + Kummer duality. That cleanly separates a small, standard, auditable
assumption from the already machine-checked analytic and commutative-algebra core.
