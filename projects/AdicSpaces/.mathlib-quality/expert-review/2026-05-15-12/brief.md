## 1. Goal

Formalize Wedhorn's *Adic Spaces* Theorem 8.28(b) (the Tate-acyclicity / `IsSheafy` upgrade) in Lean 4 / Mathlib. The downstream payoff is the unconditional sheafiness of the structure presheaf on $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$ for a Tate Huber ring $A$, via Wedhorn's faithful-flatness route (Cor 8.31, Lemma 8.33) plus the topological transfer via Lemma 8.34's refinement-tree induction.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

- $A$ is a Tate Huber ring with $\mathrm{A}^+ \subseteq A$ a plus-subring; $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$ is the adic spectrum.
- For a rational localization datum $D = (P, T, s)$ (pair of definition $P = (A_0, I)$, finite $T \subseteq A$, denominator $s \in A$, openness condition), $\mathcal{O}(D) := \mathrm{presheafValue}(D)$ is the completion of $\mathrm{Localization.Away}(s) = A[s^{-1}]$ with respect to a designated topology, called the localization topology.
- For each $D$, there is a canonical ring homomorphism $D.\mathrm{canonicalMap} : A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ factoring as $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom} \circ \mathrm{algebraMap}$, where $\mathrm{algebraMap}$ is the natural map $A \to A[s^{-1}]$ and $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$ is the completion embedding.
- $\mathcal{O}(D)$ has dense image of $A[s^{-1}]$, is uniform, and is itself a topological ring (Huber/Tate when $A$ is).
- $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(T, s) := \{v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+) \mid \forall t \in T : v(t) \le v(s),\ v(s) \neq 0\}$.

### 2.3. State of the art

The Lean 4 formalization sits in a project of ~50 modules covering: valuation spectrum, adic spectrum, Huber rings, rational subsets, presheaf values, localization topology, faithful flatness of completed rational localizations (Wedhorn 8.30 reformulation), Cor 8.32, and a Lane C topological inducing development (Wedhorn-Example-6.38-aligned). ## 3. Strategy

Wedhorn 8.34 reduces "every rational covering has a Laurent refinement" to step-by-step machinery:

1. **W1** (Hübner-style Nullstellensatz). Replace the given rational covering $C$ by a standard cover $S \subseteq A$ that refines $C$ and spans the unit ideal of $A_0$.
2. **W2** (Cor 7.32 dominating unit). From $S$, produce a unit $s \in A^\times$ and a balanced outer Laurent tree on $\{s^{-1} \cdot f : f \in S\}$ with the property that at every outer leaf $L$, the restricted cover $C|_L$ is generated by units in $\mathcal{O}(L)$ (Wedhorn's Step (ii) "units in the local ring").
3. **W3** (Wedhorn Step (iii), relative). Given that $C|_L$ is generated by units $u_f \in \mathcal{O}(L)$, refine it by a Laurent tree built from pairwise ratios $u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$ at the level of $\mathcal{O}(L) = \mathrm{presheafValue}(L)$ — call it the *relative inner tree*.
4. **W3-transport**. Descend the relative inner tree to an absolute "ratio-Laurent" tree at the level of $A$; the ratio splits become Wedhorn's denominator-cleared $\{v(g) \le v(h)\}$-type rational subsets.
5. **I.1 assembly**. Graft the per-outer-leaf inner ratio trees onto the outer tree from W2; the result is the desired Laurent refinement.

The architecture went through eleven reviewer rounds (rounds 6 through 16) to settle on the correct intermediate types — in particular, `RatioLaurentTree A` (a tree whose leaves carry pair-comparisons $(g, h)$, not just single Laurent labels), `RatioNodeData D f g` (a validity package for one ratio split), and `RatioTreeRealization t D` (an indexed inductive coherently attaching `RatioNodeData` to each ratio node so that child subtrees see fixed sub-bases). Round 16 was the first "FINAL APPROVAL" — the architecture is locked.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 ($\mathrm{rationalOpen}$).** For $T \subseteq A$ finite and $s \in A$,
$\mathrm{rationalOpen}(T, s) := \{v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+) \mid \forall t \in T : v(t) \le v(s)\ \text{and}\ v(s) \neq 0\}.$

**Definition 4.2 ($\mathrm{RationalLocData}\ A$).** A *rational localization datum* on $A$ is the tuple $D = (P, T, s, h_{\mathrm{open}})$ where $P = (A_0, I)$ is a pair of definition, $T \subseteq A$ is finite, $s \in A$, and $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ is the assertion that there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for every $b \in I^N$, the formal fraction $b/s \in A[s^{-1}]$ lies in the *locSubring* $\mathrm{locSubring}(P, T, s) := A_0\!\left[\,t/s : t \in T\,\right] \subseteq A[s^{-1}]$. The presheaf value $\mathcal{O}(D)$ is the completion of $A[s^{-1}]$ with respect to the topology generated by the additive subgroups $I^n \cdot \mathrm{locSubring}$.

**Definition 4.3 ($\mathrm{RationalCovering}\ A$).** A finite indexed family $\{D_i\}_{i \in \iota}$ of `RationalLocData A`, each contained in a fixed base $D_{\mathrm{base}}$, such that $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D_{\mathrm{base}})$ is covered by the $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D_i)$.

**Definition 4.4 (`LaurentTree A`).** Inductive type: leaf, or `node f L R` where $f \in A$ is the split label and $L, R$ are sub-trees. A `LaurentTree` $t$ at base $D$ has *Refines* and *allSplitsInducing* predicates, defined by recursion: the splits at $f$ produce $\mathrm{laurentPlusDatum}\ D\ f$ and $\mathrm{laurentMinusDatum}\ D\ f$, each defined by the standard Wedhorn formula.

**Definition 4.5 (`RatioLaurentTree A`).** Inductive type with two `node` constructors: `nodeLaurent f L R` (an ordinary Laurent split at $f$) and `nodeRatio f g L R` (a ratio split at $(f, g)$, which conceptually cuts by $v(f) \le v(g)$ vs $v(g) \le v(f)$). The architecturally important new piece, post-round-12.

**Definition 4.6 (`RatioNodeData D f g`).** A validity package for one ratio split at $(f, g)$ over a base $D$. Its fields:

- $\mathrm{plus}, \mathrm{minus} : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\ A$,
- $\mathrm{plus\_subset} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$,
- $\mathrm{minus\_subset} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{minus}) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$,
- $\mathrm{cover\_proof} : \forall v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D), v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) \vee v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{minus})$,
- $\mathrm{plus\_open\_eq} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \mid v(f) \le v(g)\}$,
- $\mathrm{minus\_open\_eq} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{minus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \mid v(g) \le v(f)\}$.

**Definition 4.7 (`RatioTreeRealization t D`).** An indexed inductive type coherently attaching `RatioNodeData` to each `nodeRatio` of $t$, so that the child subtrees of `nodeRatio f g L R` see fixed sub-bases `data.plus` and `data.minus` (from the chosen `RatioNodeData D f g`). Predicates `ρ.Refines` and `ρ.allSplitsInducing` recurse along the same realization $\rho$.

**Definition 4.8 ($\mathrm{relativeUnitGenerator}\ L\ C\ f\ h_\mathrm{base}$).** Given a rational locality datum $L$ and the unit witness $h_\mathrm{base} : \mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s))$ in $\mathcal{O}(L)$, the *relative unit generator at $f$* is the element $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(f) \cdot (h_\mathrm{base}.\mathrm{unit}^{-1}) \in \mathcal{O}(L)$. Conceptually this is "the $f$-part of $C$ rescaled so $C.\mathrm{base}.s$ goes to 1". For $f$ in the standard cover, the relativeUnitGenerator at $f$ is a unit precisely when $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{insert}\ f\ C_\mathrm{base}.T,\ C_\mathrm{base}.s) \supseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L)$.

## 5. Established results (since round 16 approval)

**Theorem 5.1 (P1: `isUnit_relativeUnitGenerator_from_W2_unit`).** *Let $L : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\ A$, $C : \mathrm{RationalCovering}\ A$, $f \in A$, $s \in A^\times$. Suppose $\mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(s^{-1} \cdot f))$. Then the relative unit generator at $f$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.*

*Sketch.* Decompose $f = s \cdot (s^{-1} \cdot f)$. Apply $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$: it factors as $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(s) \cdot L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(s^{-1} \cdot f)$. The first factor is a unit because $s \in A^\times$ and ring maps preserve units; the second is a unit by hypothesis. The relative unit generator equals the first product times $h_\mathrm{base}.\mathrm{unit}^{-1}$, a third unit. Product of units. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (P2: `isUnit_base_s_in_presheafValue_of_subset`).** *Let $L, C$ be as above, and suppose $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(C_\mathrm{base})$. Then $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(C_\mathrm{base}.s)$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.*

*Sketch.* Direct application of Wedhorn Prop 8.2 (the existing project lemma `isUnit_canonicalMap_s`), with $D := C_\mathrm{base}$ and $D' := L$. ∎

**Theorem 5.3 (Bridge sub-lemma, partial; `comap_canonicalMap_not_vle_zero_of_isUnit_aux`).** *Let $D : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\ A$, $g \in A$, and $v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$. If $\mathrm{IsUnit}(D.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g))$, then $v(g) \neq 0$.*

*Sketch (intended).* From $v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$, extract $v(s) \neq 0$. Lift $v$ along the localization map to $w \in \mathrm{Spv}(A[s^{-1}])$ via the project's `localizationLift` (existing); $w$ is continuous in the localization topology (existing helper). From $\mathrm{IsUnit}(D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g)))$ in $\mathcal{O}(D)$, get an inverse $y \in \mathcal{O}(D)$ with $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g)) \cdot y = 1$. By density of $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$, approximate $y$ by $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}(y_n)$ with $y_n \in A[s^{-1}]$. Then $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g) \cdot y_n) \to 1$ in $\mathcal{O}(D)$, and by uniform-inducing of $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$, $\mathrm{algebraMap}(g) \cdot y_n \to 1$ in $A[s^{-1}]$ (locTopology). By continuity of $w$ and non-arch strict-triangle, $w(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g) \cdot y_n) = 1$ for all sufficiently large $n$. By multiplicativity, $w(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g)) \cdot w(y_n) = 1$, so $w(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g)) \neq 0$. By the lift identity $w \circ \mathrm{algebraMap} = v$, conclude $v(g) \neq 0$. ∎

**Status.** Steps 1–3 (extract $v(s) \neq 0$, build the powers-of-$s$-disjoint-from-$\mathrm{supp}(v)$ condition, perform the localization lift) are formalized. Steps 4–5 (density + non-arch + strict-triangle multiplicativity transfer) are documented as the focused next step.

## 8. Where we're stuck

### **Stuck point 8.1.** *`plus_open_eq` in `RatioNodeData L g h` requires a `RationalLocData A` whose `hopen` is not constructible from unit-in-$\mathcal{O}(L)$ alone.*

In the context of P3, the unit hypotheses give $\mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g))$ and $\mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h))$ in $\mathcal{O}(L) = \mathrm{presheafValue}(L)$. These are units in the *completion*, not in $A$ or in $A[L.s^{-1}]$.

The natural denominator-cleared construction of the plus piece, modelled on the round-13 sketch (denominator $L.s \cdot h$, numerators $\{L.s \cdot g\} \cup \{t \cdot h : t \in L.T\}$), gives:

$$\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}.T, \mathrm{plus}.s) = \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \cap \{v \mid v(g) \le v(h)\} \cap \{v \mid v(h) > 0\}.$$

The extra factor $\{v(h) > 0\}$ comes from $\mathrm{plus}.s = L.s \cdot h$ requiring $v(L.s \cdot h) > 0$, hence $v(h) > 0$. This factor is *not literally* in the RHS of `plus_open_eq`. The structure requires literal set equality

$$\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}.T, \mathrm{plus}.s) = \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \cap \{v \mid v(g) \le v(h)\}.$$

The bridge lemma (Theorem 5.3) is *one* of the two reductions needed: $\mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g)) \Rightarrow v(g) \neq 0$ on $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L)$, then $v(g) \le v(h)$ and $v(g) > 0$ force $v(h) > 0$. So the extra factor *does* collapse on the RHS, and `plus_open_eq` holds.

**This is not the obstruction; this works.** The obstruction is the *second* requirement: $\mathrm{plus}.\mathrm{hopen}$.

### **Stuck point 8.2.** *`plus.hopen` requires $1/h \in \mathrm{locSubring}$ but the project's `HasLocLiftPowerBounded` typeclass only supplies $1/s$.*

The `hopen` condition on $\mathrm{plus}$ is the requirement

$$\exists N : \forall b \in L.P.I^N,\ \mathrm{divByS}(b, L.s \cdot h) \in \mathrm{locSubring}(L.P, \mathrm{plus}.T, L.s \cdot h).$$

The standard reduction (used in the project's `laurentMinusDatum` / `ratioPlusDatum` proofs) factors $\mathrm{divByS}(b, L.s \cdot h)$ via `divByS_factor'` and `divByS_factor2'` into pieces of the form $\mathrm{divByS}(\ldots \cdot h, L.s \cdot h) = \ldots/L.s$ (in locSubring by $L$'s hopen via the lift `divByS_mul_f_mem'`) **and** $\mathrm{divByS}(L.s \cdot L.s, L.s \cdot h) = L.s / h$ (or equivalently $\mathrm{divByS}(L.s, L.s \cdot h) = 1/h$). The latter requires either:

- $L.s \cdot L.s \in \mathrm{plus}.T$ (the `laurentMinusDatum`-style approach), which adds the rationalOpen constraint $v(L.s) \le v(h)$ — *unwanted*, since $\{v(L.s) \le v(h)\}$ is not in the RHS of `plus_open_eq`; or
- $h_{\mathrm{inv}} \in L.P.A_0$ with $h \cdot h_{\mathrm{inv}} = 1$ (the `ratioPlusDatum`-style approach), so that $\mathrm{algebraMap}(h_{\mathrm{inv}}) = 1/h \in L.P.A_0 \subseteq \mathrm{locSubring}$ — *unavailable*, since the unit hypothesis lives in $\mathcal{O}(L)$ and gives no element of $A$.

Adding $1 \in \mathrm{plus}.T$ gives $\mathrm{divByS}(1, L.s \cdot h) = 1/(L.s \cdot h) \in \mathrm{locSubring}$, but adds the constraint $v(1) \le v(L.s \cdot h)$, i.e., $v(L.s \cdot h) \ge 1$ — also *unwanted*.

There is no choice of $\mathrm{plus}.T$ that simultaneously (a) gives the right rationalOpen equality (RHS of `plus_open_eq`) and (b) admits a proof of `hopen` from $L.P$ alone and the unit hypothesis in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.

### **What might resolve it (three approaches).**

**Option A — weaken `plus_open_eq` to a subset and strengthen `cover_proof`.** Change

$$\mathrm{plus\_open\_eq} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \mid v(f) \le v(g)\}$$

to

$$\mathrm{plus\_open\_in} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) \subseteq \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \mid v(f) \le v(g)\}.$$

The reverse inclusion (which fails without the bridge) is moved into `cover_proof`, which now needs to assert: "every $v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$ with $v(f) \le v(g)$ lies in $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus})$" (essentially the bridge-using direction). The denominator-cleared construction with `laurentMinus`-style hopen satisfies the subset version unconditionally. Downstream consumers (`RatioTreeRealization.Refines`, `allSplitsInducing`, W3-transport) need to be checked: do they need the equality or only the inclusion? Our reading is that the inclusion suffices for the topological transfer in `EmbeddingTopo.lean` but the user wants reviewer confirmation.

**Option B — parameterize `RatioNodeData` by a `plus.P` field that may differ from $L.P$.** Add a field `plus_P : PairOfDefinition A` to `RatioNodeData`, with the freedom to choose $A_0$ richer than $L.P.A_0$. In particular, one would choose $\mathrm{plus}.P$ such that $h_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{plus}.P.A_0$, constructed from the unit hypothesis in $\mathcal{O}(L)$ via Wedhorn's adic-Nullstellensatz extension. This requires a new infrastructure piece (a project lemma constructing `PairOfDefinition A` from a topologically-nilpotent unit witness in the completion), but preserves the literal equality in `plus_open_eq`.

**Option C — add a literal hypothesis $h_{\mathrm{inv}}, g_{\mathrm{inv}} \in L.P.A_0$ to the P3 statement.** Reduces the transport theorem to the classical `ratioPlusDatum` construction. Too restrictive: the only $g$ and $h$ available at the W3 callsite are units in $\mathcal{O}(L)$, not in $A$.

### **Stuck point 8.3.** *The bridge lemma's density + non-arch + strict-triangle step (Theorem 5.3 step 4–5) is not formalized.*

Steps 1–3 are done. Steps 4–5 require:

(a) **Density of $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$-image.** Existing in mathlib as `UniformSpace.Completion.denseRange_coe`.

(b) **Uniform-inducing of $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$.** Existing in project (used in `Cor832.lean`) via `UniformSpace.Completion.isUniformInducing_coe`.

(c) **Non-arch strict-triangle for the lifted valuation $w$.** $w$ is a Spv element on $A[L.s^{-1}]$; the strict-triangle is the standard non-archimedean fact $v(a + b) = \max\{v(a), v(b)\}$ when $v(a) \ne v(b)$. In the project's `Spv`/`ValuativeRel` formulation, this needs to be lifted from the underlying ValuativeRel to `w.vle`.

(d) **Filter-convergence preservation by $w$.** From $\mathrm{algebraMap}(g) \cdot y_n - 1 \to 0$ in the locTopology, conclude $w(\mathrm{algebraMap}(g) \cdot y_n - 1) < 1$ eventually. This is the standard $\mathrm{IsContinuous}$ property of $w$ at level $\gamma = 1$.

None of (a)–(d) is missing from the project or mathlib individually; the obstruction is the Lean *engineering* of the proof — filter-based convergence with non-archimedean tag, plus the careful chase of $w$'s value through the multiplication.
