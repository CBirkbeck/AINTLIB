## Assessment

The obstruction is real, but it does **not** invalidate the round-16 architecture. It shows that P3 cannot be proved by constructing a denominator-cleared rational datum over the **old pair** `L.P`.

The correct Wedhorn mechanism is:

```
ratio split lives over O(L)
→ represent/transport that relative rational subdomain as an absolute rational subdomain of Spa(A)
→ package the transported absolute datum as RatioNodeData.
```

It is **not**:

```
take denominator h in A and prove b/h ∈ locSubring(L.P,{g},h)
```

That latter statement is too rigid. A unit in `O(L)` need not have an inverse in `A`, in `A[L.s⁻¹]`, or in `locSubring(L.P,L.T,L.s)`. Wedhorn does not require such an inverse. He uses the stability/transitivity of rational subdomains under rational localization.

So the right route is not Option A, not Option C, and not Option I as stated. The best route is a refined form of Option II/IV:

> Keep `RatioNodeData` with literal rational-open equalities, but produce its `plus` and `minus` fields using a theorem that **relative rational subdomains of `O(L)` are represented by absolute rational data over `A`**, not by a hand-written denominator-cleared datum over `L.P`.

This is a local infrastructure gap, not an architecture failure.

## Mathematical idea

At the leaf `L`, Wedhorn Step (iii) works in the affinoid ring

```
B := O(L).
```

The units are

```
u_f = image_L(f) / image_L(C.base.s) ∈ B×.
```

The ratio split at `g,h` is the Laurent cover of `Spa(B)` generated by

```
r = u_g * u_h⁻¹.
```

The plus piece is

```
R_B({r}/1) = {w ∈ Spa(B) : w(r) ≤ 1}.
```

Under the equivalence

```
Spa(B) ≃ rationalOpen(L) ⊆ Spa(A),
```

this becomes

```
{v ∈ rationalOpen(L) : v(g) ≤ v(h)}.
```

That is the correct source of

```lean
plus_open_eq :
  rationalOpen data.plus.T data.plus.s =
    {v ∈ rationalOpen L.T L.s | v.vle g h}
```

The absolute `data.plus` should be **some** rational datum over `A` representing that transported rational subset. It need not be the naive datum

```
T = {L.s*g} ∪ {t*h | t ∈ L.T},  s = L.s*h,  P = L.P.
```

That naive datum has the right valuation locus when `h` is nonzero, but its `hopen` field is not forced by the old pair `L.P`. This is exactly why the proof stalls.

In Wedhorn's language, rational subdomains are stable under rational localization: a rational subset of `Spa(O(L))` corresponds to a rational subset of the original space contained in `L`, and the corresponding presheaf values agree. That theorem supplies the absolute datum, including its `hopen`.

So the missing theorem is not an "exact inverse in locSubring" lemma. It is a **relative-rational-to-absolute-rational representation theorem**.

## Answers

**Q1.** The right route is not Option A or C. Option A weakens the semantic data and risks breaking the inducing/transport story. Option C is too restrictive. Option I as stated is not the standard mechanism and likely does not give exact algebraic membership in the old locSubring. The correct route is: construct `RatioNodeData` via relative rational subdomain transport. This is closest to Option II/IV, but not by explicitly enriching `L.P`; rather, let the transport theorem produce whatever absolute rational datum is needed.

**Q2.** Wedhorn does not write down an absolute datum with old pair `L.P` for the ratio split. He works in `O(V_j)` and then uses stability of rational localizations/restrictions. The absolute rational datum is obtained by the general theorem that rational subsets of a rational subset are rational subsets of the original space.

**Q3.** `RatioNodeData` is still the right structure. Do not relax it to inclusions. The missing API is the relative-to-absolute rational representation theorem.

**Q4.** The targeted rational-open transport theorem is real and is the right replacement for the density proof. It does require the valuation-extension-to-completion theorem behind Wedhorn 7.49, but that is exactly the natural theorem here. Mathlib's field-only `Valued.extensionValuation` is not the right dependency; this is a topological-ring/Spa completion statement.

**Q5.** Pause P3 just long enough to replace its proof plan by the relative-transport theorem. W3 can be worked on in parallel, since it lives entirely over `presheafValue L`. But W3-transport should wait for the new P3 API.

## Risks or missing facts

The main risk is spending more time on the false old-pair `hopen` proof. That proof should not exist in the required generality.

The second risk is weakening `RatioNodeData` to inclusions. That may help `Refines`, but it will make `allSplitsInducing` and the topological transport less canonical. Keep the equality fields.

The third risk is underestimating the relative-to-absolute rational representation theorem. It is a real theorem, essentially the transitivity/stability of rational subdomains. But it is exactly the missing theorem Wedhorn uses, and it is more reusable than an approximate-inverse hack.

The fourth risk is trying to prove unit-nonvanishing using completion-density estimates. The Spa equivalence for `O(D)` is the cleaner and more durable route.
