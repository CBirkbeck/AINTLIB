# Review brief — Tate acyclicity / IsSheafy (round 18: round-17 transport approach also obstructed)

*Prepared 2026-05-15 for ChatGPT Pro (continuing series, follow-up to round 17). Self-contained: no repo access required.*

*Round 17 approved a reframing: keep `RatioNodeData.plus_open_eq` as a literal equality, but construct the absolute `RatioNodeData` by transporting a relative rational datum over `O(L)` back to `A` via the project's Group III equivalence. On attempting to formalize this round-17 transport, we find it has its own structural obstruction. Specifically: the relative Laurent split at the ratio `r = u_g · u_h⁻¹` is **not in the canonical form** required by the existing Group III machinery, because `r` involves a completion-side inverse that is not in `L.canonicalMap(A)`. After denominator-clearing the relative datum into canonical form, the corresponding absolute `RationalLocData A` requires `hopen` for denominator `s = h`, which still needs `1/h` to be expressible in the locSubring over `L.P` — the same algebraic obstacle we hit in round 16 with the direct-denominator-cleared approach. This brief asks: which Wedhorn-side mechanism produces this `hopen`?*

## 1. Goal

Formalize Wedhorn's *Adic Spaces* Theorem 8.28(b) (Tate-acyclicity / `IsSheafy` upgrade) in Lean 4 / Mathlib. Specifically, complete the existence theorem for a refining Laurent tree (Wedhorn 8.34) needed by the topological-inducing route.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

- $A$ is a Tate Huber ring with $\mathrm{A}^+ \subseteq A$ a plus subring.
- For a rational localization datum $D = (P, T, s)$ on $A$, $\mathcal{O}(D) = \mathrm{presheafValue}(D)$ is the completion of $A[s^{-1}]$ in the locTopology (the topology generated by $I^n \cdot \mathrm{locSubring}$).
- $D.\mathrm{canonicalMap} : A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ factors as $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom} \circ \mathrm{algebraMap}$, where $\mathrm{algebraMap}$ is $A \to A[s^{-1}]$ and $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom}$ is the completion embedding $A[s^{-1}] \to \mathcal{O}(D)$.
- `locSubring`$(P, T, s) := P.\mathrm{A}_0[t/s : t \in T] \subseteq A[s^{-1}]$ — the algebraic subring of the localization generated by $A_0$ and the fractions $t/s$.
- $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(T, s) := \{v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+) \mid \forall t \in T : v(t) \le v(s),\ v(s) \neq 0\}$.

A `RationalLocData A` is the tuple $(P, T, s, h_{\mathrm{open}})$ where $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ is the technical condition: there exists $N$ such that for every $b \in P.I^N$, $b/s$ (as an element of $A[s^{-1}]$) lies in `locSubring`$(P, T, s)$.

### 2.2. References

- [Wedhorn 2019] T. Wedhorn. *Adic Spaces*. arXiv:1910.05934. Lemma 7.44–7.45 (valuation extension); Lemma 7.50 (adic Nullstellensatz); Proposition 8.2 (rational containment ⇒ $s$ is a unit); Theorem 8.28(b) (sheafiness); Lemma 8.34 (refinement-tree induction).
- [Huber 1993] R. Huber. *Étale Cohomology of Rigid Analytic Varieties and Adic Spaces*. Aspects of Mathematics 30, Vieweg 1996.

### 2.3. State of the art

The Lean project has Group I (topological-inducing route), Group II (algebraic separation + gluing), Group III (transitivity / relative-equivalence machinery), Group IV (Spa-point existence — Wedhorn 7.45), and Group V (mathlib gaps). Most of Groups II, III, IV are closed; the remaining Group I work is the Wedhorn 8.34 existence theorem `T-LAURENT-REFINEMENT-TREE-EXISTENCE`, broken down into eight sub-tickets (W1, W2, W3, W3-transport, I.1 assembly, plus three bridge lemmas P1/P2/P3).

## 3. Strategy

Wedhorn 8.34 reduces the refining-Laurent-tree existence to step-by-step machinery:

1. **W1** (Hübner Nullstellensatz). Replace the given rational covering $C$ by a standard cover $S \subseteq A$ refining $C$ and spanning the unit ideal of $A_0$.
2. **W2** (Cor 7.32 dominating unit). From $S$, produce a unit $\sigma \in A^\times$ and a balanced outer Laurent tree on $\{\sigma^{-1} \cdot f : f \in S\}$, such that at every outer leaf $L$ the restricted cover $C|_L$ is generated by units $u_f \in \mathcal{O}(L)$.
3. **W3** (Wedhorn Step (iii), relative). Given $C|_L$ generated by units in $\mathcal{O}(L)$, refine by a Laurent tree at the level of $\mathcal{O}(L)$ whose splits are the pairwise ratios $u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$.
4. **W3-transport**. Descend the relative inner tree to a `RatioLaurentTree A`.
5. **I.1 assembly**. Graft the per-outer-leaf inner trees onto the outer tree.

P3 is the "key transport theorem" that converts each ratio split in the relative inner tree into an absolute `RatioNodeData L g h`. This is where the obstruction lives.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 (`RatioNodeData L f g`).** Given a base $L : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$ and elements $f, g \in A$, a `RatioNodeData L f g` is the validity package:

- $\mathrm{plus}, \mathrm{minus} : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$;
- $\mathrm{plus\_subset}, \mathrm{minus\_subset}$: rationalOpen-containment in $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L)$;
- $\mathrm{cover\_proof}$: the two pieces cover $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L)$;
- $\mathrm{plus\_open\_eq} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \mid v(f) \le v(g)\}$ (literal set equality);
- $\mathrm{minus\_open\_eq} : \mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{minus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \mid v(g) \le v(f)\}$.

**Definition 4.2 (relative unit generator at $f$).** Given $L$, a covering $C$, an element $f \in A$, and a unit witness $h_{\mathrm{base}} : \mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s))$, the *relative unit generator* is

$$u_f := L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(f) \cdot h_{\mathrm{base}}.\mathrm{unit}^{-1} \in \mathcal{O}(L).$$

For $f \in I_{\mathrm{units}}$ (an outer-leaf-derived index set), $u_f$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.

**Definition 4.3 (`relativeRationalLocData_laurentNormalized` — Group III project construction).** Given absolute $E, D : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$ with $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(E)$, and $D$ Laurent-normalized, the relative datum

$$D\,@\,E := (P_E, D.T \text{ imaged into } \mathcal{O}(E), E.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(D.s), h_{\mathrm{open}}^{\mathrm{relative}})$$

is a `RationalLocData (presheafValue E)`, with $P_E := \mathrm{presheafValue\_pairOfDefinition\_concrete}\, E$ the natural pair of definition on $\mathcal{O}(E)$. The $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ is inherited from $D.h_{\mathrm{open}}$ via a project-internal proof.

**Definition 4.4 (`presheafValue_relative_equiv` — Group III III.1 + III.2).** Under the same setup, there is a topological ring isomorphism

$$\mathcal{O}(D) \simeq_{\mathrm{top}} \mathcal{O}(D\,@\,E).$$

This is closed in the project, sorry-free.

## 5. Established results (round 16 + round 17)

**Theorem 5.1 (P1: `isUnit_relativeUnitGenerator_from_W2_unit`).** *Let $L, C, f, \sigma : A^\times$ be as above. If $\mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(\sigma^{-1} f))$, then $u_f$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.* (5-line proof; closed.)

**Theorem 5.2 (P2: `isUnit_base_s_in_presheafValue_of_subset`).** *If $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(C_{\mathrm{base}})$, then $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s)$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.* (1-line via Wedhorn Prop 8.2.)

**Theorem 5.3 (bridge sub-lemma; partial).** *For $D : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$, $g \in A$, $v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$: if $\mathrm{IsUnit}(D.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g))$, then $v(g) \neq 0$.*

*Round-17 sketch.* Lift $v$ to $w \in \mathrm{Spv}(\mathcal{O}(D))$ via the Spa equivalence (Wedhorn 7.49). The unit $D.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g)$ is never zero at any Spv point of $\mathcal{O}(D)$ ($\mathrm{not\_vle\_zero\_of\_isUnit}$). Pull back via $\mathrm{comap}\,D.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$. ∎

*Status*: the body of the proof is closed in this form (3 lines after the lift) but depends on the sub-lemma `rationalOpen_equiv_Spa_presheafValue_aux` (the Spa equivalence existence direction, Wedhorn 7.49), which itself is open. The round-17 reviewer suggested bypassing this with a "targeted rational-open transport" — see §8.2 below.

## 8. Where we're stuck

### **Stuck point 8.1.** *The round-17 transport approach has its own obstruction at the `hopen` step.*

The round-17 reviewer-mandated approach: build the relative Laurent split over $\mathcal{O}(L)$, then transport back to $A$ via the project's Group III equivalence.

**Concrete details of the obstruction.**

(a) The relative Laurent split at $r := u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$ has the form $\mathrm{plus}_{\mathrm{rel}} = (P_L, \{r\}, 1, h_{\mathrm{open}}^{\mathrm{rel}})$ where $P_L$ is the natural pair of definition on $\mathcal{O}(L)$.

(b) The Group III machinery (`relativeRationalLocData_laurentNormalized`) converts an absolute datum $D \subseteq E$ to a relative datum over $\mathcal{O}(E)$ with $T = D.T \text{ imaged via } E.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$ and $s = E.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(D.s)$.

(c) For the **inverse direction** (relative → absolute) to apply via Group III, the relative datum must be of the form $(\cdot, D.T \text{ imaged}, E.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(D.s), \cdot)$ for some absolute $D$. In particular: the relative $T$ and $s$ must lie in $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A) \subseteq \mathcal{O}(L)$.

(d) Our $\mathrm{plus}_{\mathrm{rel}}.s = 1 \in L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A)$. ✓ But $\mathrm{plus}_{\mathrm{rel}}.T = \{r\}$ contains $r = u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$, which is **not** in $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A)$: the inverse $u_h^{-1}$ lives in $\mathcal{O}(L)$, not in $A$ or even $A[L.s^{-1}]$.

(e) We can **denominator-clear** the relative datum: multiply numerator and denominator by $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h)$ (which is a unit by hypothesis). The rationalOpen is preserved because $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h)$ is a unit on every $w \in \mathrm{Spv}(\mathcal{O}(L))$, so the extra non-vanishing constraint $w(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h)) \neq 0$ is automatic. The cleared relative datum is

$$\mathrm{plus}_{\mathrm{rel}}^{\mathrm{cleared}} = (P_L,\; \{L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(g)\},\; L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h),\; h_{\mathrm{open}}^{\mathrm{rel,clean}}).$$

This datum *is* in canonical form: it equals `relativeRationalLocData_laurentNormalized L D''` for the absolute candidate

$$D'' := (L.P,\; \{g\},\; h,\; h_{\mathrm{open}}^{D''}).$$

(f) **The obstruction.** For $D''$ to be a valid `RationalLocData A`, its $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ must hold: there must exist $N$ such that for every $b \in L.P.I^N$,

$$b/h \in \mathrm{locSubring}(L.P, \{g\}, h) = L.P.\mathrm{A}_0[g/h] \subseteq A[h^{-1}].$$

This is the same algebraic obstacle we encountered in the round-16 direct approach: to express $b/h$ in terms of $A_0$ and $g/h$, we need $1/h$ to be expressible in the locSubring. This requires either $h_{\mathrm{inv}} \in L.P.A_0$ with $h \cdot h_{\mathrm{inv}} = 1$ (which is **not** available — $h$ is only a completion-side unit) or some adic-Nullstellensatz-style "approximate inverse" lemma.

(g) Note: the Group III equivalence (`presheafValue_relative_equiv`) is one-direction in this sense — it goes from an absolute $D$ to a relative $D \,@\, E$. The reverse direction, *constructing* an absolute $D$ from a relative datum, requires building $D$'s $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ separately. Group III alone does not provide it.

### **Stuck point 8.2.** *The original bridge-lemma route ("Spa equivalence Wedhorn 7.49") is also blocked.*

Wedhorn 7.49 (continuous valuations on $\mathcal{O}(D)$ bijective with continuous valuations of $A$ lying in $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D)$) gives the natural Spa equivalence. The forward direction (Spv on $\mathcal{O}(D)$ → rationalOpen-on-$A$ via comap) is in the project. The reverse direction (rationalOpen-on-$A$ → Spv on $\mathcal{O}(D)$) requires extending a continuous valuation $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ on $A[L.s^{-1}]$ to a continuous valuation on the completion $\mathcal{O}(L) = \widehat{A[L.s^{-1}]}$.

Mathlib has `Valued.extensionValuation` for **fields**, not for commutative topological rings. The commutative-ring analogue is genuinely missing from mathlib.

### **What would resolve the obstruction (concrete asks).**

**Option I** ("approximate inverse" lemma). Prove the following adic-Nullstellensatz-style result and use it to discharge $D''.h_{\mathrm{open}}$:

> Let $A$ be a Tate Huber ring, $L : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$, $h \in A$. Suppose $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(h)$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$. Then there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ and $h_{\mathrm{inv}}^{(N)} \in L.P.A_0$ such that $h \cdot h_{\mathrm{inv}}^{(N)} \equiv 1 \pmod{L.P.I^N \cdot A}$.

From this, $b/h \in L.P.A_0[\ldots] \subseteq \mathrm{locSubring}$ for $b \in L.P.I^N$, giving $D''.h_{\mathrm{open}}$.

**Option II** (different absolute pair $D''.P \neq L.P$). Construct a different pair of definition $P'' : \mathrm{PairOfDefinition}\,A$ — possibly *smaller* than $L.P$ — for which $D''.h_{\mathrm{open}}$ holds. Specifically: $P'' = (A_0'', I'')$ where $I''$ is chosen so that $I''^N$ is "stable under division by $h$". Constructing such a $P''$ from the completion-unit hypothesis is itself non-trivial.

**Option III** (relax `RatioNodeData.plus_open_eq` to a *predicate on rationalOpen-image*, not literal set equality). Instead of demanding $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}.T, \mathrm{plus}.s) = R(L) \cap \{v(g) \le v(h)\}$, accept the image of the relative rationalOpen under $\mathrm{comap}\,L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$. This relaxes the structural commitment but pushes the downstream consumers (RatioTreeRealization.Refines / allSplitsInducing / `productRestrictionSub_isInducing_via_ratio_tree`) to work with image-equalities rather than direct rationalOpens. Round 17 explicitly ruled this out as "too weak for the clean transport/inducing story" — but is it actually too weak for the *downstream* consumers? That depends on what those consumers specifically need.

**Option IV** (different relative split target). Build the relative split not at $r = u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$ but at a "lifted" element of $\mathcal{O}(L)$ that *is* in $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A)$. For instance, the "Spa-equivalent" relative rational subset using algebraic generators in $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A)$ directly. Need a clear construction.

## 9. Open mathematical questions for the reviewer

**Q1 (lead).** Of the four options I/II/III/IV in §8 above, which is the correct route forward? Specifically:

- Option I (approximate-inverse lemma): is this the standard Wedhorn-side mechanism, formalisable from Wedhorn Lemma 7.45 + Cor 7.50?
- Option II (different $D''.P$): is this the cleaner route? If so, how is $P''$ constructed from the completion-unit hypothesis?
- Option III (relax `plus_open_eq` to image equality): was round 17's rejection of this final, or might it be acceptable if the downstream consumers do only use the image direction?
- Option IV (different relative target): is there a standard reformulation of the relative split that avoids the completion-inverse issue entirely?

**Q2 (mechanism check).** In Wedhorn's own write-up of Lemma 8.34 Step (iii), how does the descent of the ratio-Laurent cover from $\mathcal{O}(L)$ back to $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$ avoid this obstacle? Specifically: at the level of an individual ratio split, what is the absolute rational locality datum (i.e., the $T$, $s$, and pair of definition $P$) that Wedhorn uses, and how is its $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ established?

**Q3 (architectural).** If both round-17's transport approach and the original direct approach hit the same `1/h ∈ locSubring` obstacle, does this indicate the `RatioNodeData` structure itself needs to be relaxed (Option III), or does it indicate a missing project-internal API piece (the "approximate inverse" lemma, Option I)? Round 17 said the architecture is sound; round 18 asks whether this re-shows up as a more subtle issue in the construction.

**Q4 (bridge lemma).** The same `1/h` issue suggests the bridge lemma (`comap_canonicalMap_not_vle_zero_of_isUnit_aux`) — currently relying on the Spa equivalence — may not have a simpler proof either. Is the targeted rational-open transport theorem (per round 17 alternative) realisable, or does it have the same `1/h` issue under the hood?

**Q5 (whole architecture).** Is the round-16 + round-17 architecture genuinely correct *as a target* but stuck on a missing mathlib-grade "approximate inverse" infrastructure piece, OR does the recurring `1/h` obstacle indicate that the right Lean target is a slightly different structure that handles approximate inverses inside `RatioNodeData` (e.g., by taking the pair of definition $P''$ as a field of `RatioNodeData`, not fixing it to $L.P$)?
