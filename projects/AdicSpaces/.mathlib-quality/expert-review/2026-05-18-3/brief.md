## 3. The four mathematical questions

### Q-S24.1 — Does Wedhorn Example 6.38 produce a noetherian ring of definition?

Wedhorn's Example 6.38 (p.54) says: if $A$ is a strongly noetherian Tate ring with pair of definition $(A_0, I)$, then for $D = R(T/s)$ a rational subset, the structure sheaf value $\mathcal{O}_X(D)$ is again a strongly noetherian Tate ring.

The natural construction Wedhorn gives produces a specific pair of definition for $\mathcal{O}_X(D)$ — typically the closure of $A_0\langle T/s \rangle$ inside $\mathcal{O}_X(D)$, with the ideal generated by the image of $I$.

**Question.** Is the constructed ring of definition $\mathcal{O}_X(D)_0$ automatically **noetherian** when $A_0$ is, even though the abstract claim "strongly noeth Tate ⇒ noeth ring of def" is false (as established by the $\mathbb{C}_p$ counterexample)?

Mathematically, the issue is:
- $A_0$ noetherian + $A_0$ is $I$-adic ⇒ Stacks 0316 ⇒ $\widehat{A_0}$ ($I$-adic completion) is noetherian.
- $\mathcal{O}_X(D)_0$ is some adic completion of an $A_0$-finite (or $A_0$-finitely-generated) extension — if this is true, $\mathcal{O}_X(D)_0$ inherits noetherianity from $A_0$.

If the answer is yes, then in the project, given $(A, A_0, I)$ with $A_0$ noetherian, the canonical pair of definition for every $\mathcal{O}_X(D)$ is also noetherian. The project's $(P, [\mathrm{IsNoetherianRing}\,P.A_0])$ hypothesis on $A$ would automatically supply a noeth-pair on $\mathcal{O}_X(D)$ via Example 6.38. That would unblock the chain considerably: every sub-step of Prop 8.30 / Cor 8.32 that requires "$\mathcal{O}_X(D)$ has noeth ring of def" gets it for free.

If the answer is no, then the project will need to either (a) thread the noeth pair through the chain manually, or (b) restrict to a setting where it's automatic.

**The specific reference.** Does Wedhorn (or Huber, BGR) explicitly claim or prove that the canonical Example-6.38 pair-of-definition is noetherian when the original is? Or is this a routine consequence of Stacks 0316?

### Q-S24.2 — Does Wedhorn 8.31 actually need $A_0$ noetherian (not just $A$)?

Wedhorn Lemma 8.31 (p.82) is stated for "noetherian Tate ring $A$". The proof goes via Remark 8.29: $A\langle X \rangle \cong \widehat{A_0} \otimes_{A_0} A_0[X]$ for some I-adic completion $\widehat{A_0}$ of a pair-of-definition $A_0$.

**Question.** Is the proof of Wedhorn 8.31 valid under just the hypothesis "$A$ noetherian Tate" (i.e., $A$ noetherian as an abstract ring, plus Tate), or does it implicitly require "$A_0$ noetherian for some pair of definition $(A_0, I)$" (i.e., what the project encodes as $[\mathrm{IsNoetherianRing}\,P.A_0]$)?

The technical concern:
- "$A$ noetherian" + "$A$ Tate" + "$A$ has a pair of definition $(A_0, I)$" gives us $A_0$ as some subring — but not necessarily noetherian.
- Wedhorn's proof seems to implicitly use that $\widehat{A_0}$ is well-behaved (faithfully flat over $A_0$, $A_0$-modules behave well, etc.). These typically need $A_0$ noetherian.
- Specifically, "$\widehat{A_0}$ is faithfully flat over $A_0$" needs $A_0$ noetherian (or some weaker condition).

If Wedhorn 8.31 silently uses $A_0$ noetherian, then:
- The project's `TateAlgebra.faithfullyFlat_general` taking $[\mathrm{IsNoetherianRing}\,P.A_0]$ is the **honest** hypothesis profile, and Wedhorn's "noeth Tate" is shorthand for the parametric form.
- Path α (the project's commit) is the correct formalisation.

If Wedhorn 8.31 truly works with just "$A$ noetherian Tate" abstractly:
- The project's `TateAlgebra.faithfullyFlat_general` is over-tight, and could be weakened. That would partially unblock C1 (Prop 8.30) without needing P explicitly.

**What we need.** A clear verdict on whether 8.31's proof can be done without $A_0$ noeth, or whether it's secretly used.

### Q-S24.3 — How does Wedhorn 8.34 handle non-domain $A$?

Wedhorn Lemma 8.34 (p.84) is stated for strongly noetherian Tate $A$. No "IsDomain" hypothesis.

But the proof — via reduction to a Laurent cover and decomposition into $R(f/g) \cup R(g/f)$ for cover-generating elements $f, g$ — appears (to us) to require that products of cover generators behave well. The $A = k\langle T, U \rangle / (TU)$ counterexample (which you flagged round-2 as the G2 case) shows that the naive Laurent split $R(T/U) \cup R(U/T)$ degenerates in non-domain settings: there's no Spa-point separating $T$-vanishing from $U$-vanishing, because $TU = 0$ means at least one of them always vanishes.

**Question.** How does Wedhorn's Lemma 8.34 proof actually handle the non-domain case? Specifically:

- (a) Does Wedhorn implicitly use $A$ domain in step (i)–(iv) of the proof, and the statement is in fact only correct for $A$ domain?
- (b) Does Wedhorn's proof use a different decomposition (not Laurent splits) that's domain-free?
- (c) Is the non-domain case handled by a different reduction (e.g., quotient by the nilradical, prove for the reduced ring, lift back)?

If (a): the project's `[IsDomain A]` hypothesis in A1/A2/F4 is honest. F5 (the "no IsDomain" target) should be deleted as untrue.

If (b) or (c): the project needs to refactor F4 to use whichever decomposition Wedhorn uses. This is significant new infrastructure.

### Q-S24.4 — Does A3 need explicit $[\mathrm{CompleteSpace}\,A]$ in its signature?

Wedhorn 8.28(b) (p.80) is stated for "$A$ strongly noetherian Tate" but applies to **complete** affinoid rings (the convention earlier in Wedhorn is that all affinoid rings are complete by definition, or that completion is implicit; this is unclear to us).

The project's $\mathrm{IsTateRing}\,A$ typeclass extends $\mathrm{IsHuberRing}\,A$ extends $\mathrm{IsTopologicalRing}\,A$. **Neither implies $\mathrm{CompleteSpace}\,A$.**

The chain of proofs for Wedhorn 8.31 ($A\langle X \rangle$ flat etc.) uses completeness explicitly (the completion construction needs the base to be either complete or the completion to be the relevant object).

**Question.** Does Wedhorn 8.28(b) implicitly assume $A$ is **complete** (in addition to strongly noetherian Tate)? If yes, the project's A3 signature is missing $[\mathrm{CompleteSpace}\,A]$ and should add it. If no (i.e., the theorem holds for incomplete strongly noeth Tate too, perhaps because completion of strongly noeth Tate is again strongly noeth Tate and 8.28(b) for $A$ follows from 8.28(b) for $\widehat A$), then where is that argument in Wedhorn?

The reason this matters: with Path α already in place, A3 is now a parametric variant. Adding $[\mathrm{CompleteSpace}\,A]$ would further narrow it. We want to know if it's required for honest formalisation or if it's redundant.

(For context: Wedhorn defines "complete affinoid ring" at the start of §7, and most of the §8 results inherit that assumption. We want to know if it's load-bearing on 8.28(b) specifically.)
