## 1. Goal

Formalise **Wedhorn Theorem 8.28(b)** (Tate acyclicity) for strongly noetherian Tate rings:
for every finite rational covering $\{U_i\}$ of the adic spectrum $\operatorname{Spa}(A, A^+)$ of a
strongly noetherian Tate Huber pair $(A, A^+)$, the canonical map
$$
\mathcal{O}_X(\operatorname{Spa}(A, A^+)) \;\longrightarrow\; \prod_i \mathcal{O}_X(U_i)
$$
is an equaliser of the two natural restriction maps to $\prod_{i,j} \mathcal{O}_X(U_i \cap U_j)$.
Equivalently: the structure presheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ is a sheaf of complete topological rings on
rational opens.

This is the central acyclicity result needed to conclude that
$\operatorname{Spa}(A, A^+)$ is a *sheafy adic space* (Wedhorn Theorem 8.28) and hence
to establish the standard adic-space functoriality used throughout perfectoid /
p-adic Hodge / nonarchimedean geometry.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

*Huber pair.* A pair $(A, A^+)$ where $A$ is a complete Hausdorff topological ring and
$A^+ \subseteq A$ is an open, integrally closed subring of power-bounded elements
($A^+ \subseteq A^\circ$). A **Huber ring** is a topological ring $A$ admitting an open
subring $A_0$ (a *ring of definition*) with a finitely-generated ideal of definition
$I \subseteq A_0$ making the $I$-adic topology coincide with the subspace topology.
A **Tate ring** is a Huber ring containing a topologically nilpotent unit.
A pair $(A, A^+)$ is **strongly noetherian** if $A\langle X_1, \dots, X_n \rangle$ is
noetherian for every $n$.

*Adic spectrum.* $\operatorname{Spa}(A, A^+)$ is the set of equivalence classes of continuous
valuations $v$ on $A$ satisfying $v(a) \leq 1$ for all $a \in A^+$. Topology: open subsets
generated by *rational opens* $R(T/s) := \{v : v(t) \leq v(s) \neq 0 \text{ for all } t \in T\}$
for $T \subseteq A$ finite and $s \in A$ such that $T \cdot A$ is open.

*Presheaf.* For a rational open $U = R(T/s)$, the value $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ is the completion
of $A_0[T/s][1/s]$ in the topology making $T/s$ power-bounded — equivalently the
completion of the "rational localization" $A\langle T/s \rangle$. Restriction maps are
induced by inclusions $R(T'/s') \subseteq R(T/s)$ via the universal property of completed
rational localizations.

*Notation conventions.* We write $D_0$ for a base rational locale datum (the $(P, T, s)$
triple) and $D$ for an arbitrary rational locale that refines $D_0$. We write
$\mathcal{O}(D) = \mathcal{O}_X(R(T/s))$ for the presheaf value. The
*canonical map* $A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ is the composite
$A \to \operatorname{Localization.Away}(s) \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ (algebraic localization
followed by completion).

## 3. Strategy

The proof decomposes into three planes:

1. **Algebraic plane — Cor 8.32 product faithful flatness.** Build the faithful flatness
   of the product restriction $\mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \prod_i \mathcal{O}(D_i)$ via:
   (a) Wedhorn Prop 8.30 (regular sequence / flat quotient of Tate algebra by $1-fX$);
   (b) Example 6.38 (presheaf value as Tate-algebra quotient);
   (c) Lemma 2.13 (iterated rational localization identification);
   (d) abstract Cor 8.32 (descent from componentwise flatness plus prime surjectivity).

2. **Geometric plane — Lane C reduction.** Reduce an arbitrary rational covering to a
   finite *Laurent decomposition* via Wedhorn 8.34 / Hübner 3.8. Each step replaces a
   cover by a 2-element Laurent cover $\{R(T \cup \{f\} / s),\ R(T / (sf))\}$ at a chosen
   $f$ controlling power-boundedness. Induct on the depth of the decomposition.

3. **Gluing plane — Laurent Čech exactness.** For each 2-element Laurent decomposition
   $\{D_+, D_-\}$, prove the Čech complex
   $0 \to \mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \mathcal{O}(D_+) \times \mathcal{O}(D_-)
   \to \mathcal{O}(D_+ \cap D_-) \to 0$
   is exact (Wedhorn Lemma 8.33). Combined with the geometric reduction and the
   algebraic faithful flatness, this yields full Tate acyclicity.

### 3.1. The post-session-2 reframe

In session 2, the reviewer correctly diagnosed that an earlier formalization attempt
based on **Wedhorn Prop 8.15 as an algebraic `IsLocalization.Away` predicate** was
mathematically false in general. Concretely: in $A = \mathbb{Q}_p\langle X \rangle$, the
completed rational localization $A\langle T \rangle / (XT - 1)$ contains
$\sum_{n \geq 0} p^n X^{-n}$, an infinite convergent negative-power tail that no finite
power $X^N$ clears. Hence the restriction maps for rational covers are not algebraically
"localizations away from one element" in mathlib's sense.

The reviewer's prescribed fix — *refactor the Cor 8.32 abstract to consume `Module.Flat`
of each restriction map directly, with flatness supplied by Wedhorn 8.30 + Lemma 2.13 via
Tate-algebra quotient identifications* — has been **executed**. The current report is on
the architectural decision that this reframe surfaced as the new critical-path question.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 (`laurentPlusDatum D₀ f`).** For a rational locale datum $D_0 = (P, T, s)$
and $f \in A$, the *plus-piece* is the rational datum with the same $P$, with
$T' = T \cup \{f\}$, and the same denominator $s$. Geometrically, this carves out the
sub-rational-open $\{v \in R(T/s) : v(f) \leq v(s)\}$.

**Definition 4.2 (`laurentMinusDatum D₀ f`).** The *minus-piece* is the rational datum
with $T''$ given by the image of $(T \cup \{s\}) \times \{s, f\}$ under pairwise
multiplication, and denominator $s \cdot f$. Geometrically, this carves out the
sub-rational-open $\{v \in R(T/s) : v(s) \leq v(f)\}$.

**Definition 4.3 (`laurentCovering D₀ f`).** The 2-element Laurent covering of $D_0$ at
element $f$: covers $= \{D_+, D_-\}$ where $D_\pm = \text{laurent}\pm \text{Datum}(D_0, f)$.

**Definition 4.4 (`iteratedMinusDatum_B P D₀ f`).** Wedhorn 2.13 identification: at the
B-level $B := \mathcal{O}(D_0)$, the cover piece "minus shape at $f$" is the rational
datum over $B$ with singleton $T = \{1\}$ and denominator $s = D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)$
(the image of $f$ in $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$). Mathematically: the minus-piece of $D_0$ at $f$
viewed from inside $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$ becomes "invert $f$ and complete."

**Definition 4.5 (`iteratedPlusDatum_B P D₀ f`).** Analogous: at B-level, $T = \{f\}$,
$s = 1$. Mathematically: the plus-piece of $D_0$ at $f$ viewed from inside
$\mathcal{O}(D_0)$ becomes "make $f$ power-bounded and complete" — no localization
needed since $s = 1$ is a unit.

**Definition 4.6 (`per_E_local_covering` — the existing assembly construct).** Given a
rational covering $C = (D_0, \{E_i\}_i)$ and a finite set $S \subseteq A$ refining $C$
with chosen $f_0 \in A$, for each $E \in C\text{.covers}$ the per-$E$ local covering of
$E$ has cover pieces
$$
\bigl\{\text{laurentPlusDatum}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f,\ f_0) : f \in S, R(T \cup \{f\}/s) \subseteq E\bigr\}
\ \cup\
\bigl\{\text{laurentMinusDatum}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f,\ f_0) : f \in S, R(T \cup \{f\}/s) \subseteq E\bigr\}.
$$
Here $C.\text{plusDatum}\,f := \text{laurentPlusDatum}(D_0, f)$. So the per-$E$ pieces
are *iterated* (two-fold) Laurent shapes of $D_0$, not direct Laurent shapes of $E$.
**This is the construct whose flatness-over-$\mathcal{O}(E)$ is the central stuck point.**

**Definition 4.7 (`flat_over_base_tate_laurent`).** Supplier of $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$-flatness
for the cover pieces of a covering $C$ whose pieces are *direct* Laurent-minus shapes
of $D_0$. Discharged unconditionally (no sorry) via the new route. Analogous
$\text{flat\_over\_base\_tate\_laurent\_combined}$ handles plus + minus disjunctive
witness.

**Definition 4.8 (`tateAcyclicityComplete`).** Hypothesis-parameterised final wrapper.
Takes abstract suppliers `global_separation`, `lane_A_supplier`, `lane_B_supplier` and
produces Part 1 (separation) and Part 2 (gluing) of Tate acyclicity. The reframe's success
criterion: *every supplier this wrapper consumes can in principle be discharged
sorry-free* via the new flatness route.

## 5. Established results

**Theorem 5.1 (presheaf-value flatness via Tate-algebra quotient).** *Let $A$ be a
strongly noetherian Tate ring with chosen pair of definition $P$. For any rational locale
$D$ over $A$, the canonical map $A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ makes $\mathcal{O}(D)$ a flat
$A$-module, modulo the standard canonical-topology hypotheses:* `invS D` *is
power-bounded;* $A$ *is complete;* the local pair-subring is noetherian; the elements
$t \in T$ are power-bounded; and the canonical evaluation map
$A\langle X \rangle / (1 - sX) \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ is continuous in the
$I$-adic-on-coefficients topology.*

*Sketch.* The Tate-algebra quotient $A\langle X \rangle / (1 - sX)$ is flat over $A$ by
Wedhorn Prop 8.30 (multiplication by $1 - sX$ is regular on $A\langle X \rangle$-modules).
Under the listed hypotheses, this quotient is canonically isomorphic to $\mathcal{O}(D)$
as $A$-algebra (Example 6.38). Transport flatness along the isomorphism. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (Wedhorn 2.13 — minus identification).** *In the setting above with
$B = \mathcal{O}(D_0)$, there is an isomorphism of complete topological $A$-algebras
$\mathcal{O}(\text{laurentMinusDatum}(D_0, f)) \cong \mathcal{O}(\text{iteratedMinusDatum\_B}(P, D_0, f))$
where the LHS is the minus-piece of $D_0$ at $f$ and the RHS is the rational locale over
$B$ with $T = \{1\}$, $s = D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)$.*

*Sketch.* The forward and backward localization homomorphisms are constructed via
`IsLocalization.Away.lift` applied to the chains
$A \to A[1/s] \to A[1/(sf)]$ and $A \to B \to B[1/D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)]$. Continuity
on both sides (proved separately as the cited theorems
`iteratedMinus_forwardToCompletion_continuous` and `iteratedMinus_backwardLocHom_continuous`),
plus the round-trip identity at the dense `coeRingHom`-image, give the equiv via
`UniformSpace.Completion.extensionHom` + `UniformSpace.Completion.ext'`. ∎

**Theorem 5.3 (Wedhorn 2.13 — plus identification).** Analogous to Theorem 5.2 but for
the plus-shape, identifying $\mathcal{O}(\text{laurentPlusDatum}(D_0, f))$ with
$\mathcal{O}(\text{iteratedPlusDatum\_B}(P, D_0, f))$ (a rational locale over $B$ with
$T = \{D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)\}$, $s = 1$). Established.

**Theorem 5.4 (`restrictionMap_flat_via_iteratedMinus`).** *In the strongly noetherian
Tate setting, the restriction map $\mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \mathcal{O}(\text{laurentMinusDatum}(D_0, f))$
is flat as a homomorphism of $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$-modules.*

*Sketch.* By Theorem 5.2, the target is isomorphic to
$\mathcal{O}(\text{iteratedMinusDatum\_B}(P, D_0, f))$ as a $B$-algebra. Apply Theorem 5.1
at the B-level (with $A := B$, $D := \text{iteratedMinusDatum\_B}\dots$). The
$T = \{1\}$ structure makes `hT_pb` and `hb` (the power-bounded hypotheses) automatic.
Transfer flatness along the B-level isomorphism. ∎

**Theorem 5.5 (`restrictionMap_flat_via_iteratedPlus`).** *In the strongly noetherian
Tate setting, assuming that $D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)$ is power-bounded in $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$,
the restriction map $\mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \mathcal{O}(\text{laurentPlusDatum}(D_0, f))$
is flat.*

*Sketch.* Theorem 5.3 + Theorem 5.1 at B-level (with $T = \{D_0\text{.canonicalMap}(f)\}$,
$s = 1$). The $s = 1$ structure makes `invS = 1` and `hb` automatic; the extra
power-bounded hypothesis discharges `hT_pb`. ∎

**Theorem 5.6 (Cor 8.32 abstract).** *Let $C$ be a finite rational covering of $D_0$
with each $D_i \in C\text{.covers}$ giving $\mathcal{O}(D_i)$ flat over $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$
via the restriction map, and assume the prime-spectrum surjectivity
$\operatorname{Spec}\bigl(\prod_i \mathcal{O}(D_i)\bigr) \twoheadrightarrow \operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal{O}(D_0))$
(equivalently the cover sees every prime via at least one piece). Then the product
restriction $\mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \prod_i \mathcal{O}(D_i)$ is faithfully flat as a ring
homomorphism.*

*Sketch.* Componentwise flatness + finite product = product flatness (mathlib
`Module.Flat.pi_of_algebra`). Faithful flatness via mathlib
`Module.FaithfullyFlat.of_comap_surjective` consuming the prime-surjectivity hypothesis. ∎

**Theorem 5.7 (faithfully flat product for Laurent-shape covers).** *Drop-in version of
Theorem 5.6 applicable when $C$'s cover pieces are all direct Laurent shapes (plus or
minus) of $D_0$, with flatness component-supplied by Theorems 5.4 and 5.5.*

**Theorem 5.8 (sheafy formal cap).** `tateAcyclicityComplete`: from abstract suppliers
of cover-level separation, refinement-level overlap gluing, and per-$E$ local separation,
the Part 1 (separation) and Part 2 (gluing) clauses of Wedhorn 8.28(b) both follow.
Established as a hypothesis-parameterised wrapper.

**Theorem 5.9 (isSheafy structural wrapper).**
`isSheafy_ofStronglyNoetherianTate_flat_of_topo_inducing`: given a supplier
`topo_inducing` of topological inducing for the product restriction (the "Embedding"
field of `IsSheafy`), assembles a full `IsSheafy A` instance for strongly noetherian Tate
$A$, with the algebraic side (separation, gluing) supplied internally via the Laurent
refinement route.

## 6. In progress

### 6.1. Working on: `T-FLAT-PER-E` — per-$E$ flatness for the existing `per_E_local_covering`

**Status.** This is the *one remaining structural blocker* for sorry-free Tate acyclicity
via the corrected route.

**Setup.** The assembly architecture requires a `lane_B_supplier` of the form: for every
standard cover $S$ refining $C$, for every $E \in C\text{.covers}$, the restriction
$\mathcal{O}(E) \to \prod \mathcal{O}(D)$ (over $D$ in `per_E_local_covering` $S, f_0, E$)
is injective. This is supplied by Cor 8.32 applied at $E$, which requires
$\mathcal{O}(D)$ to be **flat over $\mathcal{O}(E)$** along the restriction map, for each
$D$ in `per_E_local_covering`.

**The problem.** The cover pieces of `per_E_local_covering` are iterated shapes:
$D = \text{laurent}\pm\text{Datum}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f,\ f_0)$ where
$C.\text{plusDatum}\,f = \text{laurentPlusDatum}(D_0, f)$. These are *twice-iterated*
Laurent shapes of $D_0$, with the intermediate level $C.\text{plusDatum}\,f$ sitting
between $D_0$ and the cover piece. The cover piece is contained in $E$ (by the geometric
filter), but $C.\text{plusDatum}\,f \subseteq E$ is **not** a Laurent shape of $E$ in
general — $E$'s intrinsic $(T_E, s_E)$ data is independent of $f$.

Theorem 5.4 / Theorem 5.5 give flatness of the cover piece *over*
$\mathcal{O}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f)$, *not* over $\mathcal{O}(E)$. The natural composition
$\mathcal{O}(E) \to \mathcal{O}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f) \to \mathcal{O}(\text{cover piece})$
gives flatness over $\mathcal{O}(E)$ only if the first arrow is also flat — but
$C.\text{plusDatum}\,f$ is an arbitrary intermediate rational subset, not generally a
Laurent shape relative to $E$.

**What's been built towards closure.**
- Theorem 5.4 (minus) and Theorem 5.5 (plus) — sorry-free flatness when the cover piece
  is a *direct* Laurent shape of $D_0$.
- Theorem 5.7 — faithfully flat product when *all* pieces are direct Laurent shapes
  of $D_0$ (no iterated nesting).
- *Sub-theorem.* `productRestriction_faithfullyFlat_laurentCovering_at_E`:
  faithfully flat product over $\mathcal{O}(E)$ for the **direct** 2-element Laurent
  covering of $E$ at any element $f$ (using $E$'s own $(T_E, s_E)$ as the base, not the
  outer $D_0$'s data). **Sorry-free.** This shows the corrected route *can* close per-$E$
  separation, but only via a 2-element direct covering, not the existing iterated
  `per_E_local_covering`.

**The architectural decision.** Two routes forward:

- *Route A (refactor):* Replace `per_E_local_covering` with a new direct-E-shape per-$E$
  covering — e.g., choose for each $E$ a single $f_E \in A$ and use the 2-element
  $\text{laurentCovering}(E, f_E)$ as the per-$E$ refinement. Then the per-$E$ flatness
  is immediate via the sub-theorem above. Cost: rewrite the geometric reduction
  (`refines_by_standard_cover_per_E` etc.) and the assembly wrappers
  (`tateAcyclicity_Part2_end_to_end_via_primary` and its consumers) to consume the new
  per-$E$ shape.

- *Route B (iterated 2.13):* Build a generalisation of Wedhorn 2.13 / Theorem 5.2 that
  identifies $\mathcal{O}(\text{cover piece})$ as a rational locale *over $\mathcal{O}(E)$*
  (rather than over $\mathcal{O}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f)$), via an iterated chain of
  localization identifications through the intermediate $C.\text{plusDatum}\,f$. Cost:
  new infrastructure — essentially formalize Wedhorn 2.13 at depth 2, with the chain
  $A \to \mathcal{O}(E) \to \mathcal{O}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f) \to \mathcal{O}(\text{cover piece})$
  and the natural intertwining identification.

## 8. Where we're stuck

### 8.1. The per-$E$ shape mismatch

The two sides of the per-$E$ assembly disagree on the *shape* of the cover pieces:

* **Assembly architecture** (built before the reframe) hard-codes `per_E_local_covering`
  whose pieces are iterated shapes $\text{laurent}\pm\text{Datum}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f, f_0)$.
* **New flatness route** (from the session-2 reframe) gives flatness for *direct* Laurent
  shapes of the base.

The cover piece is a Laurent shape of the *outer* $D_0$ via the iterated chain
$D_0 \to C.\text{plusDatum}\,f \to \text{cover piece}$. So flatness over
$\mathcal{O}(D_0)$ follows by composition (Theorem 5.4 / 5.5 applied twice). But the
assembly needs flatness over $\mathcal{O}(E)$, where $E$ is an intermediate rational
subset of $D_0$ that is *not* one of the levels of the iterated chain.

**Faithful descent doesn't apply.** Faithfully flat $R \to S$ lets us descend flatness
from $R$-flat to $S$-flat for $S$-modules, but $\mathcal{O}(D_0) \to \mathcal{O}(E)$ is
not faithfully flat in general (it covers only one piece of a cover, not the whole
spectrum).

**Wedhorn 2.13 at the iterated level doesn't yet exist in the formalization.** The
existing identification works for a *single* layer: $\mathcal{O}(\text{laurentMinusDatum}(D_0, f))$
as a rational object over $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$. We'd need a *twice-iterated* version for
$\mathcal{O}(\text{cover piece})$ as a rational object over $\mathcal{O}(E)$ via the
chain through $\mathcal{O}(C.\text{plusDatum}\,f)$.

### 8.2. The topological side of `IsSheafy`

The product topology on $\prod \mathcal{O}(D_i)$ should pull back to the topology on
$\mathcal{O}(D_0)$ along the product restriction, in the strongly noetherian Tate
setting. The mathematical content is Wedhorn Example 6.38 (the topology on
$\mathcal{O}(D)$ is the *quotient* topology from a Tate-algebra quotient $A\langle X \rangle / (1-sX)$)
combined with Lemma 8.33 (strictness of the Laurent Čech complex). The formalization is
unattempted; we built a hypothesis-parameterised wrapper (Theorem 5.9) so the user can
supply the topological piece when developed.
