# Review brief — Tate acyclicity (round 22)

*Prepared 2026-05-16 for ChatGPT Pro (continuing rounds 17–21).*
*Self-contained: no repo access required.*

## 1. Goal of this brief

Round 21 prescribed proving rational-open compactness for $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$
in the Tate setting **without** the mul-archimedean hypothesis $\mathrm{hArch}$,
via "the Boolean-cube argument: encode valuations by the relation
$a \preceq b \Leftrightarrow v(a) \le v(b)$, characterise the image by
finitary closed axioms in $\{0,1\}^{A\times A}$, and observe that rational
conditions are finite-coordinate clopen/closed conditions."

We attempted this and ran into a concrete obstruction: **continuity is not
finitary-closed-encodable in the Boolean product topology on $\{0,1\}^{A\times A}$**.
Wedhorn's actual proof of Theorem 7.35 (Spa quasi-compactness) doesn't use
the Boolean product directly — he uses a different spectral space
$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ defined via the characteristic-subgroup retraction
$c\Gamma_v(I)$. This brief asks for **specific Lean-level guidance** on
the right route given the project's existing infrastructure.

## 2. Background: what Wedhorn actually does

### 2.1. Definition (Wedhorn 7.3, equation 7.1.1)

For an ideal $I$ of $A$ with $\sqrt{I} = \sqrt{J}$ for some finitely
generated $J$:

$$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I) := \{\, v \in \mathrm{Spv}(A) \;;\; c\Gamma_v(I) = \Gamma_v \,\}$$

where $c\Gamma_v(I)$ is **defined as**:
- if $v(I) \cap c\Gamma_v \neq \emptyset$, then $c\Gamma_v(I) := c\Gamma_v$;
- otherwise, $c\Gamma_v(I) := $ the greatest convex subgroup $H$ of $\Gamma_v$
  such that $v(a)$ is cofinal for $H$ for all $a \in I$.

Here $c\Gamma_v$ is the *characteristic subgroup* of $\Gamma_v$ — the
convex subgroup defining the support-vs-non-support split.

### 2.2. Equivalent characterisation (Wedhorn Lemma 7.4)

$v \in \mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ iff $v(a)$ is cofinal for $\Gamma_v$ for all $a \in I$
**OR** $\Gamma_v = c\Gamma_v$.

### 2.3. Topology (Wedhorn Lemma 7.5 + Remark 7.6)

$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ carries a topology *refining* the subspace topology
inherited from $\mathrm{Spv}\,A$. The inclusion
$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{Spv}(A)$ is **not** a spectral
map in general. The refined topology has a basis of "rational subsets"
$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(T/s) := \{v : v(t) \le v(s) \neq 0\, \forall t \in T\}$
where $\sqrt{I} \subseteq \sqrt{T \cdot A}$ — i.e. $T$ must "generate $I$
up to radical".

### 2.4. Continuity characterisation (Wedhorn Theorem 7.10)

For $A$ $f$-adic with $I$ an ideal of definition:

$$\mathrm{Cont}(A) = \{\, v \in \mathrm{Spv}(A, I \cdot A) \;;\; v(a) < 1 \text{ for all } a \in I \,\}.$$

The proof shows that **once $v \in \mathrm{Spv}(A, I \cdot A)$**, the
finitary conditions $\{v(a) < 1\}_{a \in I}$ are equivalent to continuity:

> *(verbatim, Wedhorn p. 59).* "We claim that $v(a)$ is cofinal for
> $\Gamma_v$ for all $a \in I$. Indeed, if $c\Gamma_v \neq \Gamma_v$, then
> the claim follows from Lemma 7.4. Thus we may assume that
> $c\Gamma_v = \Gamma_v$. Let $a \in I$ and $\gamma \in \Gamma_v$ be given.
> By our assumptions there exists $t \in A$ with $v(t) \neq 0$ and
> $v(t)^{-1} \le \gamma$. Choose $n \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $ta^n \in I$
> (this exists because $\{t\}$ is bounded). Then $v(ta^n) < 1$ and hence
> $v(a)^n < \gamma$."

So the work is split: $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ handles the "non-microbial" case
($c\Gamma_v \neq \Gamma_v$) via the convex-subgroup retraction; the
boundedness argument handles the "microbial" case ($c\Gamma_v = \Gamma_v$).

### 2.5. Compactness (Wedhorn Theorem 7.35, p. 64)

> *Proof (verbatim).* "Let $I$ be the ideal of $A$ generated by $(A)^{oo}$.
> Then $\mathrm{Cont}(A)$ is a closed subspace of $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$.
> For all $a \in A^+$ the subset $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(\tfrac{a}{1})$ is an
> open constructible subset of $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ by Lemma 7.5. Thus
> $\mathrm{Spa}\,A = \mathrm{Cont}(A) \cap \bigcap_{a \in A^+}
> \mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(\tfrac{a}{1})$ is a pro-constructible subset of the
> spectral space $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$. In particular it is a spectral
> space."

No mul-archimedean hypothesis appears. The entire argument lives in
$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$, not in $\mathrm{Spv}(A)$ — and certainly not in the
Boolean product $\{0,1\}^{A \times A}$.

## 3. Why the Boolean-product encoding fails

### 3.1. The project's current setup

The project encodes $\mathrm{Spv}(A) \hookrightarrow \{0,1\}^{A \times A}$
via $\iota_{\mathrm{Bool}}\,v\,(a,b) := \mathrm{decide}(v(a) \le v(b))$.
It then proves rational-open compactness via an abstract criterion:

> *(project Theorem.)* If $S \subseteq \{0,1\}^{A \times A}$ is closed
> and $\iota_{\mathrm{Bool}}('' \,\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)) = \mathrm{range}\,
> \iota_{\mathrm{Bool}} \cap S$, then rational opens of $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$
> have compact preimage in the subtype.

For Tate rings, the project's concrete $S$ is:
$$S = \bigcap_{a \in A^+} \{r : r(a, 1) = \mathtt{true}\} \;\cap\; \{r : r(1, \pi) = \mathtt{false}\}.$$

(Encoding "$v \le 1$ on $A^+$" and "$v(\pi) < 1$".)

The image equality is proved using $\mathrm{hArch}$ in the **reverse
direction**, specifically at the step deriving $v$ continuous from the
boundary conditions via `Valuation.isContinuous_of_le_one_and_pow_cofinal`,
whose cofinality hypothesis $\forall \gamma > 0, \exists n, v(\pi)^n < \gamma$
is discharged via `exists_pow_lt_zero` (which requires
$\mathrm{MulArchimedean}$).

### 3.2. The fundamental obstruction

We tried to add a continuity-encoding condition to $S$ — replacing
$\mathrm{hArch}$ with a finitary description of cofinality. The natural
encoding

$$\forall b \in A,\ \exists N \in \mathbb{N},\ r(\pi^N, b) = \mathtt{true}$$

is an $F_\sigma$ condition in the Boolean product topology, **not closed**:
each "$\exists N$" is a countable union of clopens (hence open, but not
necessarily closed), and the universal over $b$ is an intersection of
opens (hence $G_\delta$).

So the closed-image criterion *cannot* directly encode Wedhorn 7.7
continuity for non-mul-archimedean valuations using only finitary conditions
on the Boolean cube.

### 3.3. What Wedhorn does instead

Wedhorn's $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ is a **different spectral space**, *not*
the subspace of $\mathrm{Spv}(A)$ in the inherited topology. Its
topology is *refined* (Remark 7.6 says the inclusion is not spectral).
In $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$:
- The rational subsets $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(T/s)$ are constructible (basic
  open compacts), and
- The condition $v(a) < 1$ for $a \in I$ becomes constructible-closed.

The whole compactness argument then runs *inside* $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$
using spectral-space machinery; the inclusion of $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$
in $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ as pro-constructible is the punchline.

## 4. What the project has and doesn't have

### 4.1. Has

- $\mathrm{Spv}(A)$ as a topological space (Sierpinski / Bool product).
- $\mathrm{CompactSpace}\,(\mathrm{Spv}\,A)$.
- Abstract closed-image criterion for $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$ compactness.
- Tate-case image description **with** $\mathrm{hArch}$.
- Discrete-case image description **without** $\mathrm{hArch}$ (continuity
  trivial for discrete topology).
- "$\mathrm{Valuation.isContinuous\_of\_le\_one\_and\_pow\_cofinal}$"
  — the bridge from boolean-style conditions to continuity, requiring
  cofinality.
- An auxiliary file `Lemma745.lean` invoking
  `restrictToConvex` (the project's name for Wedhorn's
  retraction $r$ from equation 7.1.2).

### 4.2. Doesn't have

- $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ as a distinct topological space with its own
  spectral structure.
- The characteristic subgroup $c\Gamma_v$ as a callable construction on
  $v$.
- The convex-subgroup retraction in topological / spectral form (the
  algebraic restriction `restrictToConvex` exists but is not packaged
  as a continuous retraction).
- A pro-constructible-subset framework specialised to $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$.
- $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ as a spectral space (the four-fold instance:
  $\mathrm{CompactSpace} + \mathrm{QuasiSober} + \mathrm{PrespectralSpace}
  + \mathrm{QuasiSeparatedSpace}$).

### 4.3. What Mathlib has

- `Topology.Spectral.ConstructibleTopology`: the type
  `WithConstructibleTopology X` plus the instance
  `compactSpace_withConstructibleTopology` requiring the four-fold
  spectral structure on $X$.
- `Topology.Spectral.Prespectral`: the `PrespectralSpace` class.
- `Topology.QuasiSeparated` and `Topology.Sober`: classes.

Mathlib has the *general* spectral-space machinery, but does not have
$\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ or anything specific to valuation spectra.

## 5. Where we're stuck

**Stuck point 5.1.** *Building $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ as a spectral space
inside the project, with the refined topology of Wedhorn 7.5.*

This needs:
- The convex-subgroup retraction $r : \mathrm{Spv}(A) \to \mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$
  packaged as continuous in the refined topology;
- Proof that $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ is a spectral space (the four-fold
  structure);
- The basis of "rational subsets" $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(T/s)$ as
  constructible-open;
- $\mathrm{Cont}(A) \cap \bigcap_{a \in A^+} \mathrm{Spv}(A, I)(a/1) =
  \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$ as pro-constructible.

This is order-of-magnitude *hundreds* of lines of Lean infrastructure,
faithfully translating Wedhorn §7.1–§7.2 + §7.4.

**Stuck point 5.2.** *Alternative: refactor the existing project closed-
image route to bypass the cofinality discharge.*

The existing `Valuation.isContinuous_of_le_one_and_pow_cofinal` is a
fine bridge in the rank-1 / mul-arch case but is exactly where
$\mathrm{hArch}$ enters. Replacing it with a "split-into-microbial-vs-
non-microbial" proof à la Wedhorn 7.10 requires either:
- packaging Wedhorn 7.10 directly (which itself uses $c\Gamma_v$ and
  `restrictToConvex`), or
- finding a continuity criterion for $f$-adic rings that works without
  the cofinality discharge.

**Stuck point 5.3.** *Acceptable engineering compromise.*

Round 21 ruled out propagating $\mathrm{hArch}$. We are open to a
**private/local** sub-lemma with $\mathrm{hArch}$ for the domination
lemma only, gated behind a TODO comment, while the spectral-space
infrastructure is built. The reviewer's round-21 reply explicitly
permits this: *"A legitimate temporary engineering compromise would be
a private local lemma named as a TODO, but not a public critical-path
theorem and not propagated to final statements."*

Is using this compromise route the right call for now?

## 6. Questions

**Q1 (the engineering question).** Given the project state (§4) and the
specifics of Wedhorn's framework (§2), is the right next-step:
- **(a)** Build $\mathrm{Spv}(A, I)$ as a spectral space infrastructure
  in the project (~hundreds of lines, faithful to Wedhorn §7.1–§7.4),
  then close compactness from there.
- **(b)** Use the round-21 "private TODO" compromise: add a local
  $\mathrm{hArch}$-assuming sub-lemma for the domination lemma only,
  gated behind a comment marking the spectral-infrastructure TODO,
  and continue Tate acyclicity. The eventual outer Tate-acyclicity
  statement does not propagate $\mathrm{hArch}$ because the inner sub-
  lemma is private.
- **(c)** Find a Lean-friendly direct continuity criterion for Tate
  rings that does *not* go through cofinality. If such exists in the
  literature, please point us at it.

If (a): can you sketch the order-of-construction (which Wedhorn lemma
to formalise first, what intermediate spectral-space lemmas to write)?

**Q2 (the microbial case in 7.10).** Wedhorn's reverse direction of
Theorem 7.10 has *two* cases: (i) $c\Gamma_v \neq \Gamma_v$ — easy by
Lemma 7.4; (ii) $c\Gamma_v = \Gamma_v$ — uses boundedness of singletons
and "$ta^n \in I$". In case (ii), what *exactly* is the value-group-
level cofinality argument? Is "$c\Gamma_v = \Gamma_v$" implying that
$v$'s value group is essentially mul-archimedean *up to the characteristic
subgroup*, and so the singleton-boundedness argument is essentially the
project's existing `exists_pow_lt_zero` route in disguise?

**Q3 (project-specific cofinality criterion).** The project has
`Valuation.isContinuous_of_ideal_pow_lt` — a continuity criterion that
asks "for every $\gamma > 0$, some power $I^n$ maps into
$\{a : v(a) < \gamma\}$". This is *not* `exists_pow_lt_zero`; it's
phrased directly on $I^n$. Can this be discharged for $v \in \mathrm{Spv}(A,
I \cdot A)$ without $\mathrm{hArch}$, using the algebraic statement of
boundedness in $A$ (rather than the value-group statement)?

**Q4 (alternative routes via mathlib).** Mathlib's
`compactSpace_withConstructibleTopology` requires CompactSpace,
QuasiSober, PrespectralSpace, QuasiSeparatedSpace on the underlying
space. The project has $\mathrm{CompactSpace}\,(\mathrm{Spv}\,A)$ but
not the other three. Is the right move to upstream
$\mathrm{QuasiSober}\,(\mathrm{Spv}\,A)$,
$\mathrm{PrespectralSpace}\,(\mathrm{Spv}\,A)$, and
$\mathrm{QuasiSeparatedSpace}\,(\mathrm{Spv}\,A)$ as mathlib
contributions first, then use the general
`compactSpace_withConstructibleTopology`? Or is the Spv-specific
spectral structure too project-bound for mathlib?
