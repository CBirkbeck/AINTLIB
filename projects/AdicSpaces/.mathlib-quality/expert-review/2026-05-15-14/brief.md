# Review brief — Tate acyclicity / IsSheafy (round 19: project audit + Wedhorn 2.13 shape question)

*Prepared 2026-05-15 for ChatGPT Pro (continuing series, follow-up to round 18). Self-contained: no repo access required.*

*Round 18 prescribed the use of a "general relative-rational-to-absolute-rational representation theorem" (Wedhorn 2.13 reverse direction) to construct `RatioNodeData` in P3. We then audited the project to see whether this theorem (or a workable specialisation) already exists from months of prior work. **Finding: the project contains Wedhorn 2.13 in three specific shapes — iterated-minus, iterated-overlap, and Laurent-normalized — but the shape required for P3 (a Laurent split at a relative element `r = u_g · u_h^{-1}` involving a completion-side inverse) is NOT covered by these specifics, and the general reverse direction is not packaged.** This brief reports what we have, what we need, and asks the reviewer whether (a) we should reformulate P3 to fit one of the existing shapes, (b) build a new specialisation for our shape, or (c) build the truly general theorem.*

## 1. Goal

Formalize Wedhorn's *Adic Spaces* Theorem 8.28(b) (Tate-acyclicity / `IsSheafy` upgrade) in Lean 4 / Mathlib. Specifically, complete the existence theorem for a refining Laurent tree (Wedhorn 8.34) needed by the topological-inducing route.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

- $A$ is a Tate Huber ring with $\mathrm{A}^+ \subseteq A$ a plus subring.
- For a rational localization datum $D = (P, T, s)$ on $A$, $\mathcal{O}(D) = \mathrm{presheafValue}(D)$ is the completion of $A[s^{-1}]$ in the locTopology.
- $D.\mathrm{canonicalMap} : A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ factors as $D.\mathrm{coeRingHom} \circ \mathrm{algebraMap}$.
- $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(T, s)$ is the corresponding rational subset of $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$.
- A `RationalLocData A` is the tuple $(P, T, s, h_{\mathrm{open}})$ with $h_{\mathrm{open}}$ the technical openness condition.

### 2.2. References

- [Wedhorn 2019] T. Wedhorn. *Adic Spaces*. arXiv:1910.05934. Lemma 2.13 (stability/transitivity of rational localizations); Lemma 7.45 (valuation extension); Lemma 7.50 (adic Nullstellensatz); Proposition 8.2 ($s$ a unit on subsets); Theorem 8.28(b); Lemma 8.34 (refinement tree).
- [Huber 1993] R. Huber. *Étale Cohomology of Rigid Analytic Varieties and Adic Spaces*. Aspects of Mathematics 30, Vieweg 1996.

### 2.3. State of the art

The project has been developed over ~12 months, ~250 Lean files. Group I (topological inducing), Group II (algebraic separation/gluing), Group III (transitivity bridge), Group IV (Spa-point existence) all have substantive sorry-free infrastructure. The Wedhorn 8.34 existence theorem is the final remaining Group I residual.

## 3. Strategy

The reviewer-approved architecture (rounds 16-18) reduces Wedhorn 8.34 to:

1. **W1** (Hübner Nullstellensatz). Standard cover $S \subseteq A$ refining $C$.
2. **W2** (Cor 7.32). From $S$: unit $\sigma \in A^\times$, outer Laurent tree, per-leaf $C|_L$ generated by units in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.
3. **W3** (Wedhorn Step iii, relative). Relative Laurent tree at pairwise ratios $u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$ over $\mathcal{O}(L)$.
4. **W3-transport**. Descend relative tree to absolute `RatioLaurentTree A`.
5. **I.1**. Graft outer tree + per-leaf inner ratio trees.

The descent step 4 is where the structural obstruction sits — the per-`nodeRatio` construction (P3) needs to package each ratio split as `RatioNodeData L g h` with literal `plus_open_eq` and `minus_open_eq` equalities.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 (`RatioNodeData L f g`).** Same as round-18 brief §4.1. Fields: `plus`, `minus : RationalLocData A`; `plus_subset`, `minus_subset` (set inclusions); `cover_proof` (existential cover); `plus_open_eq`: $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(\mathrm{plus}) = \{v \in \mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \mid v(f) \le v(g)\}$ literal set equality; `minus_open_eq` symmetric.

**Definition 4.2 (`relativeRationalLocData_laurentNormalized` — project's existing API).** Given absolute $E, D : \mathrm{RationalLocData}\,A$ with $D$ Laurent-normalized and $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(E)$, the relative datum

$$D\,@\,E := (P_E^{\mathrm{concrete}},\ D.T \text{ imaged via } E.\mathrm{canonicalMap},\ E.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(D.s),\ h_{\mathrm{open}}^{\mathrm{rel}})$$

is a `RationalLocData (presheafValue E)`, with $P_E^{\mathrm{concrete}}$ the concrete pair of definition on $\mathcal{O}(E)$.

**Definition 4.3 (`presheafValue_relative_equiv` — project's Group III, closed).** Under the same setup, there is a topological ring isomorphism

$$\mathcal{O}(D) \simeq_{\mathrm{top}} \mathcal{O}(D\,@\,E).$$

**Definition 4.4 (relative unit generator at $f$).** Given $L, C, f, h_{\mathrm{base}} : \mathrm{IsUnit}(L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s))$:

$$u_f := L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(f) \cdot h_{\mathrm{base}}.\mathrm{unit}^{-1} \in \mathcal{O}(L).$$

For $f \in I_{\mathrm{units}}$, $u_f$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.

## 5. Project audit — what's there

We audited the ~250 Lean files for Wedhorn-2.13-related infrastructure. **Three specific shapes are proved sorry-free:**

**Shape A: Iterated-minus (Wedhorn 2.13 instance).** For absolute $D_0, f \in A$:

$$\mathcal{O}(\mathrm{laurentOverlapDatum}\ D_0\ f) \simeq_{\mathrm{ring}} \mathcal{O}(\mathrm{iteratedMinusDatum}_B\ P\ D_0\ f)$$

where $\mathrm{laurentOverlapDatum}\ D_0\ f = \mathrm{laurentMinusDatum}(\mathrm{laurentPlusDatum}\ D_0\ f)\ f$ (the absolute datum representing the "overlap" $v(f) = v(D_0.s)$), and $\mathrm{iteratedMinusDatum}_B$ is its relative-over-$\mathcal{O}(D_0)$ counterpart with relative $s = D_0.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(f)$, $T = \{1\}$. Proven; topological isomorphism upgraded; presheaf-value equivalence sorry-free.

**Shape B: Iterated-overlap (Wedhorn 2.13 instance).** A similar equivalence relating $\mathrm{laurentOverlapDatum}\ D_0\ f$ to a B-side overlap datum $\mathrm{iteratedOverlapDatum}_B\ P\ D_0\ f$ over $\mathcal{O}(D_0)$. Proven; topological isomorphism upgraded; presheaf-value equivalence sorry-free.

**Shape C: Laurent-normalized (Wedhorn 2.13 instance).** For absolute $D_0, D$ with $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D) \subseteq \mathrm{rationalOpen}(D_0)$ and $D$ Laurent-normalized:

$$\mathcal{O}(D) \simeq_{\mathrm{top}} \mathcal{O}(D\,@\,D_0).$$

This is `presheafValue_relative_equiv` (Group III's main result). The relative datum is constructed from absolute $D$ by imaging through $D_0.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$; the relative $s = D_0.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(D.s)$ and $T = D.T.\mathrm{image}\ D_0.\mathrm{canonicalMap}$.

**Common feature of A, B, C.** Each provides an equivalence between $\mathcal{O}(D_{\mathrm{abs}})$ and $\mathcal{O}(D_{\mathrm{rel}})$ for **specific shapes of the absolute datum**. In each case, the relative datum is constructed from an absolute datum (forward direction), and the equivalence is then proved.

## 8. Where we're stuck (round-19 specific)

### **Stuck point 8.1.** *The required `exists_absolute_rationalLocData_of_relative` is not directly covered by the project's existing Wedhorn 2.13 instances.*

We have shapes A, B, C in §5. Our P3 use case is: the relative datum is $\mathrm{plus}_{\mathrm{rel}} = (\cdot, \{r\}, 1, h_{\mathrm{open}}^{\mathrm{rel}})$ where $r = u_g \cdot u_h^{-1} \in \mathcal{O}(L)$. The relative $T$ contains $r$, which is **not** in the image of $L.\mathrm{canonicalMap}(A)$ (because the inverse $u_h^{-1}$ lives in the completion). So:

- **Shape A** (iterated-minus): the absolute counterpart is $\mathrm{laurentOverlapDatum}\ D_0\ f$ for some absolute $D_0$ and $f \in A$. Its rationalOpen is $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(D_0) \cap \{v(f) = v(D_0.s)\}$, the "overlap" set. **This is NOT the rationalOpen we want** ($\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \cap \{v(g) \le v(h)\}$ is a "half-space", not an "overlap").
- **Shape B** (iterated-overlap): same rationalOpen as A. Doesn't fit.
- **Shape C** (Laurent-normalized): requires the absolute datum $D$ to exist as input. We don't have one — we're trying to construct it.

None of A, B, C directly applies. The required reverse-direction theorem (build absolute datum from arbitrary relative datum) is **not packaged**.

### **Stuck point 8.2.** *The same situation applies to the bridge lemma (Wedhorn 7.49 Spa equivalence reverse direction).*

The project has:
- Spv (presheafValue D) → rationalOpen via comap (multiple lemmas).
- Spa points on presheafValue (via Lemma 7.45 — `exists_spa_point_supp_ge_in_presheafValue`).

The project does NOT have:
- rationalOpen → Spv (presheafValue D) lift (the reverse direction).

The bridge lemma's round-17 reframed proof depends on this lift. Currently sorry-stubbed.

## 9. Open mathematical questions for the reviewer (round 19)

**Q1 (concrete: which shape fits, or none).** Of the three project-existing Wedhorn 2.13 instances (A = iterated-minus, B = iterated-overlap, C = Laurent-normalized) listed in §5, do any of them — possibly after a clever reformulation of the P3 relative datum — fit the construction we need? Specifically:

- Can we **avoid** building a relative Laurent split at $r = u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$ directly, and instead express the P3 ratio piece via an iterated-minus or iterated-overlap construction at an absolute $f \in A$ chosen so that the rationalOpen comes out as $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \cap \{v(g) \le v(h)\}$?
- Wedhorn's Step (iii) constructs Laurent splits at $u_i \cdot u_j^{-1}$ inside $\mathcal{O}(V_j)$. Is the reformulation we need essentially: "interpret the ratio split as an iterated-minus / iterated-overlap of two absolute Laurent splits"?

**Q2 (if no existing shape fits — generality decision).** If Q1's answer is "no existing shape fits", do we:

(a) Build a new specific Wedhorn 2.13 instance tailored to the relative Laurent at $r = u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$? This would be a fourth shape, smaller in scope than the general theorem, but specifically engineered for the P3 callsite.

(b) Build the truly general `exists_absolute_rationalLocData_of_relative` (Wedhorn 2.13 reverse direction for arbitrary relative datum)? This is more reusable but a bigger commitment.

(c) Some other route the reviewer prefers?

**Q3 (Wedhorn-side recipe).** In Wedhorn's text, Step (iii) at the leaf $V_j$ produces a refinement by Laurent splits. The descent back to $\mathrm{Spa}(A)$ — does Wedhorn (or any other source) write down an **explicit absolute rational datum** in $A$ that captures the per-piece rational subset $\mathrm{rationalOpen}(L) \cap \{v(g) \le v(h)\}$, with the pair of definition and hopen? Or does he leave this as "by the stability of rational localization" without producing the explicit datum? If the latter — that's the gap we're stuck on.

**Q4 (Spa equivalence reverse direction).** The same question for Wedhorn 7.49: the project has the forward direction; the reverse direction (Spv on completion from rationalOpen valuation) isn't packaged. Does Wedhorn write this down explicitly, or rely on a non-explicit existence claim?

**Q5 (effort estimate).** Given the project has spent ~12 months on adjacent infrastructure (Groups II/III/IV closed; ~250 files; ~12k lines), is building the missing reverse-direction theorems (one for Wedhorn 2.13, one for Wedhorn 7.49) a focused effort within the project's existing apparatus, or does it need substantial new mathlib-level infrastructure (e.g., continuous valuation extension to commutative-ring completions, currently absent from mathlib)?
