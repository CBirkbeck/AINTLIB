## 1. Goal

We are formalizing **Wedhorn, *Adic Spaces*, Theorem 8.28(b)**: *if `A` is a strongly
noetherian Tate ring and `A⁺ ⊆ A` a ring of integral elements, then the structure presheaf
`𝒪_X` on `X = Spa(A, A⁺)` is a sheaf of complete topological rings.*

Wedhorn's proof has two halves. The **embedding (injectivity) half** — the product
restriction `𝒪_X(U) → ∏ᵢ 𝒪_X(Uᵢ)` is a topological embedding — is essentially done in our
development (via faithful flatness, Cor 8.32). This brief is about the **gluing half**: the
`𝒪_X`-acyclicity of rational covers, which Wedhorn proves on pp. 83–84 via Lemma 7.54
(cover refinement) + Lemma 8.34 (acyclicity of `T`-generated covers) + Proposition A.3
(acyclicity descends along refinements).

The session that produced this brief made substantial progress on Lemma 7.54 but bottomed
out at **two pieces of missing infrastructure** and surfaced **a cluster of mis-stated
lemmas** in the surrounding chain. We want the reviewer's guidance on the cleanest route
past these, and confirmation that our re-architecting is correct.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting and notation

Throughout, `A` is a **Tate ring**: a complete topological ring whose topology is given by
a *ring of definition* `A₀` (an open, bounded subring) with an *ideal of definition* `I`
(so `{Iⁿ}` is a basis of neighbourhoods of `0` in `A₀`), and which contains a
**topologically nilpotent unit** `ϖ` (a unit with `ϖⁿ → 0`). We write:

- `A°` = the **power-bounded** subring (elements `a` with `{aⁿ}` bounded);
- `A°°` = the set of **topologically nilpotent** elements (`aⁿ → 0`);
- `A⁺` = a **ring of integral elements**: an open, integrally closed subring with
  `A°° ⊆ A⁺ ⊆ A°`. The pair `(A, A⁺)` is an *affinoid ring*.

`Spv(A)` is the valuation spectrum (equivalence classes of valuations `v : A → Γ ∪ {0}`,
written multiplicatively; we write `v(a) ≤ v(b)` for the binary relation, and `v(a) ≠ 0`
for "`a` not in the support"). `Cont(A) ⊆ Spv(A)` is the subset of *continuous* valuations;
`(Cont A)ᵃ` (resp. `(Spa A)ᵃ`) the **analytic** points (those whose support is not open,
equivalently `v(a) ≠ 0` for some `a ∈ A°°`). For an affinoid ring,

`Spa(A, A⁺) = { v ∈ Cont(A) : v(a) ≤ 1 for all a ∈ A⁺ }`.

For a finite `T ⊆ A` and `s ∈ A`, the **rational subset** is

`R(T/s) = { v ∈ Spa(A, A⁺) : v(t) ≤ v(s) for all t ∈ T, and v(s) ≠ 0 }`.

We call a rational cover **generated by `T`** (Wedhorn, p. 83) when it is `(R(T/t))_{t ∈ T}`
with `T · A = A` (i.e. `T` generates the unit ideal). The **structure presheaf** assigns to
`R(T/s)` the completion `𝒪_X(R(T/s)) = A⟨T/s⟩` of the localization `A[1/s]` for the natural
topology (Wedhorn §7.5 / Example 6.38); restriction maps come from the universal property of
these completions.

Two topological-algebra notions are central to the blockers and must be distinguished:

- **Linear topology** (`IsLinearTopology A A` in Mathlib): the *open ideals* form a
  neighbourhood basis of `0`.
- **Non-archimedean (additive) group** (`NonarchimedeanAddGroup A`, Wedhorn Def. 5.23): the
  *open subgroups* form a neighbourhood basis of `0`.

For a Huber/Tate ring the second always holds; the first essentially never does (§8.1).

## 3. Strategy

Wedhorn's proof of 8.28(b), gluing half (p. 83, skeleton):

1. **Lemma 7.54.** Every open cover of `X = Spa A` admits a refinement `(R(T/t))_{t∈T}`
   with `T ⊆ A` generating the unit ideal.
2. **Lemma 8.34.** Such a `T`-generated cover is `𝒪_X`-acyclic, in four steps:
   (i) a *Laurent* cover (iterated `{R(f/1), R(1/f)}` splits) is acyclic, via Lemma 8.33 and
   Prop A.3(3); (ii) the `T`-generated cover is refined by a Laurent cover whose restriction
   to each `R(T/t)` is generated by *units* — this uses Cor 7.32 (the dominating unit);
   (iii)–(iv) combine via Prop A.3(1)+(2).
3. **Prop A.3(2)** transfers acyclicity from a refinement back to the original cover; the
   sheaf condition for every rational cover then gives (Rmk 8.20) that `𝒪_X` is a sheaf.

## 4. Definitions the reviewer needs

**Definition 4.1 (rational cover; cover generated by `T`).** As in §2.1. A rational cover of
`U = R(T₀/s₀)` is a finite family `R(Tᵢ/sᵢ) ⊆ U` with union `U`; *generated by `T`* means it
is `(R(T/t))_{t∈T}` with `Ideal.span T = A`.

**Definition 4.2 (`𝒪_X`-acyclic).** A rational cover `𝒞` of `U` is *`𝒪_X`-acyclic* if:
*(separation)* a section `x ∈ 𝒪_X(U)` restricting to `0` on every piece is `0`; and
*(gluing)* every compatible family `(x_i)` (the `x_i, x_j` agree on `𝒪_X(W)` for every
rational `W ⊆ U_i ∩ U_j`) comes from a (unique) `x ∈ 𝒪_X(U)`. (The degree-`≤0` sheaf
condition.)

**Definition 4.3 (Laurent cover).** For `f₁,…,f_r`, the `2^r`-fold cover with pieces
`⋂ᵢ R(fᵢ^{εᵢ}/1)`, `ε ∈ {±}^r`. Its restriction to `V ⊆ U` is the Laurent cover of `V` by
the images `fᵢ|V` (Wedhorn 8.34(i)).

**Definition 4.4 (the two topological notions).** `A` has a *linear topology* if open ideals
form a basis of `𝒩(0)`; `A` is a *non-archimedean additive group* if open subgroups do. For
a Tate ring the latter holds (a ring of definition with `{Iⁿ}` gives open subgroups), the
former fails (§8.1).

## 5. Established results (this development; combinatorial core of Lemma 7.54)

Formalized **axiom-clean** (only `propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`): the whole
combinatorial/algebraic core of the **whole-space** Lemma 7.54, via Huber's product trick.
Let `X = Spa A`. Given a finite rational cover `𝒱 = (R(Tᵢ/sᵢ))ᵢ` of `X` that is
**normalised** — each `1 ∈ Tᵢ` and `sᵢ ∈ Tᵢ` (so each `Tᵢ` generates the unit ideal and
`R(Tᵢ/sᵢ)` is cut out by `v(t) ≤ v(sᵢ)`) — define

> `P = { ∏ᵢ tᵢ : tᵢ ∈ Tᵢ }` (transversal products), and
> `S = { ∏ᵢ tᵢ : tᵢ ∈ Tᵢ, with tᵢ = sᵢ for at least one i }` (distinguished products).

**Theorem 5.1 (product identity, = [Hu3] 2.6 Step 3).** For a transversal `(tᵢ)`,
`R(P / ∏tᵢ) = X ∩ ⋂ᵢ R(Tᵢ/tᵢ)`. *Sketch.* Induction via `R(T·Q / s·q) = R(T/s) ∩ R(Q/q)`. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (covering half of Cor 7.53).** If `Ideal.span T = A` then every `v ∈ X` lies
in `R(T/t)` for some `t ∈ T`. *Sketch.* A `v`-maximal `t`; if all `v(t)=0` then
`T ⊆ supp(v)`, so `span T = A ⊆ supp(v)`, contradicting properness of the prime `supp(v)`.
Elementary; no completeness needed. ∎

**Theorem 5.3 (the distinguished cover covers `X`, = Step 4).** With `𝒱` normalised,
`(R(P/s))_{s∈S}` covers `X`. *Sketch.* For `v`: the `Wᵢ = R(Tᵢ/sᵢ)` cover `X` so
`v ∈ W_{i₀}`; on the other factors take `v`-maximal `tᵢ` (Thm 5.2); set `t_{i₀}=s_{i₀}`;
then `∏tᵢ ∈ S` and `v ∈ R(P/∏tᵢ)` by Thm 5.1. ∎

**Theorem 5.4 (`R(P/s) = R(S/s)` for `s ∈ S`, = Step 6).** `⊆` antitone in numerator
(`S ⊆ P`); `⊇` via Thm 5.3 and `v(s') ≤ v(s)`. ∎

**Theorem 5.5 (`S` generates the unit ideal, = Step 5).** `Ideal.span S = A`. *Sketch.* By
Thm 5.3 the `(R(P/s))_{s∈S}` cover `X`, so `S` has no common zero; Cor 7.53's Nullstellensatz
direction (already formalized, using a complete pair of definition and `A⁺ ⊆ A₀`) gives
`span S = A`. ∎

**Theorem 5.6 (refinement direction).** For `s ∈ S`, `R(P/s) ⊆ R(T_i/s_i)` for an `i` with
distinguished factor `t_i = s_i`; hence `R(S/s) ⊆ W_i ⊆` some original piece. *Sketch.*
Induction on the recursive description of `S`, using `R(X·Q / c·e) ⊆ R(X/c)` (a common
factor `e ≠ 0` cancels). ∎

**Theorem 5.7 (whole-space Lemma 7.54, assembled).** Given a normalised rational cover `𝒱`
of `X`, there is a finite `S` with `Ideal.span S = A` such that `(R(S/f))_{f∈S}` covers `X`
and each `R(S/f)` lies in some piece of `𝒱`. **No integral-domain, no height-1 hypothesis.**
The complete pair Thm 5.5 needs is supplied from the ambient hypotheses (a strongly
noetherian Tate ring with compatible `A⁺` and complete `A` has an adically complete
principal pair — also formalized). ∎

The **only** remaining input to Thm 5.7 is the normalization producing `𝒱` from an arbitrary
open cover — §8.1–§8.2.

## 8. Where we're stuck

### 8.1. (Q1) `A°°` is an open ideal of a Tate ring — but Mathlib gates this on `IsLinearTopology`, unsatisfiable for Tate rings

Wedhorn Prop 6.13(1): in a Tate ring `A`, the set `A°°` of topologically nilpotent elements
is an **open ideal**. We need this (and Cor 7.31/7.32, which use that `A°°` is open) to
construct the *dominating units* underpinning the normalization (§8.2) and Lemma 8.34(ii).

The obstruction is a **typeclass mismatch in Mathlib**. Mathlib's `topologicalNilradical R`
— the ideal of topologically nilpotent elements — is defined **only** under
`[IsLinearTopology R R]`; concretely the additive closure "`a, b` top. nilpotent ⟹ `a + b`
top. nilpotent" (`IsTopologicallyNilpotent.add_of_commute`) is proved only under that
hypothesis, and there is **no** `NonarchimedeanAddGroup`-based version in Mathlib.

But `IsLinearTopology A A` is **unsatisfiable for any nontrivial Tate ring**: a Tate ring
has a topologically nilpotent *unit* `ϖ`, so any open ideal contains `ϖⁿ` for large `n` (as
`ϖⁿ → 0`), and `ϖⁿ` is a unit, forcing the open ideal to be `A`. So the only open ideal is
`A`, hence open ideals are *not* a neighbourhood basis of `0` and `IsLinearTopology A A`
fails. (We confirmed empirically that every lemma in our 7.31/7.32 chain that "looked like"
it used `IsLinearTopology` cosmetically in fact uses it genuinely-transitively, bottoming out
exactly at Mathlib's `topologicalNilradical`-as-ideal, which cannot be `omit`-ted.)

The faithful notion is `NonarchimedeanAddGroup` (open *subgroups* basis), Wedhorn Def. 5.23,
which **does** hold for Tate rings — our development already has "Huber ring ⟹
`NonarchimedeanAddGroup`", and the 8.28(b) hypothesis bundle supplies `NonarchimedeanRing A`.
The additive closure of `A°°` is true non-archimedeanly (if `U` is an open subgroup with
`aⁿ, bⁿ` eventually in `U`, then `(a+b)ⁿ` eventually lands in `U`).

**What we tried.** We attempted to `omit` `IsLinearTopology` from the 7.31/7.32 chain; it
fails at `A°°`-open because that lemma genuinely consumes the Mathlib `topologicalNilradical`
ideal structure. We reverted to keep the build green.

Precisely: we need "`A°°` is an (open) ideal" / "top. nilpotents closed under addition" under
`[NonarchimedeanAddGroup A]` (or `[NonarchimedeanRing A]`), not `[IsLinearTopology A A]`.

### 8.2. (Q1, cont.) Downstream: Cor 7.31/7.32 and the normalization

Wedhorn 7.31 / Cor 7.32 (no-archimedean form): for quasi-compact `Y ⊆ Spa A` and `s`
non-vanishing on `Y`, there is a *unit* `π ∈ A^×` with `v(π) < v(s)` on `Y`. Our proof is
otherwise complete and needs **no** height hypothesis — only the topologically nilpotent
unit, `A°°` open, and quasi-compactness. But because `A°°`-open is gated on `IsLinearTopology`
(§8.1), the whole no-hArch dominating-unit lemma is currently *vacuous* for Tate rings.

The per-point normalization for Lemma 7.54 — write `R(T/s) ∋ v` with a dominating unit `π`
as `R(T'/s')`, `s' = s·π⁻¹`, `T' = {1, s'} ∪ π⁻¹·T`, so `1 ∈ T'`, `sᵢ ∈ Tᵢ`,
`v ∈ R(T'/s') ⊆ R(T/s)` — is written and correct *modulo* this dominating unit. So §8.1's
fix directly unblocks the normalization.

### 8.3. (Q3, Q4) Mis-stated lemmas in the surrounding chain — re-architecture

Two statements in our decomposition turned out **false as stated**, both imposing a uniform
height-1 / global-span condition the geometry does not support.

**(a) Whole-space vs. general-base 7.54.** Our `every_rational_cover_is_OXAcyclic` is stated
for a cover of an *arbitrary* rational subset `U = R(T₀/s₀)`, routed through a "form-(a)
refinement at base `U`" lemma whose conclusion demands `Ideal.span S = A` (globally) *and*
every `R(S/f) ⊆ U`. This is **false whenever `U ⊊ X`**: if `span S = A` then (Thm 5.2) the
`(R(S/f))_f` cover all of `X`, contradicting `R(S/f) ⊆ U ⊊ X`. So 7.54 with a global
unit-ideal condition is correct only at the whole space `X`. Wedhorn applies 7.54 to
`X = Spa A` and propagates to rational subsets via Prop A.3 (and via `U = Spa 𝒪_X(U)`, again
the spectrum of a strongly noetherian Tate ring). We believe the correct architecture is
**whole-space 7.54 (Thm 5.7) + relative 7.54 over `𝒪_X(U)`**, with
`every_rational_cover_is_OXAcyclic` re-routed through the relative version. Confirm? (Q3)

**(b) Dominating unit, height-1 vs. no-hArch.** A lemma asserts that *every* continuous
valuation on a strongly noetherian Tate ring has a **multiplicatively-archimedean** (height
≤ 1) value group, cited "Wedhorn 7.40(6)". On re-reading: this is **false**. Wedhorn
**Remark** 7.40 says (5) every analytic point has height **≥ 1**, and (6) a point has height
**= 1 iff it is a maximal point** of `(Spa A)ᵃ`. Non-maximal analytic points have height
`> 1`. This false lemma fed the *height-1* form of Cor 7.32. But the **no-archimedean** form
(§8.2) needs no height assumption, so the height-1 lemma is both false and unnecessary, and
the faithful route is the no-hArch dominating unit (once §8.1 is fixed). A neighbouring lemma
asserts a principal pair with `A₀ ⊆ A⁺`; we suspect this is the wrong direction (Rmk 7.17
gives `A⁺ ⊆ A₀`) and is likewise unnecessary on the no-hArch route. Confirm? (Q4)

### 8.4. The Prop A.3(3) gluing cocycle (context, not a question)

Lemma 8.34(i) reduces, via Prop A.3(3) ("an acyclic cover whose pieces are themselves
acyclically covered is acyclic"), to gluing. Given `V = 𝒰_f × (⊔_Q 𝒱_Q)` with `𝒰_f` a
Laurent 2-cover (acyclic) and each `𝒱_Q` acyclic, we glue a compatible family on `V` by
gluing per `𝒰_f`-piece `Q` (each restricted subfamily is compatible, via `𝒱_Q`-gluing) to a
section `g_Q`, then gluing the `g_Q` via `𝒰_f`-gluing. Separation is formalized; the gluing
half's genuine content is the cocycle "the `g_Q` agree on a common refinement", reducing to
separation on that refinement after covering it by the `V`-pieces — ordinary
double-complex bookkeeping, not a math gap (we hit dependent-type-transport friction in Lean,
not missing mathematics). Included so the reviewer sees the full route.

## 9. Questions for the reviewer

**Q1 (central blocker). `A°°` as an open ideal without linear topology.** What is the
cleanest way to obtain "the topologically nilpotent elements of a Tate (or Huber) ring form
an open ideal" — equivalently "`a, b` top. nilpotent ⟹ `a + b` top. nilpotent" — under a
*non-archimedean group* hypothesis (open subgroups basis), given that Mathlib proves it only
under `IsLinearTopology` (open *ideals* basis), which is unsatisfiable for Tate rings?
Specifically:
  (i) Is there existing Mathlib API for the additive closure of topologically nilpotent
      elements under `NonarchimedeanAddGroup`/`NonarchimedeanRing` we have missed?
  (ii) If not, is the right move a small project-local development (prove `a+b` top. nilpotent
       from the open-subgroup basis, then assemble `A°°` as an open ideal à la Wedhorn
       6.13(1)), or worth upstreaming (relax `topologicalNilradical`'s hypothesis from
       `IsLinearTopology` to `NonarchimedeanAddGroup`)? Any pitfall — e.g. does the *ideal*
       (not just additive subgroup) structure of `A°°` need more than the additive
       non-archimedean axiom (the `r·a` absorption for `r ∈ A`)?

**Q2. No-archimedean quasi-compactness of `Spa A`.** We need `Spa(A,A⁺)` (and preimages of
rational subsets in it) **quasi-compact without** a height-1/mul-archimedean hypothesis
(Wedhorn Thm 7.35(2)). We have a route via the spectral structure of the continuous-valuation
spectrum (a closed-image-in-`{0,1}^{A×A}` Bool-cylinder criterion), but it is parked behind
one hard sub-lemma (closedness of the image of `Spa A` under that embedding, no-hArch). Is
there a cleaner formalization route — e.g. directly via the `{R(T/t)}_{t∈T}` cover for `T`
generating an ideal of definition (Wedhorn's own one-line argument in the proof of
Rmk 7.40(2): `(Spa A)ᵃ = ⋃_{t∈T} R(T/t)` for `T ⊆ A°°` with `T·A` open)? What is the
least-infrastructure path you would take?

**Q3. Whole-space vs. relative Lemma 7.54.** Do you confirm that 7.54 (refinement to a cover
*generated by a unit-ideal generating set `T`*) is genuinely a **whole-space** statement
(`X = Spa A`), and that covers of a proper rational subset `U` must be handled by applying
7.54 to the strongly noetherian Tate ring `𝒪_X(U)` (with `U = Spa 𝒪_X(U)`) and descending via
Prop A.3 — rather than by any "global unit-ideal refinement at base `U`"? Equivalently: is
the global-span condition `T·A = A` incompatible with `U ⊊ X` (as §8.3(a) shows), and is
"relative 7.54 over `𝒪_X(U)` + Prop A.3" the standard factoring?

**Q4. The dominating-unit route: height-1 is false and unnecessary.** Do you confirm that
(a) "every continuous valuation on a strongly noetherian Tate ring has height ≤ 1" is false
(only *maximal* analytic points have height 1, Rmk 7.40(5)–(6)), and (b) the dominating unit
of Cor 7.32 — used in Lemma 8.34(ii) — needs **no** height/mul-archimedean hypothesis (only
the topologically nilpotent unit, `A°°` open, quasi-compactness), so the height-1 lemma can
be deleted and Cor 7.32 routed through its no-archimedean form? Secondarily: is the relevant
inclusion `A⁺ ⊆ A₀` (Rmk 7.17), making any lemma demanding `A₀ ⊆ A⁺` the wrong direction and
also unnecessary on this route?
