## 2. Background, notation, and references

### 2.1. Setting (recap)

- $A$ is a complete topological commutative ring, **Tate** (admits a topologically nilpotent unit), **strongly noetherian** (Wedhorn 6.36: $A\langle X_1,\dots,X_n\rangle$ noetherian for all $n$), Hausdorff, nonarchimedean. $A^{+} \subseteq A$ is open, integrally closed, of power-bounded elements.
- $\mathrm{Spv}(A)$ is the valuation spectrum. $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^{+}) \subset \mathrm{Spv}(A)$ is the locus of continuous valuations $v$ with $v(a) \le 1$ for all $a \in A^{+}$.
- A **rational subset** is $R(T/s) = \{v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^{+}) : v(t) \le v(s) \ne 0,\ \forall t \in T\}$, for $T \subset A$ finite and $s \in A$ with $(T,s)$ generating an open ideal.
- The structure presheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ assigns to $R(T/s)$ the completion of $A_s$ in the *rational topology* (generated by neighbourhoods $I^n/s^N$).
- A **rational covering** of a base datum $D_0 = R(T_0/s_0)$ is a finite family $C = (R(T_i/s_i))_{i \in I}$ of rational subsets of $D_0$ whose union covers $D_0$. We refer to "C-pieces" for elements of $I$.
- For $f \in A$, the **Laurent cover at $f$ within base $D_0$**, $U_f|_{D_0}$, is the 2-element rational covering with pieces:
  - **plus piece** $\mathrm{laurentPlus}(D_0, f) := R(D_0.T \cup \{f\} / D_0.s)$, where $v(f) \le v(D_0.s)$ holds;
  - **minus piece** $\mathrm{laurentMinus}(D_0, f) := R((D_0.T \cup \{D_0.s\}) \cdot \{D_0.s, f\} / D_0.s \cdot f)$, where $v(D_0.s) \le v(f)$ holds.
- A **standard cover** of $D_0$ generated by $T = (f_0, \dots, f_n) \subseteq A$ is the rational cover whose $i$-th piece is $R(\text{insert}(f_i, D_0.T)/D_0.s)$. The standard cover refines $C$ if it does so piece-by-piece, and "spans" if $(T) \cdot A = A$ (Wedhorn's hypothesis).

### 2.3. Wedhorn 8.34 (verbatim quote of the textbook statement and proof, for ground truth)

> **Lemma 8.34.** *Let $A$ be a complete strongly noetherian Tate ring and $U$ be a rational cover generated by some finite subset $T \subseteq A$ with $T \cdot A = A$. Then $U$ is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic.*
>
> *Proof.* **(i)** For $f \in A$ let $U_f$ be the open covering of $X = \mathrm{Spa}\,A$ consisting of $R(f/1)$ and $R(1/f)$. Then $U_f$ is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic by Lemma 8.33. Moreover, if $U = R(T/s)$ is any rational subset then $U_f \mid U = U_{f \mid U}$, where $f \mid U$ is the image of $f$ under the homomorphism $A \to A(T/s)$. Thus $U_f \mid U$ is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic. Using Proposition A.3 (3) it follows by induction that all open covers of the form $V := U_{f_1} \times \cdots \times U_{f_r}$ are $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic. Such a cover is called a *Laurent cover* generated by $f_1, \dots, f_r$. It is the rational cover generated by $T = \{\prod_{j \in J} f_j ;\ J \subseteq \{1,\dots,r\}\}$.
>
> **(ii)** We show the following claim. Let $T = (f_0, \dots, f_n) \subseteq A$ be finite such that $T$ generates $A$ as ideal and let $U$ be the rational cover of $X$ generated by $T$. Then there exists a Laurent cover $(V_j)_{j \in J}$ of $X$ such that $U \mid V_j$ is a rational cover generated by units of $\mathcal{O}_X(V_j)$ for all $j \in J$.
>
> Indeed, for all $x \in X$ there exists $f_i$ such that $x(f_i) \ne 0$. Thus by Corollary 7.32 there exists a unit $s \in A^{\times}$ such that for all $x \in X$ an $i \in \{0, \dots, n\}$ exists with $x(s) < x(f_i)$. Then the Laurent cover generated by $s^{-1}f_1, \dots, s^{-1}f_r$ satisfies the claim.
>
> **(iii)** Every rational cover $U$ of $X$ which is generated by units $f_0, \dots, f_n$ of $A$ has a refinement by a Laurent cover. Indeed, the Laurent cover generated by $\{f_i f_j^{-1};\ 0 \le i, j \le n\}$ is a refinement of $U$.
>
> **(iv)** As the restriction of Laurent covers to arbitrary rational subsets are $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic by (i), it follows from (iii) that all restrictions to rational subsets of all rational covers generated by units are $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic (Proposition A.3 (2)). Now let $U$ be a rational cover generated by some finite subset $T \subseteq A$ with $T \cdot A = A$ and let $V$ be a Laurent cover such that $U \mid V$ is a rational cover generated by a finite set of units for all $V$ in $V$ (which exists by (ii)). Then we have just seen that $U \mid V$ is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic. Moreover, by (i) $V \mid U$ is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic for every $U$ in $U$. Thus the $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclicity of $V$ implies the $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclicity of $U$ by Proposition A.3 (1). $\blacksquare$

### 2.4. Wedhorn Proposition A.3 (the acyclicity combinator)

> **Proposition A.3.** *Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a presheaf of abelian groups and let $U = (U_i)_{i \in I}$ and $V = (V_j)_{j \in J}$ be open coverings of $X$ such that $V \mid U_{i_0 \dots i_q}$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic for all $(i_0, \dots, i_q) \in I^{q+1}$ and for all $q \ge 0$.*
>
> *(1) Assume that also $U \mid V_{j_0 \dots j_q}$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic for all $(j_0, \dots, j_q) \in J^{q+1}$ and all $q \ge 0$. Then $U$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic if and only if $V$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic.*
>
> *(2) If $V$ is a refinement of $U$, then $U$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic if and only if $V$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic.*
>
> *(3) Then $U \times V$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic if and only if $U$ is $\mathcal{F}$-acyclic.*

### 4.1 W1 — Standard cover existence for an arbitrary rational covering

**Wedhorn correspondence.** This is the *input* to Wedhorn 8.34 — the lemma says "given a rational cover $C$, there is a finite generating set $T$ such that the standard cover generated by $T$ refines $C$ and satisfies $T \cdot A = A$." Wedhorn assumes this is given; we need it as a constructive existence claim for arbitrary $C$.

**Lemma (W1, standard cover existence).** *Let $A$ be a strongly noetherian Tate ring (with the usual Hausdorff + nonarchimedean + domain hypotheses) and let $C$ be a rational covering of a base datum $C.\mathrm{base}$. Then there exists a finite set $S \subseteq A$ satisfying:*

- *(refines\_cover)* *Every $v \in R(C.\mathrm{base}.T / C.\mathrm{base}.s)$ lies in $R(\mathrm{insert}(f, C.\mathrm{base}.T) / C.\mathrm{base}.s)$ for some $f \in S$.*
- *(refines\_contain)* *Every plus-piece $R(\mathrm{insert}(f, C.\mathrm{base}.T) / C.\mathrm{base}.s)$ for $f \in S$ is contained in some $C$-piece $E \in C.\mathrm{covers}$.*
- *(refines\_span\_top)* *The ideal generated by $S$ in $A$ is the unit ideal, i.e. $(S) = A$.*

**Lean signature.**
```lean
theorem exists_standard_cover_refining
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (C : RationalCovering A) :
    ∃ S : Finset A,
      refines_cover C S ∧
      refines_contain C S ∧
      refines_span_top S
```

**Note on the project's setup.** The project's existing infrastructure has `exists_refines_cover_per_E_of_standardShape` and the Hübner Nullstellensatz refinement, both of which produce a standard cover refining $C$ under more specific hypotheses. W1 is the abstract existence claim packaging this for arbitrary $C$.

### 4.2 W2 — First-stage Laurent tree with inducing + disjointness + unit property

**Wedhorn correspondence.** Step (i) of Wedhorn 8.34 ("Laurent products are $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic", via Prop A.3 (3)) combined with Step (ii)'s output: at every leaf $L$ of the balanced Laurent tree on $s^{-1} \cdot S$, the σ-minus indices of $S$ are units in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$. The unit $s \in A^{\times}$ is Cor 7.32's dominating unit.

**Lemma (W2, first-stage Laurent tree).** *Let $A$, $C$ be as in W1, and let $S \subseteq A$ be the standard cover from W1 (with the three refinement properties). Then there exist a unit $s \in A^{\times}$ and a balanced Laurent tree $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ over $A$ such that:*

- *$t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ is the balanced Laurent tree on the list $((S.\mathrm{toList}).\mathrm{map}(f \mapsto s^{-1} \cdot f))$, where balanced means each internal node has both children at the same shape recursively.*
- *(allSplitsInducing)* *Every internal Laurent split inside $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ (interpreted with running base descending from $C.\mathrm{base}$) gives an inducing 2-cover — i.e., the diagonal `productRestrictionSub` for the 2-piece cover is `IsInducing` at every internal node.*
- *(allNodesDisjoint)* *Every internal node of $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ has plus-data $\ne$ minus-data and the left/right sub-coverings are Finset-disjoint.*
- *(unit property at each leaf)* *For every leaf $L$ of $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ at root $C.\mathrm{base}$, there is a sub-family $I_{\mathrm{units}}(L) \subseteq S$ such that for every $f \in I_{\mathrm{units}}(L)$, the image of $s^{-1} \cdot f$ in the presheaf value $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$ is a unit.*

**Lean signature.**
```lean
theorem exists_first_stage_laurent_tree_full
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (P : PairOfDefinition A) [IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]
    (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (_hS_cover : refines_cover C S)
    (_hS_contain : refines_contain C S)
    (_hS_span : refines_span_top S) :
    ∃ (s : Aˣ) (t_outer : LaurentTree A),
      t_outer = LaurentTree.ofBalancedList
        ((S.toList).map (fun f => ((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f)) ∧
      t_outer.allSplitsInducing C.base ∧
      t_outer.allNodesDisjoint C.base ∧
      ∀ L ∈ t_outer.leaves C.base,
        ∃ I_units : Finset A,
          I_units ⊆ S ∧
          ∀ f ∈ I_units, IsUnit (L.canonicalMap (((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f))
```

**Note on $s$.** The current Lean statement leaves $s$ existentially quantified — the prover may pick $s$ freely. The intended (canonical) choice is **Cor 7.32's dominating unit**: $s \in A^{\times}$ such that for all $v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^{+})$, there exists $f \in S$ with $v(s) < v(f)$ strictly. The canonical $s$ ensures:
- The σ-true leaves of $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ are individually contained in $S$'s plus-pieces (hence in $C$-pieces, by W1's refines\_contain).
- At every $v$ in any leaf, the dominating $f$ is σ-minus (hence a unit at the leaf), which is the engine of Step (ii).

**Question Q1 (we surface this below):** Should W2's statement fix $s$ to be Cor 7.32's dominating unit explicitly, or is the existential form sufficient? The project's own I.2 (the weaker version of W2, with only the unit property) chose $s = 1$ for the proof of the unit-property at σ-minus leaves; that proof used `balancedLeafBase_isUnit_get_of_false` without any dominating-unit input. The `allSplitsInducing` + `allNodesDisjoint` properties at the outer node level may force a non-trivial $s$.

### 4.3 W3 — Inner ratio Laurent tree refining $C$

**Wedhorn correspondence.** Step (iii) of Wedhorn 8.34: a unit-generated rational cover has a refinement by a Laurent cover of the unit ratios. Specifically, the unit-generated cover $U \mid L$ at a first-stage leaf $L$ is refined by the Laurent cover generated by $\{f_i \cdot f_j^{-1} :\ 0 \le i, j \le |I_{\mathrm{units}}(L)|\}$ inside $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$.

**Lemma (W3, inner ratio Laurent tree).** *Let $A$, $C$, $S$ be as in W1, let $s \in A^{\times}$ be as in W2 (whether existential or Cor-7.32-canonical). For any rational locality datum $L$ that is a sub-base of $C.\mathrm{base}$ (i.e. $R(L.T/L.s) \subseteq R(C.\mathrm{base}.T/C.\mathrm{base}.s)$) and any finite sub-family $I_{\mathrm{units}} \subseteq S$ of $f$'s with $s^{-1} \cdot f$ a unit in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$: there exists a Laurent tree $\mathrm{inner}$ over $A$ such that:*

- *(Refines)* *Every leaf $K$ of $\mathrm{inner}$ at root $L$ has its rational open $R(K.T/K.s)$ contained in some piece $E \in C.\mathrm{covers}$.*
- *(allSplitsInducing)* *Every internal split of $\mathrm{inner}$ gives an inducing 2-cover at its running base.*
- *(allNodesDisjoint)* *Every internal node of $\mathrm{inner}$ has distinct + Finset-disjoint sub-coverings.*

**Lean signature.**
```lean
theorem exists_inner_ratio_laurent_tree_refining_C
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (P : PairOfDefinition A) [IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]
    (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (_hS_contain : refines_contain C S)
    (s : Aˣ)
    (L : RationalLocData A)
    (_hL_subset : rationalOpen L.T L.s ⊆ rationalOpen C.base.T C.base.s)
    (I_units : Finset A)
    (_hI_units_subset : I_units ⊆ S)
    (_hI_units_unit : ∀ f ∈ I_units,
      IsUnit (L.canonicalMap (((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f))) :
    ∃ inner : LaurentTree A,
      inner.Refines L C ∧
      inner.allSplitsInducing L ∧
```

**Note on the encoding of "ratio Laurent" as `LaurentTree A`.** Wedhorn's ratios $f_i \cdot f_j^{-1}$ live in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$, not in $A$ — the inverses $f_j^{-1}$ exist in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$ but not generally in $A$. The bridge from "ratio Laurent in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$" to "Laurent tree in $A$" is *not* yet formalised: it requires either (a) a relative-labels `RatioLaurentTree` type (type layer landed but bridge open), or (b) an A-level denominator-cleared formulation where each ratio split is encoded as a Laurent split at a constructed A-element with a transformed denominator (analogous to the project's existing `ratioPlusDatum`, but without requiring an A-level inverse).

### 4.4 W4 — Cross-leaf disjointness for the canonical Wedhorn inner-tree construction

**Wedhorn correspondence.** *Not present in Wedhorn explicitly* — this is a Lean-formalisation-specific structural property of the canonical Wedhorn construction, needed because our `LaurentTree.graftAt` operation and the `allNodesDisjoint` predicate are Finset-based, so the graft's disjointness requires that distinct outer-leaf inner trees produce disjoint leaf-Finsets.

**Lemma (W4, cross-leaf disjointness).** *Let $A$, $C$, $S$, $s$ be as above. Let $\mathrm{units\_of} : \{\text{rational locality data over } A\} \to \mathrm{Finset}(A)$ be a per-leaf unit family with $\mathrm{units\_of}(L) \subseteq S$ and each $f \in \mathrm{units\_of}(L)$ giving $s^{-1} \cdot f$ unit at $L$. Let $\mathrm{inner\_of} : \{\text{rational locality data over } A\} \to \mathrm{LaurentTree}\,A$ be a per-leaf inner-tree assignment with each $\mathrm{inner\_of}(L)$ satisfying $\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}$ and $\mathrm{allNodesDisjoint}$ at base $L$.*

*Then for any two distinct rational locality data $K_1 \ne K_2$, the leaf-Finsets of $\mathrm{inner\_of}(K_1)$ at base $K_1$ and $\mathrm{inner\_of}(K_2)$ at base $K_2$ are disjoint:*

$$\mathrm{Disjoint}\bigl(\mathrm{inner\_of}(K_1).\mathrm{toCoveringCovers}(K_1),\ \mathrm{inner\_of}(K_2).\mathrm{toCoveringCovers}(K_2)\bigr).$$

**Lean signature.**
```lean
theorem inner_ratio_trees_cross_leaf_disjoint
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (P : PairOfDefinition A) [IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]
    (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (hS_contain : refines_contain C S)
    (s : Aˣ)
    (units_of : RationalLocData A → Finset A)
    (h_units_subset : ∀ L, units_of L ⊆ S)
    (h_units_unit : ∀ L, ∀ f ∈ units_of L,
      IsUnit (L.canonicalMap (((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f)))
    (inner_of : RationalLocData A → LaurentTree A)
    (h_inner_spec : ∀ L,
      (inner_of L).allSplitsInducing L ∧
      (inner_of L).allNodesDisjoint L) :
    ∀ K₁ K₂ : RationalLocData A, K₁ ≠ K₂ →
      Disjoint ((inner_of K₁).toCoveringCovers K₁)
               ((inner_of K₂).toCoveringCovers K₂)
```

**Note on provability.** As stated, W4 is *almost certainly false* for an arbitrary `inner_of` — the leaves of a Laurent tree at different bases can coincide as `RationalLocData` in pathological cases. The intent is that the **canonical Wedhorn construction** of inner trees (from W3's existence) satisfies this property because each leaf's data carries $K_1$-specific elements (the σ-minus units at $K_1$) in its $T$-field. The current statement leaves `inner_of` as an *abstract* function, but the lemma is only true (and only needed) for the *canonical* `inner_of`.

**Question Q3 (we surface this below):** Should W4 be strengthened so its `inner_of` argument is specified to be the canonical W3 construction (e.g., made `inner_of := canonicalWedhornInner(units_of, s, P, C, S, hS_contain)`), or should W4 take an extra hypothesis like "for each $L$, the leaves of `inner_of(L)` at $L$ all have $\{f \in L.T : f \in S\}$ matching `units_of(L)`" that distinguishes them?

## 5. The fifth helper (W5) — closed

For completeness: W5 is the non-existential form of I.4's structural-induction graft preservation:

**Lemma (W5, closed).** *Let $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ be a Laurent tree, $D_0$ a base datum, $h$ a per-leaf inner-tree assignment. Suppose $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ satisfies $\mathrm{allNodesDisjoint}$ at $D_0$, each $h(L)$ for $L$ a leaf of $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ at $D_0$ satisfies $\mathrm{allNodesDisjoint}$ at $L$, and the cross-leaf disjointness from W4 holds for $h$. Then the grafted tree $t_{\mathrm{outer}}.\mathrm{graftAt}(D_0, h)$ satisfies $\mathrm{allNodesDisjoint}$ at $D_0$.*

**Lean signature.**
```lean
theorem graftAt_allNodesDisjoint
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A]
    (t_outer : LaurentTree A) (D₀ : RationalLocData A)
    (h : RationalLocData A → LaurentTree A)
    (h_outer_disj : t_outer.allNodesDisjoint D₀)
    (h_inner_disj : ∀ L ∈ t_outer.leaves D₀, (h L).allNodesDisjoint L)
    (h_cross_disj : ∀ K₁ K₂ : RationalLocData A, K₁ ≠ K₂ →
      Disjoint ((h K₁).toCoveringCovers K₁) ((h K₂).toCoveringCovers K₂)) :
    (t_outer.graftAt D₀ h).allNodesDisjoint D₀
```

The proof is by structural induction on $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$. The leaf case uses the inner-disj hypothesis at $D_0$ directly.
