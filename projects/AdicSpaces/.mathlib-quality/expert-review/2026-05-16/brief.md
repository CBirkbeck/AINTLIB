## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

Let $A$ be a strongly noetherian Tate ring with plus-subring $A^+$. We work inside the adic spectrum $\mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+)$. For a finite set $T \subset A$ and $s \in A$ write
$$R(T, s) := \{v \in \mathrm{Spa}(A, A^+) \mid v(t) \le v(s)\ \forall t \in T,\ v(s) \ne 0\}.$$

A *rational localisation datum* $D = (P, T, s)$ packages a pair of definition $P = (A_0, I)$ for $A$ (Wedhorn 6.1), a finite set $T \subset A$, an element $s \in A$, and an openness condition `hopen` that makes the localisation topology on $A_s$ well-defined: $\exists N$ such that $\forall b \in I^N$, $b/s$ lies in the subring $\mathrm{locSubring}(P, T, s) \subset A_s$ generated by $\mathrm{image}(A_0) \cup \{t/s : t \in T\}$.

Write $\mathcal{O}(D)$ for the completion of $A_s$ in the localisation topology of $D$, and $\rho_D : A \to \mathcal{O}(D)$ for the canonical map.

### 2.2. References

- **[Hübner 2019]** Katharina Hübner, "The adic completion of the étale fundamental group", *J. Algebra* 537, pp. 254–268.
- **[Zavyalov 2022]** Bogdan Zavyalov, "Quotients of admissible formal schemes and adic spaces", §2.3.

### 2.3. State of the art

Wedhorn 8.28(b) is classical. The formalisation is the novelty. Mathlib has the field-only `Valued.extensionValuation` for completion-side valuation extension; not the commutative-ring version we need. Wedhorn 7.49 (the Spa equivalence $\mathrm{Spa}(\mathcal{O}(D)) \simeq R(D)$) is classical but **not formalised** in the form we need.

## 3. Strategy (Wedhorn 8.34 chain)

1. **W1.** Given a rational covering $C$, find $S \subset A$ with $\mathrm{span}(S) = A$ such that each plus-piece $R(\{f\} \cup C_{\mathrm{base}}.T, C_{\mathrm{base}}.s)$ for $f \in S$ refines $C$.
2. **W2.** Cor 7.32 produces a dominating unit $s \in A^\times$; the balanced Laurent tree on $s^{-1} \cdot S$ has `allSplitsInducing` at every internal node; at every leaf $L$ there is $I_{\mathrm{units}} \subseteq S$ such that each $\rho_L(s^{-1} \cdot f)$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(L)$.
3. **W3.** At each leaf $L$, the unit-generated cover over $\mathcal{O}(L)$ is refined by the relative Laurent tree at the pairwise unit ratios $u_g \cdot u_h^{-1}$.
4. **W3-transport.** The relative tree transports to an absolute ratio-Laurent tree over $A$.
5. **Assembly.** W1 + W2 + W3 + W3-transport → ratio-Laurent tree refining $C$ with `allSplitsInducing`. This feeds the topological-inducing argument for `IsSheafy`.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 (relative unit generator).** For a leaf $L$ with $h_{\mathrm{base}} : \mathrm{IsUnit}(\rho_L(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s))$, and $f \in I_{\mathrm{units}}$, the *relative unit generator* is
$$u_f := \rho_L(f) \cdot h_{\mathrm{base}}^{-1} \in \mathcal{O}(L).$$
Equivalently $u_f$ corresponds to $f / C_{\mathrm{base}}.s$ in the completed localisation.

**Definition 4.2 (`ratioPlusDatum`, project-level — Wedhorn 2.13 reverse, A-side-inverse case).** Given $D_0 \in \mathrm{RationalLocData}(A)$, $f, g, g^{-1} \in A$ with $g \cdot g^{-1} = 1$ and $g^{-1} \in D_0.A_0$, the ratio plus datum has $T_{\mathrm{plus}} := (\{D_0.s\} \cup D_0.T) \times \{f, g\}$ (multiplied), $s_{\mathrm{plus}} := D_0.s \cdot g$, and `hopen` proven by $\mathrm{divByS}\ b\ (D_0.s \cdot g) = \mathrm{algebraMap}(g^{-1}) \cdot \mathrm{divByS}(b \cdot g)\ (D_0.s \cdot g)$. The rational open is $R(D_0) \cap \{v(f) \le v(g)\} \cap \{v(g) \ne 0\}$. **Requires $g^{-1} \in A$, not just in the completion.**

**Definition 4.3 (canonical relative base).** $L_{\mathrm{rel}}$ over $\mathcal{O}(L)$ is *canonical* if $L_{\mathrm{rel}}.T = \emptyset$ and $L_{\mathrm{rel}}.s = 1$. Then $R(L_{\mathrm{rel}}) = \mathrm{Spa}(\mathcal{O}(L), \mathcal{O}(L)^+)$.

**Definition 4.4 (`IsRelativeUnitPieceFor`).** A relative rational data piece $E$ over $\mathcal{O}(L)$ is the *relative unit piece for $f$* if
- $E.T = \{u_f\}$, $E.s = 1$, and
- $\mathrm{image}\ (\mathrm{comap}\ L.\rho)\ (R(E)) = R(L) \cap \{v(f) \le v(C_{\mathrm{base}}.s)\}$.

The second clause is the Spa-equivalence statement *as a transport equality*, supplied at definition time. The W2 → W3 chain produces $\mathrm{unitCover}$ whose pieces are unit-pieces, so this equality is available where it's needed.

## 5. Established (without disallowed hypotheses)

**Theorem 5.1 (`comap_canonicalMap_not_vle_zero_of_isUnit_via_locLift`).** *Let $D \in \mathrm{RationalLocData}(A)$ with $D.A_0 \le A^+$. For $v \in R(D)$, $g \in A$, if $\rho_D(g)$ is a unit in $\mathcal{O}(D)$ then $v(g) \ne 0$.*

*Sketch.* `localizationLift` $v$ to $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ on $A_s$ (continuous in locTopology). Density of $A_s \hookrightarrow \mathcal{O}(D)$ + multiplicative continuity + non-arch T2 argument: the inverse of $\rho_D(g)$ lifts via filter to "approximate inverses" $y_n$ in $A_s$; $g \cdot y_n \to 1$ in $A_s$; if $v(g) = 0$ then $w_{\mathrm{loc}}(g \cdot y_n) = 0$ constantly, contradicting convergence to $w_{\mathrm{loc}}(1) = 1$ in T2. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (project: `ratioPlusDatum`, `ratioMinusDatum`).** Wedhorn 2.13 reverse for the A-side-inverse case (Definition 4.2). Fully proved; the hopen identity uses the explicit inverse.

## 7. What we tried and why it failed

### For Target A (P3, Wedhorn 2.13 reverse without A-side inverse)

Tried shapes, all failed for the same structural reason:

1. **Denominator-cleared with $L.P$.** Set $T_{\mathrm{plus}} = L.T \cdot h \cup \{L.s \cdot g, L.s \cdot h\}$, $s_{\mathrm{plus}} = L.s \cdot h$, $P_{\mathrm{plus}} = L.P$. Set equality $R(D) = R(L) \cap \{v(g) \le v(h)\}$ works (after using Theorem 5.1 to clean $v(h) \ne 0$). The hopen requires showing $\mathrm{divByS}\ b\ (L.s \cdot h) \in \mathrm{locSubring}$ for $b \in L.P.I^N$. This requires $1/(L.s \cdot h)$ to be in the locSubring, which requires either $1$ or $L.s$ in $T_{\mathrm{plus}}$. Either insertion adds a non-automatic constraint to $R(D)$, breaking the set equality.

2. **`laurentMinusNormalizedDatum L h` (which inserts $1$ into $T$).** Works only when $v(L.s \cdot h) \ge 1$ is forced, which holds for the original `laurentMinusDatum` case because $v(L.s) = 1$ (under `LaurentNormalized`) and $v(L.s) \le v(h)$ from the existing $T$. For our $T$ without the $L.s$ forcing, $v(L.s) \le v(h)$ is not automatic; so the trick fails.

3. **`ratioPlusDatum L g h h^{-1}`.** Works if $h^{-1} \in L.P.A_0$ — disallowed.

The structural pattern: the locSubring generated by $T_{\mathrm{plus}}$ does not contain $1/(L.s \cdot h)$ without inserting an element that costs a non-automatic constraint on $R$. Some other pair $P_{\mathrm{plus}} \ne L.P$ must be the right answer — but we can't see what to write down.

### For Target B (P4 leaf, Wedhorn 7.49 reverse)

The mathematical content: $v \in R(L)$ continuous → lift to $w$ on $\mathcal{O}(L)$ continuous.

Algebraic part (Spv-level lift through $A_s$): fine, via `localizationLift`. Gives $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ continuous on $A_s$ with $\mathrm{comap}\ \mathrm{algebraMap}\ w_{\mathrm{loc}} = v$.

Completion part (lift $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ from $A_s$ to $\mathcal{O}(L)$): mathlib's `Valued.extensionValuation` for fields uses the inversion estimate, which fails for commutative rings.

Our attempted approach: `IsDenseInducing.continuous_extend` for the function $w_{\mathrm{loc}} : A_s \to \Gamma_0$. Requires:
- $\Gamma_0$ is T3 (we can supply this via WithZeroTopology).
- For every $b \in \mathcal{O}(L)$, the filter $\mathrm{Filter.comap\ coe}\ \mathcal{N}(b)$ on $A_s$ has its image under $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ convergent in $\Gamma_0$.

The "strict-triangle Cauchy" argument: $\mathrm{Filter.comap\ coe}\ \mathcal{N}(b)$ is Cauchy on $A_s$. By non-arch strict triangle, $w_{\mathrm{loc}}$ of a Cauchy filter is either eventually 0 or eventually constant nonzero. Either way the image converges. This is mathematically correct but we've not written it cleanly in Lean.
