## 2. Revised lemma statements (math + Lean)

### 2.1 New helper definition: restricted-standard-cover-generated-by-units

**Mathematical content.** For a first-stage Laurent leaf $L$ (with sub-base of $C.\mathrm{base}$), a subfamily $I_{\mathrm{units}} \subseteq S$ generates the restricted standard cover $U \mid L$ by units if:
- $I_{\mathrm{units}} \subseteq S$ (the chosen subfamily comes from the standard cover);
- every $f \in I_{\mathrm{units}}$ has $s^{-1} \cdot f$ a unit in $\mathcal{O}_X(L)$;
- the $I_{\mathrm{units}}$' plus-pieces (at $C.\mathrm{base}$'s denominator) cover $L$'s rational open: $\forall v \in R(L.T/L.s),\ \exists f \in I_{\mathrm{units}},\ v \in R(\mathrm{insert}(f, C.\mathrm{base}.T)/C.\mathrm{base}.s)$.

The third clause is what forces $I_{\mathrm{units}} \ne \emptyset$ and captures Wedhorn Step (ii)'s "restricted cover is generated by units" content.

**Lean.**
```lean
def restricted_standard_cover_generated_by_units
    (L : RationalLocData A) (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (s : Aˣ) (I_units : Finset A) : Prop :=
  I_units ⊆ S ∧
  (∀ f ∈ I_units, IsUnit (L.canonicalMap (((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f))) ∧
  (∀ v ∈ rationalOpen L.T L.s, ∃ f ∈ I_units,
    v ∈ rationalOpen (insert f C.base.T) C.base.s)
```

### 2.2 W2 (revised): first-stage Laurent tree with restricted-cover-by-units

**Mathematical content.** *Let $A$, $C$, $S$ be as before with the standard-cover refinement properties. Then there exist $s \in A^{\times}$ (Cor 7.32 dominating unit) and a balanced Laurent tree $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ on $((S.\mathrm{toList}).\mathrm{map}(f \mapsto s^{-1} \cdot f))$ such that:*

- *$t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ satisfies $\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}$ at $C.\mathrm{base}$ (every internal Laurent split is an inducing 2-cover);*
- *for every leaf $L$ of $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ at $C.\mathrm{base}$, there is a sub-family $I_{\mathrm{units}}(L) \subseteq S$ with $\mathrm{restricted\_standard\_cover\_generated\_by\_units}(L, C, S, s, I_{\mathrm{units}}(L))$.*

**Lean.**
```lean
theorem exists_first_stage_laurent_tree_unit_generated
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (P : PairOfDefinition A) [IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]
    (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (_hS_cover : refines_cover C S)
    (_hS_contain : refines_contain C S)
    (_hS_span : refines_span_top S) :
    ∃ (s : Aˣ) (t_outer : LaurentTree A),
      t_outer = LaurentTree.ofBalancedList
        ((S.toList).map (fun f => ((s⁻¹ : Aˣ) : A) * f)) ∧
      t_outer.allSplitsInducing C.base ∧
      ∀ L ∈ t_outer.leaves C.base,
        ∃ I_units : Finset A,
          restricted_standard_cover_generated_by_units L C S s I_units
```

### 2.3 W3 (revised): relative-labels inner ratio Laurent refinement

**Mathematical content.** *Let $L$ be a sub-base of $C.\mathrm{base}$ and $I_{\mathrm{units}} \subseteq S$ a subfamily generating the restricted standard cover by units. Suppose the relative ring $\mathcal{O}(L) = \mathrm{presheafValue}\,L$ carries the project's standing hypotheses (Tate, Noetherian, Strongly Noetherian, Hausdorff, Nonarchimedean, Domain, plus the analytic-class data). Then there exists a relative Laurent tree $\mathrm{inner\_rel} \in \mathrm{LaurentTree}(\mathcal{O}(L))$ (whose splits are labelled by elements of $\mathcal{O}(L)$) with $\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}$ at the relative base $L_{\mathrm{rel}}$.*

### 2.4 W3-transport: relative-to-absolute Laurent tree

**Mathematical content.** *Given a relative inner tree $\mathrm{inner\_rel} \in \mathrm{LaurentTree}(\mathcal{O}(L))$ at $L_{\mathrm{rel}}$ with $\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}$, and the W2 data $(C, S, s, L, I_{\mathrm{units}})$ with the restricted-cover-by-units property, there exists an absolute tree $\mathrm{inner\_abs} \in \mathrm{LaurentTree}(A)$ at $L$ with:*

- *$\mathrm{inner\_abs}.\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}\ L$ — inducing at the absolute level;*
- *$\mathrm{inner\_abs}.\mathrm{Refines}\ L\ C$ — every absolute inner-tree-leaf is contained in some $C$-piece (the descent from relative to absolute happens here, via $\mathrm{presheafValue\_relative\_equiv}$ (III.1) + its homeomorphism (III.2) + W1's $\mathrm{refines\_contain}$).*

**Lean.**
```lean
theorem relative_laurent_tree_to_absolute
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A] [DecidableEq A]
    (P : PairOfDefinition A) [IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]
    (C : RationalCovering A) (S : Finset A)
    (_hS_contain : refines_contain C S)
    (s : Aˣ)
    (L : RationalLocData A)
    (_hL_subset : rationalOpen L.T L.s ⊆ rationalOpen C.base.T C.base.s)
    (I_units : Finset A)
    (_h_unit_generated : restricted_standard_cover_generated_by_units L C S s I_units)
    [IsNoetherianRing (locSubring L.P L.T L.s)]
    [IsTopologicalRing (presheafValue L)] [PlusSubring (presheafValue L)]
    [IsHuberRing (presheafValue L)] [HasLocLiftPowerBounded (presheafValue L)]
    (L_rel : RationalLocData (presheafValue L))
    (inner_rel : LaurentTree (presheafValue L))
    (_h_split_rel : inner_rel.allSplitsInducing L_rel) :
    ∃ inner_abs : LaurentTree A,
      inner_abs.allSplitsInducing L ∧
      inner_abs.Refines L C
```

### 2.5 W4 (revised): prune preserves refinement and inducing

**Mathematical content.** *Let $t$ be a Laurent tree over $A$ with $t.\mathrm{Refines}\ D_0\ C$ and $t.\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}\ D_0$. Then there exists a pruned Laurent tree $t_{\mathrm{pruned}}$ such that:*

- *$t_{\mathrm{pruned}}.\mathrm{toCovering}\ D_0 = t.\mathrm{toCovering}\ D_0$ — same represented cover;*
- *$t_{\mathrm{pruned}}.\mathrm{Refines}\ D_0\ C$ — refinement preserved;*
- *$t_{\mathrm{pruned}}.\mathrm{allSplitsInducing}\ D_0$ — inducing preserved;*
- *$t_{\mathrm{pruned}}.\mathrm{allNodesDisjoint}\ D_0$ — disjointness added.*

**Lean.**
```lean
theorem prune_preserves_refinement_and_inducing
    [IsTateRing A] [IsNoetherianRing A] [IsStronglyNoetherian A] [T2Space A]
    [NonarchimedeanRing A] [IsDomain A]
    (t : LaurentTree A) (D₀ : RationalLocData A) (C : RationalCovering A)
    (_h_refines : t.Refines D₀ C)
    (_h_split : t.allSplitsInducing D₀) :
    ∃ t_pruned : LaurentTree A,
      t_pruned.toCovering D₀ = t.toCovering D₀ ∧
      t_pruned.Refines D₀ C ∧
      t_pruned.allSplitsInducing D₀ ∧
      t_pruned.allNodesDisjoint D₀
```

## 3. The I.1 chain (Wedhorn 8.34 headline)

The composition that closes I.1:

1. **W1**: get standard cover $S$ refining $C$.
2. **W2**: get Cor 7.32 unit $s$ and outer tree $t_{\mathrm{outer}}$ with `allSplitsInducing` + per-leaf $I_{\mathrm{units}}(L)$ satisfying the restricted-cover-by-units property.
3. **W3 + W3-transport per outer leaf $L$**: choose an absolute inner tree `inner_of(L)` with `allSplitsInducing L ∧ Refines L C`. For non-outer $L$, use `.leaf` (graftAt only consults `inner_of` at outer leaves).
4. **Graft**: form $t_{\mathrm{graft}} := t_{\mathrm{outer}}.\mathrm{graftAt}(C.\mathrm{base}, \mathrm{inner\_of})$. By the existing `Refines_graftAt` and `allSplitsInducing_graftAt`, $t_{\mathrm{graft}}$ has `Refines C.base C ∧ allSplitsInducing C.base`.
5. **W4 prune**: apply to $t_{\mathrm{graft}}$ to obtain $t_{\mathrm{pruned}}$ with all three predicates: `Refines C.base C ∧ allSplitsInducing C.base ∧ allNodesDisjoint C.base`.

Step (3) is the most delicate: composing W3 with W3-transport requires the typeclass instances on `presheafValue L` at each outer leaf. The current I.1 proof body sketches this chain and notes that the full closure depends on the project's pending T-LOCLIFT-PRESERVATION ticket (which gives `HasLocLiftPowerBounded (presheafValue L)`).

## 4. Questions

**Q1.** Does the revised W2 statement (with the new `restricted_standard_cover_generated_by_units` clause) now faithfully capture Wedhorn 8.34 Step (ii)? Specifically, is the cover-decomposing clause $\forall v \in R(L.T/L.s),\ \exists f \in I_{\mathrm{units}},\ v \in R(\mathrm{insert}(f, C.\mathrm{base}.T)/C.\mathrm{base}.s)$ the right formal expression of "the restricted cover $U \mid L$ is generated by units in $\mathcal{O}(L)$"? Or is "generated by" stronger (e.g., a categorical equivalence of covers rather than a covering relation)?

**Q2.** Is the W3 + W3-transport split correctly structured? W3 produces a relative `LaurentTree (presheafValue L)` with only `allSplitsInducing`; W3-transport descends it to absolute `LaurentTree A` with both `allSplitsInducing` AND `Refines L C`. The descent (Refines) is concentrated in the transport step, not in W3 itself. Is this the right division of labor, or should W3 already carry a relative `Refines` claim (which would require defining relative `RationalCovering` over `presheafValue L`)?

**Q3.** Is W4's prune statement provable? The reviewer suggested Option C as a clean alternative to the abstract cross-leaf disjointness. The current statement asserts the prune existentially without constructing it. Two sub-questions:
- (a) Is "any tree with `Refines + allSplitsInducing` has a pruned equivalent with `Refines + allSplitsInducing + allNodesDisjoint` and same `toCovering`" true in this generality?
- (b) If yes, is the natural proof structural induction on the tree, removing trivial / duplicate splits as we go?

**Q4.** Does the I.1 chain (W1 → W2 → W3 → W3-transport → graft → W4 prune) correctly compose? The graft step uses the existing `Refines_graftAt` and `allSplitsInducing_graftAt` (project-side, already proved). The prune step (W4) is applied at the end to clean up `allNodesDisjoint`. Are there any subtle dependencies (typeclass coherence on `presheafValue L`, or hidden hypotheses from Group III's transitivity bridge) that the chain might miss?
