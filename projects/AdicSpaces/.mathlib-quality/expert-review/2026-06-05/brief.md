## 1. Goal

Prove **Wedhorn Theorem 8.28(b)** (p. 80): *Let `A = (A, A⁺)` be an affinoid ring with `A` a
**strongly noetherian Tate ring**, and `X = Spa A`. Then the structure presheaf `O_X` is a sheaf of
complete topological rings, and `H^q(U, O_X) = 0` for all `q ≥ 1` and all rational subsets `U`.*

We formalise case (b) only (cases (a) "Â has a noetherian ring of definition" and (c) "Â discrete"
are out of scope, though (c) is done separately). The headline object is a single statement
"`O_X` is sheafy", packaged as: for every finite rational covering of every rational subset, the
restriction `O_X(base) → ∏ᵢ O_X(Uᵢ)` is a **topological embedding onto the equaliser** (separation +
the topological/strictness data), together with the **gluing** (every compatible family glues).

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting and conventions

- `Spv A` — the valuation spectrum (equivalence classes of valuations `v : A → Γ ∪ {0}`), with the
  topology generated by `{v : v(a) ≤ v(b) ≠ 0}`. Quasi-compact.
- `Cont(A) ⊆ Spv A` — continuous valuations (Wedhorn 7.7–7.10).
- `Spa(A, A⁺) = {v ∈ Cont(A) : v(a) ≤ 1 ∀ a ∈ A⁺}`, the adic spectrum.
- A **Tate ring** is an f-adic ring with a topologically nilpotent unit (a *pseudo-uniformizer*).
  Equivalently it has a ring of definition `A₀` with ideal of definition `I = (s)` for a
  topologically nilpotent unit `s ∈ A₀`.
- **Strongly noetherian**: `A⟨X₁,…,Xₙ⟩` (the Tate algebra of restricted power series, = completion
  of `A[X₁,…,Xₙ]` for the Gauss/Tate topology) is noetherian for **every** `n ≥ 0`. We treat this as
  a property of the *Tate ring* `A`, **not** of any ring of definition.
- `R(T/s) = {x ∈ X : x(t) ≤ x(s) ≠ 0 ∀ t ∈ T}` — rational subset; `O_X(R(T/s)) = A⟨T/s⟩`, the
  completed localisation (a complete Tate ring). We write `O_X(U)` for the section ring.
- **Height-1 / microbial / analytic**: a point `x` is *analytic* if `supp x` is non-open; *microbial*
  if it has a height-1 vertical generalisation; `Xᵃ` is the analytic locus.

**A convention that matters for Q2/Q4.** Wedhorn 7.10 characterises continuity as
`Cont(A) = {v ∈ Spv(A, I) : v(a) < 1 for all a ∈ I}`, where `I` is the **ideal of definition inside
the ring of definition `A₀`** — an ideal of `A₀`, *not* its extension `I·A` to `A`. We had a bug
where a lemma quantified over `I·A` instead of `I`; over `I·A` the statement is false (for a microbial
`v`, a pseudo-uniformizer `t` with large `v(t)` makes `v(t·g) > 1` for `g ∈ I`). The corrected
quantifier is the `A₀`-ideal `I`. We flag this in Q2 because the same distinction recurs.

## 4. Definitions the reviewer may want pinned down

- **`O_X(R(T/s)) := A⟨T/s⟩`**, the completed localisation: completion of `A[1/s]` for the topology
  making the images of `T/s` power-bounded. For a Tate ring with `T·A = A` this is a complete Tate
  ring and the universal complete `A`-algebra in which the `T/s` are power-bounded and `s` is
  invertible (Wedhorn 7.52). We model `O_X` on the basis of rational subsets and extend by A.4's
  inverse-limit formula `O_X(V) = lim_{U⊆V rational} O_X(U)`.
- **"acyclic cover"**: the augmented alternating Čech complex `0 → O_X(U) → Č⁰ → Č¹ → …` is exact.
- **"sheafy" (the headline)**: for every finite rational cover, `O_X(base) → ∏ O_X(Uᵢ)` is a
  topological embedding whose image is the equaliser of the two maps to `∏ O_X(Uᵢ ∩ Uⱼ)`, and every
  compatible family lifts. (This is `q = 0` exactness as topological rings; `q ≥ 1` is the gluing
  acyclicity.)

Everything else (Spv, Spa, Cont, rational subset, Tate algebra, strongly noetherian) is standard and
as in §2.1.

## 8. Where we're stuck (the heart)

### 8.1. The three deepest leaves coincide with Wedhorn's own external citations

This is the observation we most want sanity-checked. The deepest things our tree bottoms out at are
**exactly the results Wedhorn does not prove**:

- **Prop 6.17** (`A` complete Tate: `A` noetherian ⟺ every ideal closed) — Wedhorn writes literally
  "**Proof. Missing**."
- **Prop 6.18** (`A` complete noetherian Tate: f.g. modules have a unique complete topology with
  countable basis at 0; linear maps of f.g. modules are continuous and open onto image) — again
  "**Proof. Missing**."
- **Prop 7.48** (`Spa Â → Spa A` is a homeomorphism preserving rational subsets) — "Proof. [Hu2]
  Prop. 3.9."
- **Lemma 7.54** (every covering refines to a rational cover generated by an ideal-generating `T`) —
  "Proof. [Hu3] Lemma 2.6."

**What 6.17 and 6.18 actually demand, and why they are the same circle.** Both are non-archimedean
**open-mapping** statements. 6.18(1) says a finitely generated module `M` over a complete noetherian
Tate ring `A` carries a *unique* complete module topology with a countable basis at `0` (concretely,
`{sⁿM₀}` for a f.g. `A₀`-lattice `M₀` and pseudo-uniformizer `s`, Wedhorn 6.19); 6.18(2) says any
`A`-linear `f : M → N` between two such modules is automatically continuous and **open onto its
image**. The proof one expects is: present `M`, equip everything with the lattice topologies, and
invoke a Banach open-mapping theorem to upgrade "continuous bijection of complete modules" to
"homeomorphism". 6.17 is the companion: `A` noetherian ⟺ every ideal is closed, which is the
open-mapping theorem applied to `A → A/𝔞`. These feed our two open leaves: 6.18(1)(2) drive Remark
8.29 (`µ_M` bijective, via the 5-lemma with continuous-open rows), and **6.18(2) is precisely the
topological inducing leaf** (the embedding `O_X(base) ↪ ∏ O_X(Uᵢ)` is open onto its image).

**The obstruction that makes this non-trivial.** A Tate ring is **not σ-compact** in general (its
underlying additive group need not be a countable union of compacts — e.g. `ℂ_p⟨X⟩`), so the
classical Banach OMT (complete + σ-compact / Baire) does **not** apply verbatim; an earlier version
of the project assumed `[SigmaCompactSpace]` and it was unfulfillable for `Aⁿ`. The faithful
replacement we have built is an open-mapping theorem for **rings with a zero-sequence of units**
(the pseudo-uniformizer powers `sⁿ → 0` are units), à la Henkel (arXiv:1407.5647), realised in the
project via a units-`→0` dilation cover rather than σ-compactness. We currently treat 6.17/6.18/7.48/
7.54 as **external inputs** discharged by this OMT (for 6.17/6.18) or deferred to Huber (7.48/7.54).
**Q3 asks whether this is the right call and what the cleanest self-contained reference is** — in
particular whether the zero-sequence-of-units OMT is the standard tool here or whether [BGR] §2.8
gives a more directly citable statement in the Tate generality.

### 8.2. The quasi-compactness keystone without assuming height-1

Wedhorn uses "`U` is quasi-compact" freely (Remark 7.55 "since `U` is quasi-compact", Cor 7.32,
Lemma 7.54). To get `Spa A` quasi-compact we need it **closed in the quasi-compact `Spv A`** (which
we have, via the cylinder encoding + Tychonoff). The precise source statement is:

> **Theorem 7.10 (Wedhorn, verbatim).** Let `A` be an f-adic ring and `I` an ideal of definition of
> a ring of definition of `A`. Then
> `Cont(A) = { v ∈ Spv(A, I·A) ; v(a) < 1 for all a ∈ I }`.

Two things must be read carefully, and they are the crux of Q2:

1. **The ambient is `Spv(A, I·A)`**, the spectral retraction space `{v ∈ Spv A : cΓ_v(I·A) = Γv}`
   (Wedhorn 7.1.1), onto which `r : Spv A → Spv(A, I·A)`, `v ↦ v|cΓ_v(I·A)`, is a continuous spectral
   retraction (7.1.2). By Lemma 7.5(1), `Spv(A, I·A)` is a **spectral space** with a basis of
   quasi-compact opens `Spv(A,I·A)(T/s) = {v : v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 ∀ t ∈ T}` (`I·A ⊆ √(T·A)`), stable
   under finite intersection; and `Cont(A)` is itself spectral (Cor 7.12).
2. **The cut condition `v(a) < 1` ranges over the `A₀`-ideal `I`, not its `A`-extension `I·A`.** This
   is the bug we just fixed: we had quantified `v(a) < 1` over `I·A`, which is **false** — for a
   microbial `v`, a pseudo-uniformizer `t` with `v(t)` large gives `v(t·g) > 1` for `g ∈ I`, so
   `t·g ∈ I·A` violates `< 1`. Wedhorn quantifies the cut over `I` (the `A₀`-ideal) and uses `I·A`
   *only* for the ambient spectral membership. The forward direction (`v` continuous ⟹ `v(a) < 1`
   ∀`a ∈ I`) is then exactly Wedhorn's three-line argument: every `a ∈ I` is topologically nilpotent,
   so `v(a)ⁿ < γ` for some `n`, whence `v(a)` is cofinal and `< 1`. The converse (the harder half)
   is the cofinality-via-boundedness argument (`v(t)⁻¹ ≤ γ`, `t·aⁿ ∈ I`, so `v(a)ⁿ < γ`) ending in
   the `Iⁿ⁺¹` continuity bound.

So the faithful realisation of the keystone is: `Spa(A,A⁺) = { v ∈ Spv(A, I·A) : v(a) < 1 ∀ a ∈ I } ∩
{ v : v(f) ≤ 1 ∀ f ∈ A⁺ }`. In the project's constructible/Bool encoding each `{v(a) < 1}` (`a ∈ I`)
and each `{v(f) ≤ 1}` (`f ∈ A⁺`) is a **closed cylinder**, and the `Spv(A, I·A)`-membership is the
cofinality coordinate constraint `cΓ_v(I·A) = Γv` (Wedhorn 7.1.1) — the only non-cylinder ingredient,
and the one our open obligation must encode in the no-height-1 case. Under the false blanket
"every valuation is height-1 (Archimedean/`hArch`)", the cofinality constraint is automatic and the
keystone reduces to cylinders alone; **the faithful version must keep the `Spv(A, I·A)` coordinate
constraint instead of assuming it away.** That is exactly the open leaf.

The subtlety that makes height-1 *faithful* rather than a cheat: Wedhorn's Lemma 7.45 *constructs* a
height-1 (microbial) point — height-1 is a **property of the constructed witness**, obtained via the
continuity characterisation (7.10) + Remark 7.40(5) (microbiality) + a vertical generalisation
(Remark 4.12) + Prop 7.41 (a height-1 analytic valuation lies in `Spa A`). So height-1 enters
Wedhorn's proof legitimately, point by point; what is *not* legitimate is assuming every valuation in
`Spv A` is height-1. We want the keystone proved with height-1 only where Wedhorn uses it. **Q2 asks
you to confirm this `Spv(A, I·A)`-spectral route and the `A₀`-ideal quantifier, or to point us at a
cleaner standard path to Spa quasi-compactness (e.g. Huber's spectral-space theorem directly).**

### 8.4. A hypothesis-hygiene worry we cannot fully settle ourselves

We have engineered the signatures so that the whole of (b) uses only "`A` strongly noetherian Tate".
One step makes us nervous: to feed the flatness lemmas we promote each section ring `O_X(V)` to
**strongly** noetherian, and one internal route does this from "`O_X(V)` noetherian + Tate". But
Wedhorn carefully keeps *noetherian* and *strongly noetherian* distinct (and "noetherian ⟹ strongly
noetherian" is false in general). The faithful source of `O_X(V)`'s strong-noetherianity is Example
6.38 (`A` strongly noetherian ⟹ `A⟨T/s⟩` strongly noetherian, via topological finite type + Remark
6.37(1), *or* via the `C/a` representation with `C = A⟨X⟩` noetherian *because `A` is strongly
noetherian*). **Q4 asks whether our promotion is sound or smuggles in the false implication.**
