# Reviewer reply — 2026-06-09 (gluing-leaf relativization fork)

## Executive verdict

Choose **Route R2**. This is exactly what Wedhorn is doing in the highlighted sentence. A rational
subset U ⊆ X = Spa(A,A⁺) is itself an affinoid adic spectrum: U ≅ Spa(B,B⁺), B = O_X(U). A rational
covering of U is therefore a rational covering of the whole space of the affinoid ring B. Correct
proof of acyclicity for a cover of a proper rational subset: replace A by B = O_X(U), apply the
whole-space theorem to Spa(B), transport back along Spa(B) ≃ U. This is the intended meaning, not a
workaround. R1 would duplicate machinery that should instead be obtained by instantiating the absolute
machinery at B. Only real requirement: the comparison Spa(O_X(U), O_X(U)⁺) ≃ U and the restriction
compatibility O_X(V) ≃ O_{Spa(O_X(U))}(V) for rational V ⊆ U. Wedhorn supplies exactly this in
**Proposition 8.2, Remark 8.4, and Proposition 8.16**.

## Q1 — Is Route R2 sound? YES.

Wedhorn **Prop 8.2**: for U = R(T/s) ⊆ Spa(A,A⁺), the map Spa(A⟨T/s⟩) → Spa(A) is a homeomorphism
onto U, inducing a bijection between rational subsets of Spa(A⟨T/s⟩) and rational subsets of Spa(A)
contained in U. **Remark 8.4**: if B = A⟨T/s⟩ and j : Spa(B) → Spa(A) has image U, then for any
rational V ⊆ U the natural map O_X(V) → O_{Spa(B)}(j⁻¹(V)) is an isomorphism of complete topological
rings. That is precisely the Čech-complex compatibility needed: every object in the Čech complex for a
rational cover of U is identified with the corresponding object over Spa(B), and restriction maps
match by functoriality/uniqueness in Prop 8.2(1). No bespoke relative IsGeneratedBy over A should be
built.

## Q2 — Correct B⁺.

B⁺ = O_X⁺(U) = {b ∈ B : v(b) ≤ 1 ∀ v ∈ U} = A⟨T/s⟩⁺ = integral closure in B of the image of A⁺
**together with T/s** (the "together with T/s" part is essential). Wedhorn **Prop 8.16**:
(O_X(U), O_X⁺(U)) = (A⟨T/s⟩, A⟨T/s⟩⁺). This matters because Spa(B,B⁺) ≅ U is an affinoid-PAIR
statement, not merely topological about B. For Čech acyclicity, once the pair is correct, the objects
are the complete topological rings O, and Remark 8.4 supplies the isos.

## Q3 — Completeness.

Route R2 needs only the ring instantiated (B = O_X(U)) complete — automatic by definition of the
presheaf value. For the global theorem over non-complete A, the usual reduction to Â is separately
needed. **If the project assumes [CompleteSpace A] (it does), no extra completeness issue remains.**

## Q4 — Avoid relativization? Not cleanly.

{R(T/t) ∩ U} IS precisely the relative cover of U generated by images of t in O_X(U). Keeping
everything over A forces reproving Prop 8.2 / Remark 8.4 piece by piece (intersections are rational
subsets of U; section rings agree with relative localizations over O_X(U); Laurent covers restricted
to U are Laurent covers generated by images f|_U; unit-generated covers over U are generated by units
in O_X(U), not necessarily A). A "lighter" no-base-change proof is an illusion. **Use R2.**
