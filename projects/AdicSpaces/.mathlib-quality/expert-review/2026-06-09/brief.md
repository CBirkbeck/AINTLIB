# Review brief — Wedhorn Thm 8.28(b): the gluing-leaf relativization fork

*Prepared 2026-06-09 for a senior expert in adic spaces / non-archimedean geometry. Self-contained: no repo access required.*

## 1. Goal

We are formalizing **Wedhorn's Theorem 8.28(b)**: *for an affinoid ring $(A, A^+)$ with $A$ a **strongly noetherian Tate** ring, the structure presheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ on the adic spectrum $X = \mathrm{Spa}(A)$ is a sheaf.* The proof reduces (via the embedding/gluing split of the sheaf condition) to four leaves; three are settled or in hand, and this brief concerns the deepest remaining one: the **gluing** leaf, i.e. $\mathcal{O}_X$-**acyclicity of every covering of $X$ by rational subsets**. We have built most of the acyclicity machinery and want guidance on the single architectural decision that gates its completion.

## 2. Background and setting

- $X = \mathrm{Spa}(A) = \{$ continuous valuations $v$ on $A$ with $v(a) \le 1\ \forall a \in A^+\}$, with its rational-subset topology. For a finite $T \subseteq A$ and $s \in A$ with $T \cdot A$ open, the **rational subset** is $R(T/s) = \{v : v(t) \le v(s) \neq 0\ \forall t \in T\}$.
- $\mathcal{O}_X(R(T/s)) = \widehat{A\langle T/s\rangle}$, the completed rational localization. The repo realizes this as `presheafValue D` for a "rational-localization datum" $D$ encoding $(T, s)$.
- A **rational covering** $\mathcal{C}$ of a rational subset $U = R(\mathcal{C}.\mathrm{base})$ is a finite family of rational subsets $\subseteq U$ covering it. $\mathcal{C}$ is **$\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic** if the augmented Čech complex of $\mathcal{O}_X$ for $\mathcal{C}$ is exact in all degrees.
- **Reference.** [Wedhorn] T. Wedhorn, *Adic Spaces* (arXiv:1910.05934v1), §8.2 (Thm 8.28, Lemmas 8.31–8.34), §A (Prop A.3, A.4), Cor 7.32, Lemma 7.54. [Hu3] R. Huber, *A generalization of formal schemes and rigid analytic varieties*, Math. Z. 217 (1994) — Wedhorn's Lemma 7.54 is [Hu3] Lemma 2.6.

## 3. Wedhorn's strategy for the gluing leaf (his §8.2, verbatim structure)

$\mathcal{O}_X$ is a sheaf $\Leftarrow$ **every rational covering is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic** (Prop A.4, basis stable under finite intersection). Then:

1. (Lemma 7.54 = [Hu3] 2.6) Every covering of $X$ refines to a **rational cover generated by $T$**: $U_t = R(T/t)_{t \in T}$ with $T \subseteq A$ generating the unit ideal of $A$. By Prop A.3(2), acyclicity is refinement-invariant, so it suffices to prove acyclicity for these (Lemma 8.34).
2. (Lemma 8.34) A rational cover generated by $T$ (with $T\cdot A = A$) is acyclic, via: **(i)** two-set "Laurent" covers $U_f = \{x(f)\le 1\} \cup \{x(f)\ge 1\}$ are acyclic (Lemma 8.33), and by A.3(3)-induction so are products $U_{f_1}\times\cdots\times U_{f_r}$; **(ii)** any $T$-generated cover admits a Laurent cover $V$ on whose pieces the cover restricts to a **unit-generated** one (using Cor 7.32 to find a dominating unit); **(iii)** unit-generated covers refine to Laurent covers; **(iv)** combine by A.3(1).
3. (Lemma 8.33) The two-set acyclicity is a $3\times3$ diagram chase using $\mathcal{O}_X(U_1) = A\langle\zeta\rangle/(f-\zeta)$, $\mathcal{O}_X(U_2) = A\langle\eta\rangle/(1-f\eta)$, $\mathcal{O}_X(U_1\cap U_2) = A\langle\zeta,\zeta^{-1}\rangle/(f-\zeta)$ (Examples 6.38/6.39).

**The crucial sentence (Wedhorn, mid-§8.2):** *"If $U$ is any rational subset, then $\mathcal{U}|_U$ is the rational cover of $U = \mathrm{Spa}\,\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ generated by the set of images of $t$ in $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$."* — i.e. the argument for a **proper** base $U \subsetneq X$ is run **over the ring $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$**, not over $A$.

## 4. What is built (established, machine-checked)

- The **abstract Čech engine** — Prop A.3(1)(2)(3) and the acyclicity-transfer/refinement lemmas — is formalized and **axiom-clean**.
- The two-set/Laurent cover constructions ($U_f$, products) and the **full assembly** "every rational covering is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic" $\to$ "sheaf" exist; given that assembly, the headline gluing leaf is a one-line consequence.
- **Just landed (this session):** $\mathcal{O}_X(U) = \widehat{A\langle T/s\rangle}$ is itself a **complete strongly noetherian Tate ring** (Wedhorn Example 6.38 propagation), proved axiom-clean.
- The deep analytic sub-lemmas — Lemma 8.33's diagram chase, Cor 7.32's dominating unit (a quasi-compactness argument), Lemma 7.54 — remain open, but are *in-project, faithful-to-Wedhorn* targets.

## 5. Where we're stuck — the relativization fork (the heart of this brief)

The repo encodes "$\mathcal{C}$ is the rational cover generated by $T$" as the predicate
$$\mathrm{IsGeneratedBy}(\mathcal{C}, T) \;:\equiv\; \big(\mathrm{span}_A(T) = A\big)\ \wedge\ \big(\mathcal{C}\text{'s pieces are exactly } R(T/t)_{t\in T}\big),$$
and Lemma 8.34 is stated as: $\mathrm{IsGeneratedBy}(\mathcal{C}, T) \Rightarrow \mathcal{C}$ is acyclic.

**The problem.** $\mathrm{span}_A(T) = A$ forces the pieces $R(T/t)$ to cover **all of $X = \mathrm{Spa}(A)$**. So $\mathrm{IsGeneratedBy}(\mathcal{C}, T)$ is **unsatisfiable when $\mathcal{C}.\mathrm{base} = U \subsetneq X$ is a proper rational subset** (the pieces cannot lie inside a proper base). But the sheaf condition (Prop A.4) needs acyclicity for coverings of **every** rational subset $U$, not just $X$. A previous attempt to paper over this with a single "$A$-level refinement for proper bases" lemma was found **provably false** (it forces $\mathrm{span}_A(S)=A$ — hence $\bigcup R(S/f) = X$ — *and* each $R(S/f) \subseteq U \subsetneq X$, a contradiction); it has been excised.

The faithful resolution is Wedhorn's own sentence in §3 above: for a proper base $U$, **relativize the entire chain over the ring $B := \mathcal{O}_X(U)$**. Concretely the cover $\mathcal{U}|_U$ is the rational cover of $\mathrm{Spa}(B)$ generated by the images of $T$ in $B$ (which generate $B$'s unit ideal since $A \to B$ is unital), and one runs Lemma 8.34 / 7.54 over $B$.

**Two ways to formalize this, and we want to pick the right one before committing multi-session effort:**

- **Route R1 (bespoke relative machinery).** Build a *relative* rational-cover / relative-`IsGeneratedBy` / relative-7.54 apparatus indexed over an arbitrary base $U$ and the ring $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$, mirroring the absolute one. Self-contained but a large new development, and it duplicates the absolute theory "one level up".

- **Route R2 (instantiate the absolute engine at $B := \mathcal{O}_X(U)$).** Since we *now* know $B = \mathcal{O}_X(U)$ is itself a complete strongly noetherian Tate ring, apply the **already-built absolute** acyclicity theorem **to the ring $B$** (for coverings of $\mathrm{Spa}(B)$, i.e. the whole space of $B$), then transport the conclusion back along a homeomorphism/identification $\mathrm{Spa}(B) \cong U$ under which rational coverings of $U$ inside $X$ correspond to rational coverings of $\mathrm{Spa}(B)$, and $\mathcal{O}_X|_U \cong \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Spa}(B)}$ as presheaves of rings. If this correspondence holds at the level of the Čech complexes, R2 collapses the deepest blocker into existing, partly-proven code — no new "relative" theory.

## 6. Open questions for the reviewer

**Q1 (the fork).** Is **Route R2** sound — i.e. is it legitimate to obtain acyclicity of a covering $\mathcal{C}$ of a proper rational subset $U \subseteq \mathrm{Spa}(A)$ by *instantiating the whole-space acyclicity theorem at the Tate ring $B = \mathcal{O}_X(U)$*, given the standard identification $\mathrm{Spa}(\mathcal{O}_X(U)) \cong U$ and $\mathcal{O}_X|_U \cong \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Spa}(B)}$? Or is there a genuine obstruction (e.g. the identification is only of underlying spaces / only for $A$ complete / fails to match the *integral* subrings $A^+$ vs $B^+$, or the Čech complexes do not correspond on the nose) that forces the bespoke Route R1?

**Q2 (the $A^+$ / integral structure).** The adic spectrum depends on the pair $(A, A^+)$. For $U = R(T/s)$, what is the correct $B^+ \subseteq B = \mathcal{O}_X(U)$ making $\mathrm{Spa}(B, B^+) \cong U$ as adic spaces (not just topological spaces), and does the structure presheaf genuinely restrict to it? Is "$B^+ = $ integral closure of the image of $A^+$ in $B$" the right choice, and is the resulting identification an iso of *presheaves of complete topological rings* (which is what our sheaf condition / Čech complex sees)?

**Q3 (completeness bookkeeping).** Wedhorn says "we may assume $A$ complete" at the top of the 8.28 proof. $B = \mathcal{O}_X(U)$ is by construction complete. Does Route R2 need $A$ itself complete for the $\mathrm{Spa}(B) \cong U$ identification, or only $B$ complete (which we have for free)? I.e. does relativizing dodge any completeness hypothesis that the absolute statement needed?

**Q4 (is the relativization even necessary?).** Is there a still-lighter path that avoids both R1 and R2 — e.g. proving acyclicity for proper-base covers *directly* from the whole-space case by intersecting the whole-space $T$-cover with $U$ (the cover $\{R(T/t) \cap U\}_t$), using only Prop A.3 and the already-proven whole-space acyclicity, without ever changing the base ring? Our concern is that $\{R(T/t)\cap U\}$ need not be "generated by $T$" in the $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ sense, so A.3(2)/refinement may not directly apply — but we may be missing a cleaner reduction.

**Q5 (prioritization sanity-check).** Independent of R1/R2: among the remaining in-project analytic leaves — Lemma 8.33's two-set diagram chase, Cor 7.32's dominating-unit (quasi-compactness), and Lemma 7.54 (= [Hu3] 2.6) — is any one of these the genuine "long pole" that we should de-risk first, or are they all routine-but-laborious given the completed Example 6.38 machinery?
