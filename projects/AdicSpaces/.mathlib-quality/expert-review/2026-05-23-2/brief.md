## 1. Goal (unchanged)

We are formalizing **Theorem 8.28(b) of Wedhorn**: *if A is a strongly
noetherian Tate ring, then the structure presheaf 𝒪_X on the adic spectrum
X = Spa(A, A⁺) is a sheaf of complete topological rings.* The target
`IsSheafy` value packages this together with the auxiliary topological-
embedding condition from Wedhorn Remark 8.20.

## 2. Setting (compact recap)

A Tate affinoid ring A = (A, A⁺) is an f-adic ring with a topologically
nilpotent unit, together with an open integrally-closed subring A⁺ of
integral (= power-bounded) elements. A *strongly noetherian* Tate ring is
one all of whose Tate-algebra extensions A⟨X_1, …, X_n⟩ are noetherian
(Wedhorn §6.7).

The adic spectrum X = Spa A = Spa(A, A⁺) is the set of continuous valuations
v on A with v(f) ≤ 1 for all f ∈ A⁺, with the topology generated by *rational
subsets* R(T/s) = {v : v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 for all t ∈ T}. Rational subsets
form a basis of quasi-compact opens (Wedhorn 7.35).

The structure presheaf 𝒪_X is defined on rational subsets by
𝒪_X(R(T/s)) := A⟨T/s⟩ (completed Tate localization), and extended to
arbitrary opens by projective limit. The target IsSheafy bundle encodes
sheafiness in the topological-embedding form of Wedhorn Remark 8.20.

The project's terminology uses several non-standard typeclasses worth flagging:

- **`PlusSubring A`** — an abstract typeclass providing A⁺, not necessarily
  aligned with the canonical maximal A° of Wedhorn Rem 7.15(1). Several of
  the B2s below trace to this abstract-vs-canonical gap.
- **`presheafValue D`** — the completion A⟨T/s⟩ for a rational locality
  datum D = (T, s) with extra book-keeping. Several B2s trace to typeclasses
  that A inherits not being automatically derived for `presheafValue D`.
- **`HasLocLiftPowerBounded`** — a project-specific typeclass packaging
  certain "rational-localization images are power-bounded" facts; central
  to the W3 transport chain.
- **`LaurentNormalized`** — a Laurent-cover-shape constraint used by the
  sorry-free variants of the main theorem; restricts to Laurent covers
  (not all rational covers).
- **`RationalCovering A`** — a finite covering of `Spa A` by rational
  subsets sitting over a base rational subset, plus coverage data.

## 4. What round-1's coordinated audit revealed (the new picture)

The round-1 letter advised that the cover-side compactness extraction
(C2 / `exists_per_D_finite_cover_of_localBasisHyp`) should be strengthened
to apply to the refined opens R(insert f T/s) ∩ R({f}/f), so that each
extracted witness automatically satisfies v(f) ≠ 0. We accepted this and
have applied the **predicate update** (the `LocalBasisHyp` definition now
includes the non-vanishing clause); the build is clean.

The next step was supposed to be straightforward: prove the strengthened
`LocalBasisHyp` and then the L4 replacement falls out in ~30 lines. Before
starting that proof, the project's own decomposition was re-audited
lemma-by-lemma, with the surrounding code read in detail and Wedhorn
cross-referenced for each citation. The audit turned up the following
unexpected picture:

- **Nine lemmas are false as stated** (and have been logged as B2). Most
  are typeclass-deficient: hypotheses are missing that the proof body
  cannot survive without (the most common are `[CompleteSpace A]` and an
  alignment between the abstract `PlusSubring` and the canonical `A°`).
- **Three more are suspect** on the same typeclass-deficiency pattern.
- **Six obligations are blocked on infrastructure that does not yet
  exist**: a "propagation API batch" for `presheafValue D` typeclasses
  (none of `[IsHuberRing (presheafValue D)]`, `[HasLocLiftPowerBounded
  (presheafValue D)]`, `[IsStronglyNoetherian (presheafValue D)]`, etc.
  are derivable from the corresponding facts on A), and a substantial
  Spa.comap framework (~500 LOC sub-development on its own).
- **Two obligations are structural** — the relevant theorem is proved
  downstream, but an import cycle blocks an in-place close. The earlier
  "F12 move" plan to fix this turns out to be entangled with the L-atoms
  rather than being a standalone reorganization.
- **Four obligations are off the main critical path** (an off-chain
  Stacks 0316 development, a categorical packaging of the sheaf data, the
  no-hArch Spv(A,I)-spectrality leaf, and a no-Noetherian Artin-Rees
  witness).
- **Two obligations are intentional dead code** — explicit "do not pick
  this up; route elsewhere" markers.
- **Zero obligations are truly atomic leaves** that one could close today
  without prerequisites.

Realistic remaining effort: ~1500-2000 lines of coordinated multi-file
work, versus the 700-900 the earlier non-adversarial passes suggested.

The remainder of this brief lays out the 26 critical-path / near-critical-
path obligations one by one (the 35 total includes 9 off-path Presheaf
sorries that are listed briefly at the end), classifies each, and asks
guidance on the strategic questions in §7.

`presheafValue` instances.

## 6. The 35 sorries, one by one (the heart of this brief)

Each item below is a single proof obligation that is currently open. We
give the mathematical statement (in English; no Lean syntax), the
project's claimed source, and our verdict. The verdicts:

- **B2** — the statement is false as written; logged in the project's B2
  tracker with the counterexample. Cannot be proved; must be replaced.
- **SUSPECT** — likely B2 for the same kind of reason but not yet
  conclusively verified.
- **BLOCKED** — the statement may be true, but the proof requires
  infrastructure (typeclass propagation, framework, etc.) that does not
  yet exist and is itself a sub-development.
- **STRUCTURAL** — provable downstream but currently behind an import
  cycle; a file relocation is needed.
- **OFF-PATH** — open obligation but not on the main critical path to
  `IsSheafy_ofStronglyNoetherianTate`.
- **DEAD** — intentional sorry on a deprecated approach; should be
  deleted with its surrounding theorem.

### 6.1 The IsSheafy / structure-presheaf layer (8 obligations)

(All in the project's `StructureSheaf` module — the layer that packages the
`IsSheafy` target and the structure presheaf of complete topological rings.)

**Obligation 6.1.1 (`structurePresheaf_isSheaf`).** *Let A be an f-adic
ring with a designated ring of integral elements A⁺. Then the structure
presheaf 𝒪_X on Spa(A, A⁺), regarded as a presheaf with values in the
category of complete topological rings, is a sheaf (equivalently, for
every complete topological ring T, U ↦ Hom(T, 𝒪_X(U)) is a sheaf of sets,
which by Wedhorn Rem 8.20 is the topological-embedding criterion).*
**Verdict: OFF-PATH (categorical packaging).** The standard mathlib
`isSheaf_iff_isSheaf_comp` lemma does not apply since the forgetful
functor from complete topological rings to types does not reflect
isomorphisms; the Hom-by-Hom route is the project's plan. Independent
~100-200 line categorical-glue development; not on the immediate
critical path for the `IsSheafy` value itself.

**Obligation 6.1.2 (`_aux_nonOpen_hSpa_principalPair_A₀_completeSpace`).**
*Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate ring (T₂, nonarchimedean). Let A₀
denote the ring of definition of the canonical principal pair of A. Then
A₀ is complete as a uniform subspace (with the subspace uniformity from
A's canonical right-uniform-additive-group structure).*
**Verdict: B2.** The hypothesis bundle does **not** include
`[CompleteSpace A]`. The discharge route is Wedhorn Cor 5.50 (A₀ closed
in A under T₂ + complete) followed by the closed-subset-of-complete-is-
complete pattern. A non-complete Tate ring has A₀ ⊆ A inheriting
incompleteness via the subspace uniformity. The companion theorem
`_aux_nonOpen_hSpa_principalPair_isAdicComplete` compiles only because
this sorry absorbs the obligation. **Same defect appears at
`principalPair_A₀_completeSpace_of_stronglyNoetherianTate` (mirror in the
TateAcyclicityResiduals layer).** Fix: add `[CompleteSpace A]` to the
hypothesis bundle; this is natural for the Wedhorn 7.45 setting.

**Obligation 6.1.3 (`_aux_nonOpen_hSpa_Aplus_le_principalPair_A₀`).**
*Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate ring. Then the project's A⁺ (the
`PlusSubring`) is contained, as a set, in the ring of definition A₀ of
the canonical principal pair.*
**Verdict: B2.** Wedhorn Rem 7.15(1) gives the chain A⁺ ⊆ A° ⊆ A₀ when
A⁺ is aligned with A° (the canonical largest power-bounded subring), but
the project's `PlusSubring` typeclass is **abstract**: an arbitrary open
integrally-closed subring. There is no guarantee A⁺ aligns with A°.
The docstring explicitly admits this: "*generally not free without an
explicit alignment hypothesis between A⁺ and P.A₀*". **Same defect at
`aplus_le_A₀_of_stronglyNoetherianTate_principal` (TateAcyclicityResiduals
mirror).** Fix: add a `[CompatiblePlusSubring A]` instance (or analogous
alignment) to the hypothesis bundle, or restate using `A⁺ = A°` directly.

**Obligation 6.1.4 (`productRestrictionSub_isInducing_tate`, the "Gap B"
headline).** *Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate ring (T₂, noetherian,
nonarchimedean). For any rational covering C of Spa A, the canonical map
from 𝒪_X(Spa A) to the sub-product of the rational-piece sections
(respecting the equaliser of overlaps) is a topological inducing.*
**Verdict: BLOCKED + SUSPECT.** This is the topological side of
Wedhorn 8.28(b) / Rem 8.20. The intended proof composes the tree-existence
infrastructure (an open obligation downstream) with the tree-induction
wrapper. But the tree-induction wrapper's atomic step (T286) carries
~17 typeclass hypotheses including `[IsHuberRing (presheafValue C.base)]`,
`[HasLocLiftPowerBounded (presheafValue C.base)]`, `[CompleteSpace
(presheafValue C.base)]`, and similar — none of which are derivable from
the standing context without the propagation API batch (see §6.6
"Infrastructure obligations"). Even with the tree, the current
signature cannot invoke T286.

**Obligation 6.1.5 (`_aux_noeth_A0_generic_of_stronglyNoetherianTate`).**
*Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate ring. Then for every pair of
definition (A₀, I) of A, the ring A₀ is noetherian.*
**Verdict: B2.** This is FALSE in general: strongly noetherian Tate does
**not** imply every ring of definition is noetherian. Only the canonical
(maximal) ring of definition is noetherian; an arbitrary ring of
definition need not be (Nagata-style examples: if A = A₀[1/s] with A₀
non-noetherian and A is the strongly-noetherian Tate completion, then
A₀ is not noetherian). The project's own internal audit chain has the
candidate discharge route deleted ("L5.2.2 deleted Session 21 per user
decision; the original intent 'A noeth ⇒ A₀ noeth for principal pair via
A = A₀[1/s] descent' is NOT in Wedhorn and the localization-descent
direction is false in general"). The established "Path α" decision is
to take `[IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]` as a parameter; this lemma should be
**deleted** and its downstream consumers updated accordingly.

**Obligation 6.1.6 (`cor_8_32_clean_sub_with_P`).** *Let A be a strongly
noetherian Tate ring with a designated pair of definition (P) satisfying
`[IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]`. For any rational covering C of Spa A, the
product restriction map from 𝒪_X(Spa A) into ∏_D 𝒪_X(U_D) is faithfully
flat (over 𝒪_X(Spa A) via the restriction-induced algebra structure).*
**Verdict: STRUCTURAL.** Wedhorn Cor 8.32, P-parameterized form. The
substantive proof exists in the project's `AuditCleanWrappers` module
(`cor_8_32_clean_proof`), but lives in a file that imports the
StructureSheaf module. The current sorry is an upstream placeholder that
StructureSheaf consumers can reach; the actual proof is downstream. Fix:
either move the lemma downstream (relocating its consumers), or break
the import cycle. This is the same structural-issue class as the F12
relocation (see §6.4).

**Obligation 6.1.7 (`_sub_lemma_L5_1_3_inductive_step`).** *Inductive step
in the Stacks 0316 chain (AdicCompletion of a noetherian ring is
noetherian), no-Noetherian-source-pair variant.* **Verdict: OFF-PATH.**
The Stacks 0316 chain is bypassed by the `[IsStronglyNoetherian A]`
hypothesis throughout the IsSheafy target chain; not needed for our
present goal.

**Obligation 6.1.8 (`Spa_presheafValue_eq_rationalOpen`).** *For a
rational locality datum D = (T, s) of A, the adic spectrum of the
completed Tate localization 𝒪_X(D) is canonically homeomorphic to the
rational subset R(T, s) ⊆ Spa A. The forward map is the contravariant
Spa-comap along A → 𝒪_X(D); the reverse map extends a valuation v on A
satisfying the rational inequalities to a valuation on 𝒪_X(D) via the
universal property of the localization, then through the completion.*
**Verdict: BLOCKED on a ~500-line sub-development.** This is Wedhorn 8.2(2)
(the homeomorphism Spa(A⟨T/s⟩) ≃ R(T/s)), foundational for the C3
Spa.comap framework. The project has a file dedicated to it, but the
file is a **planning skeleton**: every reverse-direction sub-lemma
circularly delegates back to the headline, sub-lemma 1 (valuation
extends to localization, ~80 LOC) and sub-lemma 2 (valuation extends
through completion, ~120 LOC) are explicitly unformalized per the
docstring, and the file's signature is missing required typeclasses
(`[UniformSpace A]`, `[IsUniformAddGroup A]`, `[CompleteSpace A]`,
`[HasLocLiftPowerBounded A]`, `[CompatiblePlusSubring A]`). Total residual
work: ~500 LOC including the typeclass alignment, the image identification
(~150 LOC), the IsHuberRing instance on 𝒪_X(D) (~50 LOC), and the
assembly (~100 LOC). This is the single largest sub-development gating
the W3 chain and task #38 (HasLocLiftPowerBounded propagation).

### 6.2 The Lane C / W1 layer (5 obligations: L1–L5)

(The "local-basis" sub-chain that supplies the standard-cover refinement
of any rational cover by witnesses with `v(f) ≠ 0`.)

**L1 (`localBasisHyp_of_strongly_noetherian`, strengthened predicate).**
*Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate domain (T₂, nonarchimedean). For
every rational covering C, every cover piece E, and every valuation v in
R(E.T, E.s), there exists f ∈ A such that v ∈ R(insert f base.T, base.s)
∩ R({f}/f) and R(insert f base.T, base.s) ⊆ R(E.T, E.s).* (The strengthened
form includes the non-vanishing clause R({f}/f) = {v : v(f) ≠ 0} as

**Verdict: SUSPECT.** The intended proof is Hübner Lemma 3.8 / Zavyalov
§2.3 — the local-basis theorem for strongly-noetherian Tate. Wedhorn's
own Lemma 8.34 requires "complete strongly noetherian Tate", and Hübner's
proof likely also assumes completeness. The current hypothesis bundle
omits `[CompleteSpace A]`. Pending verification: this is probably another
"add `[CompleteSpace A]`" B2.

**L2 (`principalPair_A₀_completeSpace_of_stronglyNoetherianTate`).**
Mirror of obligation 6.1.2; same B2 verdict; same fix.

**L3 (`aplus_le_A₀_of_stronglyNoetherianTate_principal`).**
Mirror of obligation 6.1.3; same B2 verdict; same fix.

**L4 (`strengthened_cover_of_basic_cover`).** *Given a per-piece witness
family `mk_S_D` and the bare-cover existential (∀ D ∀ v ∈ R(D), ∃ f ∈
mk_S_D D, v ∈ R(insert f base.T, base.s)), strengthen the witness to
additionally satisfy v(f) ≠ 0.* **Verdict: B2 (this was the round-1
finding).** Fix is **replacement** by a strengthened source extraction:
re-run compactness on the refined opens R(insert f base.T, base.s) ∩
R({f}/f). The replacement plan has been adopted; the strengthened

**L5 (`outside_rescue_of_per_D_cover`).** *Given the per-piece data,

## 7. Where we're stuck (the strategic picture)

The audit's bottom line is that the IsSheafy target depends on a tower of
sub-developments that the prior planning had collapsed into one or two
"leaves":

**(a) The typeclass-deficiency epidemic.** Five of the nine B2s (and the
three SUSPECTs) are typeclass-deficient in the same pattern: the
hypothesis bundle is missing typeclasses that the proof body cannot
survive without — most commonly `[CompleteSpace A]`, an alignment
between the abstract `PlusSubring` and the canonical `A°`, and the
typeclasses that `presheafValue` should inherit from A. The previous
"Path α" decision handled exactly one such defect (taking
`[IsNoetherianRing P.A₀]` parametrically). The question is whether
to generalize Path α into a coordinated parametric-propagation pattern.

**(b) The `presheafValue` propagation API gap.** Six of the open
obligations are blocked on the lack of API lemmas that would establish
typeclasses on `presheafValue D` from the corresponding facts on A. The
missing lemmas include `presheafValue_isStronglyNoetherian`,
`presheafValue_sigmaCompact`, `presheafValue_completeSpace`,
`presheafValue_isHuberRing` (currently not an instance), and
`presheafValue_HasLocLiftPowerBounded`. Without these, T286 cannot be
applied to relative `presheafValue` levels, blocking the W2 and W3
chains.

**(c) The Spa.comap framework.** The `Spa_presheafValue_eq_rationalOpen`
identification (Wedhorn 8.2(2): Spa(A⟨T/s⟩) ≃ R(T/s)) is the
foundational substrate for the `HasLocLiftPowerBounded` propagation and
the W3 transport. The project has a file dedicated to it that is a
**planning skeleton with circular delegations**; the substantive
content (~500 LOC: valuation extension to localization, valuation
extension through completion, image identification, typeclass instance
on the completion) is genuinely unformalized.

**(d) The F12 reorganization is entangled.** The F12 plan (moving
`tateAcyclicity` downstream to break an import cycle) was assumed to be a
standalone structural reorganization. The audit reveals the
`_via_normalizedLaurent` variants in the downstream target file carry
`[LaurentNormalized C.base]` and cannot drop-in replace the general
`tateAcyclicity` — so F12 doesn't close the sorry, it just moves it.

**(e) Several leaves were named that don't exist.** The previous
decomposition into "15 critical leaves" cited specific lemma names that
turn out either not to exist in the codebase at the named locations
(line numbers had drifted by hundreds) or to be more compositional than
"atomic leaves" (their bodies use existing sub-lemmas that the
decomposition didn't surface).

The realistic effort to close the IsSheafy target as currently scoped is
~1500-2000 lines of coordinated multi-file work, primarily:
