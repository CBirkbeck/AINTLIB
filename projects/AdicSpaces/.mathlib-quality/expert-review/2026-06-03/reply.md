## Executive verdict

The current route is mathematically sound. The project has correctly moved the hard analytic input from Wedhorn 6.16/6.17/6.18 into a completed open-mapping/closed-ideal package, and the remaining serious geometric input is now the Spa-completion comparison around Wedhorn Proposition 7.48 / Huber Prop. 3.9.

I would make one adjustment to the framing: for Corollary 8.32 you probably do **not** need to formalize all of Proposition 7.48 as a large monolithic theorem first. The critical theorem you need is the **relative Spa point lifting/uniqueness statement** for rational localizations:

    If x ∈ Spa(O(D)) and its image in Spa(A) lies in D' ⊆ D,
    then x uniquely lifts to Spa(O(D')).

This is essentially the relative form of `Spa(O(D)) ≃ rationalOpen(D)`, so it is not mathematically weaker by much, but it is a sharper Lean target for Corollary 8.32.

The injectivity half of the completion comparison should be directly provable from density plus continuity of valuations. You should not need to reproduce all of Huber's machinery if the project already has the extension/surjectivity half.

## Q1 — Is the Corollary 8.32 route via Prop. 7.48 the right one?

Yes. The route (Prop. 8.30 flat restrictions + maximal-ideal/cover bridge ⇒ Cor. 8.32 faithful flatness) is right.

Maximal-ideal bridge: (1) m ⊂ O_X(U) maximal. (2) Use the affinoid Nullstellensatz / Wedhorn 7.51–7.52 to get x ∈ Spa(O_X(U)) whose support is m or contains m. (3) View x as a point of U ⊆ Spa(A). (4) Since (U_i) covers U, choose i with x ∈ U_i. (5) Lift x to a point of Spa(O_X(U_i)). (6) Conclude m·O_X(U_i) is proper.

The subtle step is step 5 — needs the relative Spa comparison Spa(O_X(U_i)) ≃ U_i. So a weaker theorem than full Prop. 7.48 suffices:

    spa_lift_along_rational_restriction :
      x ∈ Spa(O(D)) → image(x) ∈ rationalOpen(D') →
      ∃! y ∈ Spa(O(D')), y restricts to x

I do not see a faithful route to Corollary 8.32 that avoids this geometric bridge entirely. The cover hypothesis is a statement about Spa; faithful flatness is about maximal ideals of completed rings. Some form of Nullstellensatz + Spa comparison is exactly what translates between them.

## Q2 — Clean proof of `Spa Â ≅ Spa A`

The injectivity half should be substantially more elementary than the full Huber apparatus. A useful general lemma:

    Let R → S have dense image, and let v,w be continuous valuations on S.
    If v and w agree on R, then v = w.

Proof is the standard density-and-strict-triangle argument. For x ∈ S, choose a ∈ R close to x. If v(x) ≠ 0, choose a so the error e = x − a has value strictly smaller than v(x) for v, and small enough for w relative to the same comparison value. Then v(a) = v(x), w(a) = v(a), w(e) < w(a), so w(x) = w(a) = v(x). A similar contradiction handles the case one valuation vanishes on x and the other does not.

Needs only: density of A in Â, continuity of valuations, the nonarchimedean strict triangle rule, and enough comparison infrastructure for valuation equivalence classes. No field-valued valuation extension.

Recommendation: do not start by formalizing all of Hu2 §3. First prove: continuous valuations on a Hausdorff completion are determined by the dense subring. Together with the already-proved extension/surjectivity half, this gives the bijective part of Prop. 7.48. The homeomorphism part follows because the Spa topology is generated by rational inequalities, preserved under pullback along the completion map.

## Q3 — Alternative route to Cor 8.32 without Spa-completion?

I would not pursue a purely algebraic replacement as the main route. For special standard/Laurent covers one can prove faithful flatness directly from explicit Tate-algebra presentations, but Cor 8.32 is for arbitrary finite rational covers, and the cover hypothesis is valuation-geometric. Wedhorn's "immediate" = flatness is Prop. 8.30; faithfulness because every maximal ideal gives a Spa point, the point lies in some cover member, hence the corresponding localization factor is proper. That uses 7.51/7.52 and the Spa comparison for completed rational localizations. A purely algebraic Čech computation would likely reprove a stronger acyclicity and still need a theorem connecting the cover condition to algebraic nonvanishing.

## Q4 — Packaging as embedding + gluing, and deriving embedding by OMT

Yes, the packaging is right (Remark 8.20). It is legitimate to derive the topological embedding a posteriori: (1) algebraic acyclicity gives O(U) ≅ equalizer E inside the product; (2) E is closed in a finite product of complete rings, hence complete; (3) O(U) → E is continuous and bijective; (4) Tate OMT ⇒ open; (5) hence homeomorphism; (6) compose with the closed inclusion E ↪ product. No circularity, as long as the algebraic exactness in step 1 was proved without assuming topological embedding — which it is (Cor 8.32 + Lemmas 8.33/8.34; OMT independent). Sound.

## Q5 — Open mapping / 6.16–6.18 analysis inputs

The Henkel/Bourbaki route is right for this generality. Classical σ-compact/locally-compact Banach proofs are too restrictive; the C_p / C((t)) tests are the right sanity checks. Henkel's "zero sequence of units" theorem is designed for exactly this (the Tate condition). One caution: state the theorem for the exact category used later — complete Hausdorff nonarchimedean modules over a Tate ring, with the correct first-countability assumptions — and do not accidentally prove only a σ-compact or normed-field version.
