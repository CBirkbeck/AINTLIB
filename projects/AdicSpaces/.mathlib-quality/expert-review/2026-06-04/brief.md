## 1. Goal

We are formalising **Wedhorn, *Adic Spaces*, Theorem 8.28(b)**: *if $A$ is a strongly
noetherian Tate ring, then the structure presheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ on $X = \mathrm{Spa}(A,A^+)$
is a sheaf* (equivalently, $A$ is *sheafy*). The project target is a fully checked,
axiom-clean proof (only `propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`).

The immediate blocker — and the focus of the questions in §9 — is **Lemma 7.54**, a
covering-refinement statement that Wedhorn proves by citing **Huber [Hu3], Lemma 2.6**.
Everything else on the critical path is either done or is "ordinary" formalisation work;
7.54 is the one input where we do not yet know the cleanest route.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

Throughout, $A$ is a complete **Tate ring** (an f-adic ring with a topologically nilpotent
unit $\varpi$), $A^+ \subseteq A$ an open integrally closed subring contained in the
power-bounded elements, and $X = \mathrm{Spa}(A,A^+)$ the adic spectrum: equivalence classes
of continuous valuations $v$ on $A$ with $v(a) \le 1$ for $a \in A^+$. $X$ is a **spectral
space** and is **quasi-compact**.

For a finite subset $T \subseteq A$ and $s \in A$, the **rational subset**
$R(T/s) = \{\,v \in X : v(t) \le v(s) \ne 0 \ \ \forall t \in T\,\}$.
The structure presheaf assigns to $U = R(T/s)$ the completed localisation
$\mathcal{O}_X(U) = \widehat{A\langle T/s\rangle}$ (Tate's $A\langle T/s\rangle$). "$A$ is
sheafy" means $\mathcal{O}_X$ is a sheaf on rational subsets (equivalently, by Wedhorn
Prop. A.4, every covering of $X$ by rational subsets is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic).

$A$ is **strongly noetherian** if $A\langle X_1,\dots,X_n\rangle$ is noetherian for all $n$.
(This is the hypothesis of 8.28(b); complete fields, $\mathbb{C}_p$, and t.f.t.-algebras /
quotients over them are examples. We are careful throughout *not* to assume a noetherian ring
of definition $A_0$ — strictly stronger and false for $\mathbb{C}_p$.)

We write $\mathcal{U}_f = \{R(f/1), R(1/f)\}$ for the **standard 2-element (Laurent) cover**
of $X$ at $f$, and a **Laurent cover generated by $f_1,\dots,f_r$** for the iterated product
$\mathcal{U}_{f_1}\times\cdots\times\mathcal{U}_{f_r}$ (Wedhorn p. 84); this equals the
rational cover generated by $T = \{\prod_{j\in J} f_j : J \subseteq \{1,\dots,r\}\}$.

## 3. Strategy

Wedhorn's proof of 8.28(b) (for $A$ complete strongly-noetherian Tate) has two halves,
mirrored by our formalisation:

1. **Embedding (injectivity + inducing), via Cor 8.32.** For a rational cover, the product
   restriction $\mathcal{O}_X(U) \to \prod_i \mathcal{O}_X(U_i)$ is injective and a
   topological embedding (from **Prop 8.30** flatness + a maximal-ideals criterion).
   **Fully formalised, axiom-clean** (including the Prop 7.48 input).

2. **Gluing (acyclicity), via Lemma 8.34.** By Prop A.4 it suffices to show every open
   rational cover is $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic; **by Lemma 7.54 + Prop A.3(2)** it suffices to
   treat covers *generated by a finite $T$ with $T\cdot A = A$* (Lemma 8.34). Lemma 8.34's
   own proof (steps (i)–(iv)) reduces $T$-generated covers to **Laurent covers** and the
   2-element **Lemma 8.33**, via **Prop A.3(1)(3)** and **Cor 7.32**.

The reduction in step 2 — "every open cover refines to a $T$-generated cover" — **is exactly
Lemma 7.54**. It sits at the very top of the gluing half: without it, Lemma 8.34 only proves
acyclicity for the special $T$-generated covers, not for arbitrary ones.

## 4. Definitions

**Definition 4.1 (rational subset).** For finite $T\subseteq A$, $s\in A$:
$R(T/s) = \{v\in X : v(t)\le v(s)\ \forall t\in T,\ v(s)\ne 0\}$. Adjoining $s$ to $T$ does
not change $R(T/s)$.

**Definition 4.2 (rational cover generated by $T$).** For finite $T\subseteq A$ with
$T\cdot A = A$, the **rational cover generated by $T$** is $(R(T/t))_{t\in T}$. (Cor 7.53
below: when $T\cdot A = A$ these cover $X$.)

**Definition 4.3 (Laurent cover, relativised).** For a base $D_0 = R(T_0/s_0)$ and $f\in A$,
the relative 2-element cover $\mathcal{U}_f|_{D_0}$ has the "plus" piece
$R((T_0\cup\{f\})/s_0)$ and a complementary "minus" piece (numerator/denominator built from
$T_0,s_0,f$, denominator $s_0 f$); these cover $D_0$ by the trichotomy $v(f)\le v(s_0)$ or
$v(f)\ge v(s_0)$. The **Laurent cover of $D_0$ generated by $f_1,\dots,f_r$** is the iterated
plus/minus refinement; on $D_0 = X$ it reduces to the absolute
$\mathcal{U}_{f_1}\times\cdots\times\mathcal{U}_{f_r}$. *(We recently rebuilt this notion to
be relative — see §5.4 — faithful to Wedhorn's "$\mathcal{V}|_U$ is the Laurent cover
generated by $f_i|_U$", p. 84.)*

**Definition 4.4 (degree-0 $\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclicity).** A finite rational cover of base $B$
with pieces $(D_i)$ is **$\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic** if (separation) any $x\in\mathcal{O}_X(B)$
restricting to $0$ on every $D_i$ is $0$, and (gluing) every family $(f_i)$,
$f_i\in\mathcal{O}_X(D_i)$, agreeing on all common refinements, comes from a (unique)
$x\in\mathcal{O}_X(B)$.

## 5. Established results (formalised, axiom-clean unless noted)

**Theorem 5.1 (embedding / Cor 8.32).** *For $A$ complete strongly-noetherian Tate and a
rational cover, the product restriction map is injective and inducing; the maximal-ideals
criterion holds.* Fully formalised. Depends on Prop 8.30 (flatness) and Prop 7.48.

**Theorem 5.2 (Prop 7.48 = [Hu2] 3.9, the part needed).** The relevant injectivity / unique
lifting along the completion map for structure-sheaf points. **Formalised axiom-clean** via
the density lemma (continuous valuations agreeing on a dense subring are equal).

**Corollary 5.3 (Cor 7.53).** *Let $A$ be complete affinoid, $T\subseteq A$ finite. Then
$T\cdot A = A$ iff for all $x\in\mathrm{Spa}\,A$ there is $t\in T$ with $|t(x)|\ne 0$; and
then $(R(T/t))_{t\in T}$ covers $\mathrm{Spa}\,A$.* **Formalised axiom-clean.**

*Sketch.* If $T\cdot A\subseteq\mathfrak m$ maximal, Prop 7.51 (affinoid Nullstellensatz: a
maximal ideal is the support of some $x$) gives a point with all $|t(x)|=0$. Conversely
$\sum a_i t_i = 1$ plus a common zero give $1\le\max|t_i(x)||a_i(x)|=0$. For the covering:
the $t$ maximising $|t(x)|$ puts $x\in R(T/t)$. ∎

**Theorem 5.4 (Laurent machinery, relativised — formalised this session, axiom-clean).** We
redefined the Laurent cover relative to its base (Def 4.3) so one notion serves both the
whole-space case and the restriction $\mathcal{V}|_U$. Established: leaves are contained in
and cover the base; single-generator case = the standard 2-element cover; **product
decomposition** $\mathcal{U}_{f::gs}=\mathcal{U}_f\times\mathcal{V}_{gs}$; the **relative base
case** (trivial cover $\{B\}$ of $B$ is acyclic — restriction $\mathcal{O}_X(B)\to
\mathcal{O}_X(B)$ is the identity); and the **dominating-unit construction** (Wedhorn
8.34(ii): from Cor 7.32 a unit $s$ and the Laurent cover generated by $s^{-1}f_i$).

**Theorem 5.5 (Lemma 8.33, 2-element case — partially formalised).** *$\mathcal{U}_f$ is
$\mathcal{O}_X$-acyclic.* Separation from (5.1). Gluing is the $3\times 3$ chase using the
Example 6.38/6.39 presentations $\mathcal{O}_X(R(f/1))=A\langle\zeta\rangle/(f-\zeta)$,
$\mathcal{O}_X(R(1/f))=A\langle\eta\rangle/(1-f\eta)$,
$\mathcal{O}_X(R(f/1)\cap R(1/f))=A\langle\zeta,\zeta^{-1}\rangle/(f-\zeta)$, with
$\lambda(g,h)=g(\zeta)-h(\zeta^{-1})$ surjective and $\operatorname{im}\iota=\ker\lambda$.
*In progress* (purely additive; no domain hypothesis).

## 8. Where we are stuck

### 8.1 (the heart) Lemma 7.54 — refinement to a $T$-generated cover

**Statement (Wedhorn 7.54).** *Let $A$ be a complete affinoid ring and $(V_j)_{j\in J}$ an
open covering of $X = \mathrm{Spa}\,A$. Then there exist $f_0,\dots,f_n\in A$ generating $A$
as an ideal such that for each $i$, the rational subset $R(f_0,\dots,f_n / f_i)$ is contained
in some $V_j$.* (By Cor 7.53 these $R(\cdot/f_i)$ then cover $X$.)

**Wedhorn's proof is one line: "[Hu3] Lemma 2.6."** We have no self-contained proof, and this
single statement gates the whole gluing half: it is what lets Lemma 8.34's "$T$-generated"
special case imply acyclicity of *arbitrary* covers.

**What we have in the repository (axiom-clean):**
- $X = \mathrm{Spa}\,A$ is **quasi-compact** and spectral (any open cover has a finite
  subcover; rational subsets form a basis of quasi-compact opens).
- **Cor 7.53** (5.3): the *easy* direction — a finite ideal-generating $T$ with no common
  zero yields a covering $(R(T/t))_t$, via the affinoid Nullstellensatz **Prop 7.51** and the
  "max-dominates" point $t_x = \arg\max_t|t(x)|$.

**The gap.** Cor 7.53 goes *from* an ideal-generating $T$ *to* a covering. Lemma 7.54 is the
**converse with refinement**: from an **arbitrary** cover $(V_j)$, **produce** a single finite
ideal-generating $T = \{f_0,\dots,f_n\}$ whose cover $(R(T/f_i))$ **refines** $(V_j)$.
Concretely: quasi-compactness gives a finite subcover by rational subsets $R(S_k/s_k)$,
$k=1,\dots,m$; then one must combine the $(S_k,s_k)$ into one tuple $(f_0,\dots,f_n)$ whose
pieces each land inside some $R(S_k/s_k)$. This "common refinement of finitely many rational
subsets is itself a $T$-generated rational cover" is the content of [Hu3] 2.6, and is what we
cannot yet reconstruct cleanly. (We attempted "intersect the $R(S_k/s_k)$ and re-express the
intersection lattice as a single $T$-cover," but the bookkeeping — which products of
$S_k\cup\{s_k\}$ entries form $T$, why the pieces refine, why $T$ generates the unit ideal —
is exactly the unproven part.) By analogy with Prop 7.48, we suspect a clean route exists,
perhaps bypassing the general statement.

## 9. Questions for the reviewer (all centred on Lemma 7.54)

**Q1 (elementary route).** Is there a self-contained proof of Lemma 7.54 from what we already
have — quasi-compactness of $\mathrm{Spa}\,A$, Cor 7.53, and an *explicit* combinatorial
refinement of finitely many rational subsets — that **avoids the full apparatus of [Hu3]
Lemma 2.6**? If so, what is the cleanest such argument: how does one build the single tuple
$(f_0,\dots,f_n)$ from a finite subcover $(R(S_k/s_k))_k$, and why do the resulting pieces
refine the $R(S_k/s_k)$ and generate the unit ideal? (This is the analogue of the
density-lemma de-risk that worked for Prop 7.48.)

**Q2 (avoid 7.54 entirely).** Can Wedhorn 8.28(b)'s reduction — "every open rational cover is
acyclic $\Leftarrow$ every $T$-generated cover is acyclic" — be restructured so 7.54 is not
needed? E.g. is there a different cofinal family of covers (Laurent covers? covers generated
by units?) that is (a) easier to reach from an arbitrary cover by elementary means and (b)
still handled by Lemma 8.34? We already have the Laurent layer relativised and acyclic — could
the refinement be phrased directly in terms of Laurent covers?

**Q3 ([Hu3] 2.6 skeleton).** If no shortcut exists, what is the precise statement and proof
**skeleton** of [Hu3] Lemma 2.6 — the steps, the auxiliary facts it invokes, and which of
those are themselves elementary? We would formalise it directly, but want to mirror Huber's
actual argument (a recurring failure mode for us is decompositions that drift from the source
and bottom out at false or mathlib-absent leaves).

**Q4 (absolute vs. relativised).** For Lemma 8.34's induction we relativised the Laurent-cover
notion to an arbitrary base $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$. Does Lemma 7.54 likewise need to be applied
*relatively* (to refine a cover of a rational subset $U=\mathrm{Spa}\,\mathcal{O}_X(U)$), or
does the absolute statement over $\mathrm{Spa}\,A$ suffice — invoked once at the top and then
propagated through restrictions via Prop A.3(2)? Equivalently: is the natural home of 7.54 the
whole space (with restriction handled separately), or each affinoid in the cover?
