## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

A **Huber ring** is a topological ring A admitting an open subring AРѓђ Рђћ called a **ring of definition** Рђћ together with a finitely generated ideal I Ріє AРѓђ such that {I^n}_{n РЅЦ 0} is a fundamental system of neighbourhoods of 0 in AРѓђ. The pair (AРѓђ, I) is a **pair of definition**.

A Huber ring A is **Tate** if it contains a topologically nilpotent unit ¤ќ. Equivalently, A has the topology of AРѓђ[¤ќРЂ╗┬╣] for a suitable ring of definition with ¤ќ Рѕѕ AРѓђ.

A subring AРЂ║ Ріє A is a **ring of integral elements** (or **plus subring**) if AРЂ║ is open, integrally closed in A, and contained in the **power-bounded subring** A┬░ = {a Рѕѕ A : (a^n)_{nРЅЦ0} is bounded}. The pair (A, AРЂ║) is an **affinoid ring**.

The **valuation spectrum** Spv(A) is the set of equivalence classes of continuous valuations v : A Рєњ ╬Њ Рѕф {0} (╬Њ a totally ordered group), topologised by the subbase generated by sets of the form {v : v(a) РЅц v(b) РЅа 0} for a, b Рѕѕ A.

The **adic spectrum** Spa(A, AРЂ║) is the subspace of Spv(A) consisting of continuous valuations v with v(a) РЅц 1 for all a Рѕѕ AРЂ║.

A **rational subset** R(T/s) of Spa(A, AРЂ║), for s Рѕѕ A and a finite subset T Ріє A generating an open ideal, is

  R(T/s) := {v Рѕѕ Spa(A, AРЂ║) : v(t) РЅц v(s) РЅа 0 for all t Рѕѕ T}.

These form a basis for the topology of Spa(A, AРЂ║). The **structure presheaf** ­Юњф_X on rational subsets is

  ­Юњф_X(R(T/s)) := completion of A[T/s] with respect to the topology making the locSubring (a particular subring of A[1/s] generated by adjoining t/s to a ring of definition) open.

## 4. Definitions

### 4.1. Pair of definition, plus subring, Huber/Tate rings

**Definition 4.1.** For a Huber ring A, a *pair of definition* P is a tuple (AРѓђ, I) where AРѓђ Ріє A is an open subring and I Ріє AРѓђ is a finitely generated ideal with {I^n} a fundamental system of neighbourhoods of 0 in AРѓђ.

**Definition 4.2.** A *plus subring* AРЂ║ Ріє A is an open, integrally closed subring with AРЂ║ Ріє A┬░ (the power-bounded subring).

**Definition 4.3.** A Huber ring A is *Tate* if there exists a topologically nilpotent unit ¤ќ Рѕѕ A.

**Definition 4.4.** A Tate ring A is *strongly noetherian* if AРЪеXРѓЂ, Рђд, XРѓЎРЪЕ is noetherian for all n РЅЦ 0.

### 4.2. Rational localization data and presheaf values

**Definition 4.5.** A *rational localization datum* for A is a tuple D = (P, T, s, hopen) consisting of a pair of definition P, a finite subset T Ріє A, an element s Рѕѕ A, and a non-emptiness witness that for some N Рѕѕ РёЋ, every b Рѕѕ P.I^N has b/s in the locSubring (the subring of A[1/s] generated by P.AРѓђ together with elements t/s for t Рѕѕ T).

Two rational localization data D, D' satisfy R(D'.T / D'.s) Ріє R(D.T / D.s) iff the corresponding rational subsets are nested.

**Definition 4.6.** The *presheaf value* ­Юњф_X(D) is the completion of the localization A[1/D.s] under the uniform structure with the locSubring of D as a fundamental open neighbourhood of 0.

**Definition 4.7.** For D, D' with rational containment, the *restriction map* ­Юњф_X(D) Рєњ ­Юњф_X(D') is the unique continuous ring map extending the algebraic localisation map (well-defined since D.s becomes a unit in A[1/D'.s] by the containment).

### 4.3. Rational coverings and Laurent splits

**Definition 4.8.** A *rational covering* C consists of a base datum C.base, a Finset C.covers of refined data, subset proofs (each in C.covers is contained in C.base rationally), and a cover witness (every valuation in R(C.base.T / C.base.s) lies in R(D.T / D.s) for some D Рѕѕ C.covers).

**Definition 4.9.** For a rational datum DРѓђ and f Рѕѕ A, the *Laurent cover at f* is the rational covering with base = DРѓђ and covers = {plus, minus}, where:

  - the *plus piece* `laurentPlusDatum DРѓђ f` has T = insert f DРѓђ.T, s = DРѓђ.s (constraints v(t) РЅц v(s) for all t in T Рѕф {f}, equivalently v(f) РЅц v(s));
  - the *minus piece* `laurentMinusDatum DРѓђ f` has T = (insert DРѓђ.s DРѓђ.T) ┬и {DРѓђ.s, f} (Finset product under pointwise multiplication then image-of-product), s = DРѓђ.s ┬и f (constraints implying v(DРѓђ.s) РЅц v(f)).

### 4.4. The sheafy condition

**Definition 4.10 (IsSheafy A).** An affinoid ring (A, AРЂ║) is *sheafy* if for every rational covering C:

  (i) **(embedding)** The diagonal map ­Юњф_X(C.base) Рєњ РѕЈ_{D Рѕѕ РєЦC.covers} ­Юњф_X(D.1) is a topological embedding (continuous, injective, and the source carries the topology induced from the product).

  (ii) **(gluing)** For every compatible family of sections (f_D)_{D Рѕѕ C.covers} Рђћ compatibility meaning that any pair (DРѓЂ, DРѓѓ) restricts equally to any third datum DРѓЃ Ріє DРѓЂ РѕЕ DРѓѓ rationally Рђћ there exists a global section x Рѕѕ ­Юњф_X(C.base) restricting to each f_D.

### 4.5. Standard covers and refinements

**Definition 4.11.** A *standard cover* of A is a finite Finset S Ріє A with Ideal.span S = Ріц. For a rational covering C and a standard cover S, the *plus-piece cover* is V = {C.plusDatum f : f Рѕѕ S}. The condition Ideal.span S = Ріц ensures V topologically covers R(C.base.T / C.base.s).

**Definition 4.12.** The *refined V-cover at fРѓђ* (used in Wedhorn's geometric reduction, our `refinedVCovers`) for C, S, and fРѓђ Рѕѕ A is

  V_refined = S.image (laurentPlusDatum (C.plusDatum ┬и) fРѓђ) Рѕф S.image (laurentMinusDatum (C.plusDatum ┬и) fРѓђ)

i.e., for each f Рѕѕ S, take both halves of the Laurent split at fРѓђ inside C.plusDatum f. This has 2|S| pieces and is a refinement of both the plus-piece cover and (under the right hypotheses) C itself.
