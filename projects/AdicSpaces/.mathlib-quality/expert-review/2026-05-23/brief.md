## 1. Goal

We are formalizing **Theorem 8.28(b) of Wedhorn**: *if A is a strongly
noetherian Tate ring, then the structure presheaf O_X on the adic spectrum
X = Spa(A, A⁺) is a sheaf of complete topological rings.* The Lean 4 /
Mathlib target packages this as an `IsSheafy A` instance (defined in §4
below).

Our headline question for the reviewer: **the decomposition plan we have on
hand identifies an atomic sub-lemma that we believe is false as currently
stated.** We want a quick verdict on (i) whether the falsity argument is
correct, (ii) which of three candidate fixes is cleanest, and (iii) whether
the broader 18-leaf plan is sound.

## 2. Background and references

### 2.1. Setting

We work over a Tate affinoid ring A = (A, A⁺) in the sense of Wedhorn §7.3:
A is an f-adic ring containing a topologically nilpotent unit, and A⁺ ⊆ A
is an open integrally-closed subring of integral (= power-bounded)
elements. A *strongly noetherian* Tate ring is one whose all Tate-algebra
extensions A⟨X_1, …, X_n⟩ are noetherian (Wedhorn §6.7).

The adic spectrum X = Spa A = Spa(A, A⁺) is the set of continuous
valuations on A satisfying v(f) ≤ 1 for all f ∈ A⁺, with the topology
generated by rational subsets R(T/s) = {v ∈ Spa A : v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 for
all t ∈ T} for finite subsets T ⊆ A and s ∈ A with T · A open in A. By
Wedhorn 7.35, Spa A is a spectral space and rational subsets form a basis
of quasi-compact opens.

The structure presheaf O_X is defined on rational subsets by
O_X(R(T/s)) := A⟨T/s⟩, the completed Tate localization, and extended to
arbitrary opens by projective limit. Thm 8.28 asserts that O_X is in fact
a sheaf (of complete topological rings) under any of three hypotheses;
our target is the strongly-noetherian Tate case.

### 2.3. State of the art

Wedhorn 8.28 is a textbook synthesis of Huber's original proofs in
[Hub94, Hub96]; the case-(b) argument uses Čech cohomology over Laurent
covers (Wedhorn Lemma 8.33–8.34). The structure presheaf is known *not*
to be a sheaf in general for Tate rings (Wedhorn Rem 8.17 cites a
counter-example in [Hub94] §3.2). Sheafiness in the strongly-noetherian
Tate case is the foundational case that makes Spa(A, A⁺) a *bona fide*
adic space.

Mathlib has the spectral-spaces framework but not yet adic spaces; this
formalization is the first comprehensive treatment.

## 3. Strategy

Wedhorn's proof of 8.28(b) proceeds via:

1. **Lemma 8.31**: A⟨X⟩ is faithfully flat over A (and A⟨X⟩/(f − X),
   A⟨X⟩/(1 − fX) are flat) — Wedhorn Remark 8.29 (tensor identity
   M ⊗_A A⟨X⟩ ≅ M⟨X⟩) is the key technical input.

2. **Proposition 8.30**: For rational subsets U ⊆ V, the restriction
   O_X(V) → O_X(U) is flat. Reduces to Lemma 8.31 via Remark 7.55
   (Laurent-normalization chain).

3. **Corollary 8.32**: For any finite covering of Spa A by rational
   subsets, the product restriction O_X(X) → ∏ O_X(U_i) is faithfully
   flat, in particular injective.

4. **Lemma 8.33** (2-set base case): The augmented Čech complex
   `0 → O_X(X) → O_X(U_1) × O_X(U_2) → O_X(U_1 ∩ U_2) → 0` is exact for
   the Laurent 2-cover `U_1 = R(f/1)`, `U_2 = R(1/f)`.

5. **Lemma 8.34** (inductive step): Every rational cover generated by a
   finite ideal-spanning T ⊆ A is O_X-acyclic. Proof: (i) iterated 2-set
   Laurent covers are acyclic by induction on 8.33 + the Čech machinery
   of [Hub94, Prop. A.3]; (ii) any rational cover has a Laurent refinement
   to a unit-generated cover via Wedhorn Cor 7.32 dominating-unit; (iii)
   unit-generated covers admit a Laurent refinement by ratios
   `{f_i f_j^{-1}}`; (iv) acyclicity transfers via refinement.

The Lean formalization follows the "Lane C" strategy:

- **Atomic step (T286, complete)**: A single 2-set Laurent split is
  inducing for the product-restriction map on a strongly-noeth-Tate
  affinoid. This is the topological half of 8.33.
- **Tree induction (complete)**: An iterated Laurent tree, structured as
  a binary tree of 2-set splits with `allSplitsInducing` predicate, lifts
  the atomic case to multi-step refinement.
- **Tree existence (the open chain)**: Constructing the tree from any
  given rational cover, faithful to Wedhorn 8.34 steps (i)–(iv). This is
  where most of the open work lives.

## 4. Definitions

### 4.1. Sheafiness of a presheaf of topological rings

**Definition 4.1.** A presheaf O_X of topological rings on a topological
space X is a *sheaf of complete topological rings* if (i) it is a sheaf
of rings, and (ii) for every open U and every open cover (U_i)_{i ∈ I}
of U, the canonical map O_X(U) → ∏_{i ∈ I} O_X(U_i) is a *topological
embedding* (injection + the subspace topology from the product topology
on the codomain coincides with the source topology). This is **Wedhorn
Remark 8.20** (p.80).

The project's `IsSheafy A` predicate encodes (i)+(ii) as the existence,
for every rational covering C of Spa A, of three data:

- *embedding*: the canonical map `productRestrictionSub` from O_X(Spa A)
  to a sub-product of `(O_X(U_i))_{i ∈ I}` (where the sub respects the
  equaliser-of-overlaps constraint) is a topological inducing.
- *separation*: that map is injective.
- *gluing*: every overlap-compatible tuple in the sub-product is hit.

### 4.2. Rational coverings and the cover-piece data

A *rational covering* of Spa A consists of (a) a *base* rational subset
R(T_0/s_0), and (b) a finite family of *cover pieces*, each itself a
rational subset whose rational-open contains the union of the others
modulo coverage of R(T_0/s_0). (The Lean type `RationalCovering A`
packages this; the *base* is the rational subset on which the covering
is mounted.)

We write `R(T/s) := {v ∈ Spa A : v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 for all t ∈ T}` for the
rational open associated to T = (t_1, …, t_n) and s ∈ A with T · A open.

### 4.3. Laurent trees, ratio trees, and the `Refines` predicate

A **Laurent tree** on A is a finite binary tree whose internal nodes are
labelled by elements f ∈ A and whose two children at each node correspond
to the rational subsets R(f/1) and R(1/f). A leaf of the tree, traversed
from the root, accumulates the iterated rational subset R(f_1, …, f_r /
s) for a sign pattern σ.

A **ratio tree** is the natural generalisation where each internal node
splits on a *ratio* `t_i / s_i`. We have `Refines` and `allSplitsInducing`
predicates on trees, the latter asserting that each binary split is
topologically inducing.

### 4.4. The cover-piece witness construction (this is where the bug candidate L4 lives)

Given a `RationalCovering` C with cover pieces (D_α)_α and base
R(C.base.T / C.base.s), the project constructs, via Hübner's local-basis
hypothesis + quasi-compactness, a finite-witness assignment

> `mk_S_D : RationalLocData A → Finset A`

with the property that for each cover-piece D and each valuation v in
D's rational open R(D.T / D.s), some f ∈ mk_S_D(D) satisfies v ∈
R(insert f C.base.T / C.base.s) — i.e., v lies in the *plus-piece*
over the base witnessed by inserting f into the base's numerator
family. This is Wedhorn 8.34 step (ii)'s witness on a single cover piece.

The construction works as follows. Set

> `W(D) := { f ∈ A : R(insert f C.base.T / C.base.s) ⊆ R(D.T / D.s) }`.

The local-basis hypothesis gives `R(D.T / D.s) ⊆ ⋃_{f ∈ W(D)} R(insert f
C.base.T / C.base.s)`. By compactness of `R(D.T / D.s)` (Wedhorn 7.35),
a finite subfamily suffices; we extract it via the mathlib lemma
`IsCompact.elim_finite_subcover_image` and let `mk_S_D(D)` be that
finite-subfamily Finset.

## 5. Established results (closed in Lean)

The following milestones are sorry-free at their wrapper level. Below
each we sketch the proof.

**Theorem 5.1 (`isSheafy_ofStronglyNoetherianTate_proof`).** *Let A be
a strongly noetherian Tate ring satisfying the standard package of
hypotheses (complete Hausdorff topological ring, integral domain, with
a designated pair of definition (A₀, I) where A₀ is noetherian). Then
`IsSheafy A` holds.*

*Sketch.* Compose two ingredients: (a) an `IsSheafy`-builder that takes
a Laurent-tree existence hypothesis as input, and (b) the existence of
such a realized ratio tree (Wedhorn 8.34 chain). The builder consumes
T286 (atomic 2-set inducing) + tree induction to discharge `embedding`;
the equaliser descent via Cor 8.32 + Lemma 8.33's gluing-side exactness
discharges `separation` and `gluing`. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (`cor_8_32_clean_proof`, Wedhorn Cor 8.32).** *Let A be a
strongly noetherian Tate affinoid ring, X = Spa A, and (U_i) a finite
covering of X by rational subsets. Then the product restriction*
*O_X(X) → ∏_{i=1}^n O_X(U_i)* *is faithfully flat (in particular
injective).*

*Verbatim Wedhorn statement (p.83):*

> **Corollary 8.32.** *Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate affinoid ring,*
> *X = Spa A, and let (U_i)_{1 ≤ i ≤ n} a finite covering of X by rational*
> *subsets. Then the homomorphism* O_X(X) → ∏_{i=1}^n O_X(U_i), *f ↦*
> *(f|_{U_i}), is faithfully flat (and in particular injective).*

*Sketch.* The restriction-product factors through a faithfully-flat base
change (Prop 8.30 + Lemma 8.31). ∎

**Theorem 5.3 (`prop_8_30_flat_clean`, Wedhorn Prop 8.30).** *For
rational subsets U ⊆ V ⊆ Spa A (strongly noetherian Tate affinoid), the
restriction O_X(V) → O_X(U) is flat.*

*Verbatim Wedhorn statement (p.82):*

> **Proposition 8.30.** *Let A = (A, A⁺) be a strongly noetherian Tate*
> *affinoid ring, and let U ⊆ V ⊆ X := Spa A be two rational subsets.*
> *Then the restriction homomorphism O_X(V) → O_X(U) is flat.*

*Sketch.* By Wedhorn Example 6.38, O_X(V) is again a strongly noeth Tate
ring; reduce to V = X via base change. By Wedhorn Rem 7.55 (Laurent
normalization), reduce U to either R(f/1) or R(1/f). Then apply
Lemma 8.31. ∎

**Theorem 5.4 (Wedhorn Lemma 8.31).** *Let A be a complete noetherian
Tate ring. Then A⟨X⟩ is faithfully flat over A, and A⟨X⟩/(f − X) and
A⟨X⟩/(1 − fX) are flat over A.*

*Sketch.* The flatness uses Wedhorn Remark 8.29: for finitely-generated
M, the natural map `M ⊗_A A⟨X⟩ → M⟨X⟩` (Tate power series with
coefficients in M, tending to 0 in M) is bijective. Together with a
5-lemma argument on a finite presentation of M, this gives flatness.
Faithfulness is standard (every prime of A lifts to a prime of A⟨X⟩ with
the right contraction). Quotient flatness follows from a long exact
sequence + careful tracking of injectivity at the f − X / 1 − fX
multiplications. ∎

**Theorem 5.5 (tateAcyclicity separation and gluing wrappers).** *For
strongly noetherian Tate affinoid A and any rational covering, the
augmented Čech complex is exact in degrees 0 and 1.*

*Sketch.* Part 1 (separation): direct consequence of injectivity in
Cor 8.32. Part 2 (gluing): faithfully-flat descent of the equaliser via
Cor 8.32 + Lemma 8.34 reduction to Laurent covers + Lemma 8.33 base
case. ∎

**Theorem 5.6 (T286, "atomic 2-set Laurent inducing").** *Let A be a
strongly noetherian Tate ring (integral domain), C a rational covering
with base R(T/s), and f ∈ A. Then the product restriction map for the
Laurent 2-cover {R(f/1) ∩ R(T/s), R(1/f) ∩ R(T/s)} on R(T/s) is a
topological inducing.*

*Sketch.* The atomic case of Wedhorn Lemma 8.33's topological content,
proved via direct construction of a continuous section + open-mapping
argument on the explicit 2-set quotient. ∎

**Theorem 5.7 (tree induction; tree existence at the bare level).**
*Given an arbitrary rational covering C of Spa A, there exists a Laurent
tree refining C with `allSplitsInducing`.*

*Sketch.* Use Wedhorn 7.54 to extract a "standard cover" R(f_0, …, f_n /
f_i) refining C; this exists since Spa A is quasi-compact. Apply
8.34 step (ii) at the first stage to get a unit-generated outer tree
(the balanced Laurent tree on `s⁻¹ · S` where s is a Wedhorn 7.32
dominating unit). At each outer leaf, apply 8.34 step (iii) to refine
the unit-generated cover by ratios `{f_i f_j^{-1}}`. Graft the inner
ratio trees onto the outer leaves and verify `Refines` and
`allSplitsInducing` are preserved by grafting. ∎

(This bare-level assembly is sorry-free; the inputs it composes are open
sub-lemmas — see §6 below.)

## 8. Where we're stuck — the L4 candidate B2

This is the headline question we want the reviewer's verdict on.

### 8.1. Setup

The reduction sequence

> `exists_standard_cover_refining` (Wedhorn 7.54)
>   → `exists_per_D_finite_cover_of_localBasisHyp` (a.k.a. C2)
>   → `mk_S_D : RationalLocData A → Finset A`
>   → `strengthened_cover_of_basic_cover` (L4)
>   → `span_top_of_per_D_finite_cover`
>   → `spanTop_iff_noCommonZero_spa` (Prop 7.14 bridge)

is supposed to discharge "the combined witness family
`⋃_D mk_S_D(D)` spans the unit ideal in A". The C2 step gives existence
of mk_S_D and the basic cover property; L4 *strengthens* the C2 cover
property by adding `v(f) ≠ 0`; the consumer uses this to pump
no-common-zero on Spa into span-top.

### 8.2. The L4 statement

L4 says: given `mk_S_D` with the C2 property "for each cover piece D and
v ∈ R(D.T/D.s), there exists f ∈ mk_S_D(D) with v ∈ R(insert f
C.base.T/C.base.s)", then we can *strengthen* the f-witness to
additionally satisfy v(f) ≠ 0.

### 8.3. The attack

**Claim A.** `0 ∈ W(D)` is possible. Indeed,

> `R(insert 0 T / s) = { v : v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 for all t ∈ insert 0 T }`
> `             = { v : v(0) ≤ v(s) ∧ v(t) ≤ v(s) ≠ 0 for t ∈ T }`
> `             = R(T / s)`

since `v(0) = 0 ≤ v(s)` is automatic in the value monoid. So
`0 ∈ W(D) ⇔ R(C.base.T / C.base.s) ⊆ R(D.T / D.s)`, which holds whenever
D's cover piece contains the base's (a common situation in refinement).

**Claim B.** Therefore `0 ∈ mk_S_D(D)` is possible. The extracted
finite subcover from `IsCompact.elim_finite_subcover_image` is
determined by `choose` and may include 0 (Lean has no canonical way to
exclude it).

**Claim C.** If `0 ∈ mk_S_D(D)` and v is any Spa point, then
`v(0) = 0` always (zero element of the value monoid). The "v(f) ≠ 0"
predicate is spelled `¬ v.vle f 0` in Lean, equivalent to `v(f) > 0`
(since `v.vle f 0 ⇔ v(f) ≤ v(0) = 0 ⇔ v(f) = 0`). At `f = 0`,
`v.vle 0 0` holds, so `¬ v.vle 0 0` is false. The L4 conclusion
`∃ f ∈ mk_S_D(D), … ∧ ¬ v.vle f 0` therefore *fails to follow* from the
C2 hypothesis alone — C2 only promises *some* f, which may be the
bad 0.

**Diagnosis.** L4 is false as stated when the choose'd finite subcover
contains 0. There is no contradiction-style proof of L4 from C2 alone;
one needs an *additional input* — either (a) excluding 0 from
mk_S_D(D) at the C2 source, or (b) reformulating L4 / its consumer to
bypass the v(f) ≠ 0 condition.

### 8.4. Cascade

L4 has one direct consumer (the `span_top_of_per_D_finite_cover`
proof), which feeds the *Tier 0 wrapper* `exists_standard_cover_refining`
we have marked as sorry-free. So L4's potential falsity taints that
wrapper at the consumer level: the wrapper compiles but uses an
unprovable sub-lemma, meaning the strong-noeth-Tate IsSheafy proof would
not actually be axiom-clean once L4 is filled with a fake / impossible

Three other axiom-clean wrappers transitively consume this:

## 10. Wedhorn theorem cards (full statements, for self-containment)

### Theorem 8.28 (Wedhorn p.81)

> Let A = (A, A⁺) be an affinoid ring and X = Spa A. Assume that A
> satisfies one of the following conditions.
> (a) The completion Â has a noetherian ring of definition.
> (b) A is a strongly noetherian Tate ring.
> (c) Â has the discrete topology.
> Then O_X is a sheaf of complete topological rings. Moreover, one has
> H^q(U, O_X) = 0 for all q ≥ 1 and all rational subsets U of X.

### Remark 8.20 (p.80)

> Let X be a topological space, and let O_X be a presheaf of topological
> rings (i.e., O_X(U) is a topological ring for U ⊆ X open and the
> restriction maps are continuous). Then O_X is a sheaf of topological
> rings if and only if O_X is a sheaf of rings and for every open subset
> U of X and for every open covering (U_i)_{i ∈ I} of U the canonical
> map O_X(U) → ∏_{i ∈ I} O_X(U_i) is a topological embedding (where the
> product is endowed with the product topology).

### Proposition 8.30 (p.82)

> Let A = (A, A⁺) be a strongly noetherian Tate affinoid ring, and let
> U ⊆ V ⊆ X := Spa A be two rational subsets. Then the restriction
> homomorphism O_X(V) → O_X(U) is flat.

### Lemma 8.31 (p.82)

> Let A be a noetherian complete Tate ring.
> (1) The ring A⟨X⟩ is faithfully flat over A.
> (2) For all f ∈ A the rings A⟨X⟩/(f − X) and A⟨X⟩/(1 − fX) are flat
> over A.

### Corollary 8.32 (p.83)

> Let A be a strongly noetherian Tate affinoid ring, X = Spa A, and let
> (U_i)_{1 ≤ i ≤ n} a finite covering of X by rational subsets. Then
> the homomorphism O_X(X) → ∏_{i=1}^n O_X(U_i), f ↦ (f|_{U_i}), is
> faithfully flat (and in particular injective).

### Lemma 8.33 (p.83)

> Let A = (A, A⁺) be a strongly noetherian Tate affinoid ring,
> X = Spa A. Let f ∈ A and set U_1 = {x ∈ X ; x(f) ≤ 1} and
> U_2 = {x ∈ X ; x(f) ≥ 1}. Then the augmented Čech complex (with
> alternating cochains) for O_X and the open covering {U_1, U_2} of X
> 0 → O_X(X) → O_X(U_1) × O_X(U_2) → O_X(U_1 ∩ U_2) → 0
> is exact.

### Lemma 8.34 (p.84)

> Let A be a complete strongly noetherian Tate ring and 𝒰 be a rational
> cover generated by some finite subset T ⊆ A with T · A = A. Then 𝒰
> is O_X-acyclic.
>
> (i) For f ∈ A let 𝒰_f be the open covering of X = Spa A consisting of
> R(f/1) and R(1/f). Then 𝒰_f is O_X-acyclic by Lemma 8.33. Moreover,
> if U = R(T/s) is any rational subset, then 𝒰_{f|U} = 𝒰_{f_{|U}},
> where f_{|U} is the image of f under the homomorphism A → A⟨T/s⟩.
> Thus 𝒰_{f|U} is O_X-acyclic. Using Proposition A.3(3) it follows by
> induction that all open covers of the form 𝒱 := 𝒰_{f_1} × ⋯ × 𝒰_{f_r}
> are O_X-acyclic.
>
> (ii) We show the following claim. Let T = (f_0, …, f_n) ⊆ A be finite
> such that T generates A as ideal and let 𝒰 be the rational cover of X
> generated by T. Then there exists a Laurent cover (V_j)_{j ∈ J} of X
> such that 𝒰|_{V_j} is a rational cover generated by units of
> O_X(V_j) for all j ∈ J. Indeed, for all x ∈ X there exists f_i such
> that x(f_i) ≠ 0. Thus by Corollary 7.32 there exists a unit s ∈ A^×
> such that for all i ∈ {0, …, n} an x ∈ X exists with x(s) < x(f_i).
> Then the Laurent cover generated by s^{-1}f_1, …, s^{-1}f_r satisfies
> the claim.
>
> (iii) Every rational cover 𝒰 of X which is generated by units
> f_0, …, f_n of A has a refinement by a Laurent cover. Indeed, the
> Laurent cover generated by {f_i f_j^{-1} ; 0 ≤ i, j ≤ n} is a
> refinement of 𝒰.

### Remark 8.29 (p.81)

> Let A be a complete noetherian Tate ring and let M be a finitely
> generated A-module endowed with its canonical topology
> (Proposition 6.18(1)). Denote by M⟨X⟩ the A⟨X⟩-module of elements
> ∑_{ν ≥ 0} m_ν X^ν with m_ν ∈ M for all ν and such that for every
> neighborhood of zero U in M one has m_ν ∈ U for almost all ν.
> We claim that the homomorphism of A⟨X⟩-modules
> μ_M : M ⊗_A A⟨X⟩ → M⟨X⟩, m ⊗ a ↦ ma
> is bijective.

### Lemma 7.54 (p.70)

> Let A be a complete affinoid ring and let (V_j)_{j ∈ J} be an open
> covering of Spa A. Then there exist f_0, …, f_n ∈ A generating A as
> an ideal such that for all i ∈ {0, …, n} the rational subset
> R(f_0, …, f_n / f_i) is contained in some V_j.

### Remark 7.55 (p.70)

> Let A = (A, A⁺) be a Tate affinoid ring, s ∈ A and
> T = {t_1, …, t_n} ⊂ A a finite subset such that T · A is open in A.
> Let U = {x ∈ Spa A ; x(t_i) ≤ x(s) ≠ 0 for i = 1, …, n} be the
> corresponding rational subset. Since U is quasi-compact, there exists
> by Corollary 7.32 a unit u ∈ A^× such that |u(x)| < |s(x)| for all
> x ∈ U. We set X_0 := {x ∈ Spa A ; 1 ≤ x(s/u)} (a rational subset of
> Spa A). Then x(s) ≠ 0 for all x ∈ X_0, thus s_{|X_0} is a unit in
> A⟨su^{-1}/1⟩ (Proposition 7.52). Define now inductively rational
> subsets X_1, …, X_n of Spa A by X_i := {x ∈ X_{i-1} ; x(t_i/s) ≤ 1},
> i = 1, …, n. Thus we obtain a chain of rational subsets
> Spa A ⊇ X_0 ⊇ X_1 ⊇ ⋯ ⊇ X_n = U.

### Corollary 7.32 (p.63)

> Let A = (A, A⁺) be a Tate affinoid ring, Y ⊆ Spa A a quasi-compact
> subset and s ∈ A such that |s(y)| ≠ 0 for all y ∈ Y. Then there
> exists a unit π ∈ A^× such that |π(y)| < |s(y)| for all y ∈ Y.
