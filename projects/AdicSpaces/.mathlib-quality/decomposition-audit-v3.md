## Third-pass finding 2026-05-21 v5

### NEW Defect 15: project's `LaurentNormalized` definition mismatches Wedhorn's chain

**The project's `LaurentNormalized D₀` requires** (LaurentRefinement.lean:`class LaurentNormalized`):
1. `insert_s_T_subset_A₀ : ∀ a ∈ insert D₀.s D₀.T, a ∈ D₀.P.A₀` — every element of `{D₀.s} ∪ D₀.T` is in A₀.
2. `one_mem_T : (1 : A) ∈ D₀.T` — the element 1 is in T.

**Wedhorn 7.55's chain** (PDF p.70):
- `X_0 := R({u}/s)` has T = {u}, denominator s = s. The project's `laurentPlusDatum (base) u` with appropriate base gives this, but `1 ∈ T` requires T = {u, 1} not T = {u}.
- Wedhorn's `s` (= D'.s in our application) might not satisfy `s ∈ A₀` (the constructor takes arbitrary s ∈ A).
- Wedhorn's `u` (Cor 7.32 dominator) might not be in A₀ — Cor 7.32 gives π ∈ A^× (just a unit of A), not necessarily in A₀.

**Mismatches**:
- Wedhorn doesn't require 1 ∈ T at every step; the project does.
- Wedhorn's s and u aren't constrained to A₀ but the project's `LaurentNormalized` requires it.

**Resolution**: either
- **Option A**: Replace project's `LaurentNormalized` with a more flexible predicate. Major refactoring.
- **Option B**: Modify Wedhorn's chain to scale through A₀ — multiply s and u by topologically nilpotent unit to land in A₀, then adjust X_0 = R({u'}/s') accordingly. The rationalOpen is invariant under this scaling.
- **Option C**: Use the project's `laurentMinusNormalizedDatum` (which auto-inserts 1) and accept the resulting LaurentNormalized data is a strictly smaller rational subset than Wedhorn's X_0, then prove the inclusion is actually equality up to rationalOpen.

**Severity**: MEDIUM — needs careful definitional bridge between Wedhorn's chain and project's LaurentNormalized class.
